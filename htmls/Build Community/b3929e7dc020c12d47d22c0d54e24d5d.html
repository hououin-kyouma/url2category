        I'm relatively new to Portland. I moved here about six months ago to be with my fiancé who I was in a long term long distance relationship with. Whenever I go to a new city, I usually already have a friend there. You know...the "connector" type. Unfortunately for me, the only person I really know is my fiancé who is a great, likable guy but who is also a bit more independent and doesn't even see his own friends much.

        I love Portland. I truly do. The people are pretty great but...I still have not made one legitimate friend here. Not exactly due to lack of trying but because I just haven't been in a setting where I see people on a consistent basis where I can build a relationship with them. I currently am out of work (though I *am* trying to find a job) and am not a student (finished college years ago and have no plans to go to graduate school).

        I take classes here and there but it seems everyone around my age (about 30) is married and/or has kids and we have little in common.  On top of that, the people I've met don't happen to live in Portland. They end up being from the suburbs, which is pretty inconvenient.

        I tend to do a lot of DIY stuff like restoring vintage furniture so I'm at home in my garage a lot just power sanding away. I do have my moments where I miss going to brunch or happy hour. Or just calling up a friend to grab a coffee.

        I feel like my life lately has been mostly wedding planning, facebooking with friends from back home, restoring vintage furniture, working out, nesting in our new home, and being a good fiancé. It sounds like a handful but...I want friends. I want relationships.

        How do you make friends?

        Come to a UYE. You'll meet people of all ages (well 21-45, anyway), gender, walk of life, etc with many different interests. I showed up at my first UYE in early 2009.  I didn't know a soul in Portland and I was terrified as I am super shy until you get to know me. It was a casual night, Blazer game/March Madness at that bar on the corner of 39th and Division whose name is escaping me. I think I said approx 10 words the whole night, but everyone was super nice and I just kept showing up to the UYEs (ah.... fond memories of  Anna B. drinking Andrew C. under the table at Gold Dust) and Elite events. I made Yelp friends, made friends with their non-Yelp friends, lost friends, but Yelp really helped me out.

        Now I've got some great confidants, cat sitters, daydrinking partners and all around awesome group of people to hang out with.

        You could even set up a UYE of your own! Doesn't need to be a theme, just tell us where and when, after work and at a bar (duh) is usually best.
