Balcony collapse victim Bailley Unahi wants to give back to the community

MARY WITSEY

Last updated 13:24, February 12 2017

Hamish McNeilly/ Fairfax NZ

The balcony collapse at a Six60 gig on Castle St, Dunedin on March 4.

Bailley Unahi wants to give back to the community that rallied to support her after a tragic accident last year left her paralysed.

The 19 year-old, from Winton, Southland, suffered spinal injuries in balcony collapse during a Six60 concert in Dunedin in March 2016. 

During her recovery a Givealitle page was set up and thousands of dollars was raised.

MARY WITSEY/FAIRFAX NZ

Bailley Unahi, and her brother Jonty, set off for a ride together around Winton Streets. Unahi is focussed on building fitness as she trains for the Winton Fun Run on March 5.

Now almost 12 months later, Unahi is back at home and in training for the Winton Fun Run next month, with the funds raised going to the Winton Community Pool.

She's using the fun run as her way of giving back and she's encouraging others to join her to raise money for the struggling facility.

RILEY BAKER/SUPPLIED

Emergency services and members of the public carry an injured person to an ambulance after a balcony collapsed at the surprise Six60 concert.

"The pool is really important to Winton, not many small communities still have their own pool and I'd like to see ours retained."

A competitive swimmer in the past, she said she saw the fun run as the perfect opportunity for her to support a facility which was valued in the town.

"The community has been amazing in helping me, so this is a chance for me to give back.

"I want to get back and be involved, rather than always being on the outside."

Ad Feedback

A new "Stricker" hand cycle, from Germany, which clipped on to her wheel chair and allowed her to bike using her hands, was the catalyst in allowing her to take part in the fun run.

An avid sportsperson, Unahi said the cycle, which was delivered two weeks ago, would give her the independence she needed.

"It allows me to get back into exercise and to get out and about.

"I can bike to someone's house, unclip the cycle and go inside. It gives me lots of freedom."

Determined to get on with her life, Unahi had already completed an Outward Bound Course, was about to travel to Australia on holiday and would begin studying Occupational Therapy at Otago Polytechnic this year.

"I don't want to look back, I just want to go forward."

Unahi will compete in the 12km section of the event, which is being held on March 5, the one year anniversary of her accident.

Anyone could support Unahi, and the Winton Pool, by entering the fun run at Sports Southland in Invercargill, or Winton New World or The Local.
