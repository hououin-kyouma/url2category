How To Make Friends On Campus When You Know No One

Chegg

I think one of the most nerve-racking things for any incoming freshman is not knowing anyone else on campus. When I was a freshman, a lot of my friends were going to the same schools, and that made their transition to college easier. But what was I supposed to do? I didn’t know anyone.

But here I am all these semesters later, and so far so good. Here are few tips on making friends on campus based on my own experiences (and what I’ve been told by friends). Trust me, it’s not as bad as it seems!

Orientation

If your university has orientation and you have a chance to attend, I say definitely go. Everyone is really friendly, so it’s one of the best chances you’ll get to meet new friends! I met one of my first friends at orientation. We ended up sitting next to each other, began talking, and realized we had a lot of similar interests as well as a similar sense of humor. We exchanged numbers to keep in contact and agreed to meet up again once school started. Turned out we were taking a few of the same classes first quarter, so it was awesome already having someone to sit next to the first day of class. Two years later, we’re still really close!

 

Volunteering

Many universities now have freshmen take part in something called “Volunteer Day,” which usually takes place the first few week students start school in the fall. For one whole day, students volunteer at different locations all over town. When I participated my freshman year, I met a lot of new people who eventually became my really good friends. We all started shyly talking to each other on the bus ride over to the volunteer site. At the end of the day, we were laughing non-stop on the bus ride back.

If your university doesn’t have a volunteer day, no worries, volunteer anyway! You’ll still meet really cool people who care about the same things you care about.

 

Online

My university really utilizes Facebook. A lot of incoming freshman should look out for Facebook pages specific to your university and graduating class year. For example, when I was an incoming freshman, I searched for “UCLA Class of 2016” and a group popped up. Within that Facebook group, I found hundreds of incoming freshmen with the same questions and worries I had. It was awesome because at the beginning of the year, some people posted in the group asking if anyone wanted to grab lunch at one of the dining halls. Since people didn’t really know anyone else, a lot of people were up for it and went!

People In Your Dorm

If you’re living in a dorm, the first week is prime time to make new friends. Often people will leave their dorm-room doors open. People will walk in and out and introduce themselves. It’s a lot about getting to know each other and finding out who on your floor has similar interests. Everyone usually loves decorating their room with things from home and favorite band/movie posters. You’ll definitely find that person with the same Game of Thrones poster you have on your wall, and having that shared interest makes it incredibly easy to get a conversation started.

 

Your Classes

During the first few days of class (especially if you have discussion sections), other students will introduce themselves to one another. Getting to know people in your classes is another great way to start making friends. Plus, it automatically helps you start to form study groups, which become really helpful in college. You’ll often find people in your class are pursuing the same major as you, which means you’ll be seeing them around often for the next four years, so why not get to know them?

 

Events

There are A TON of activities that go on during the first week of the fall semester/quarter for freshmen. You can usually find out about them through your Resident Assistant (aka R.A.), people on your floor, and club events. During my first week at school, my R.A. held a fun event in our lounge. There was food and ice breaker activities for people to get to know each other.

If your R.A. doesn’t have something like that, the best thing to do is to just keep your eyes open for things posted on bulletin boards around campus. You’re bound to come across an event you’ll want to attend, and you’ll definitely meet people there!

Making friends in college can actually be a lot easier than you think.  I understand how putting yourself out there to meet people can be difficult, but know you’re not alone! My suggestion for you is to attend events that you’re interested in because you’re more likely to find people who share the same interests as you. I’m definitely an introvert, but I enjoyed meeting new people and learning about them. Most importantly, I realized I had nothing to be shy about!

Go Forth & good luck!
