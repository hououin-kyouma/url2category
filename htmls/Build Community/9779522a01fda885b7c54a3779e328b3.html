How to Make New Friends While Living Off-Campus

Meeting friends in grade school and even high school isn’t that difficult; after all, you share all the same classes and are on campus all day long. But meeting new friends in college can be a bit more challenging.

Get a Hobby — And Try New Things

College is when you should be experiencing and experimenting new things. If you don’t have a lot of social hobbies, it may be time to try some new ones. College students have groups for everything from knitting to martial arts, and many of them will meet up around your university student apartments. Eventually, you can even consider hosting your own events! You may not know that you like something before you try it.

Hobbies are an excellent way to meet people because it’s very low pressure; you’re doing something you enjoy or learning a new skill; so, even if you don’t become fast friends with others in the group right off the bat, you won’t feel as though you’re wasting your time.

Go to Events — Even If You Aren’t Always “Feeling It”

Many adults, from their early 20s to senior years, find the process of gaining “adult friends” rather difficult. In school, you see someone every day, and they become your friend. But once you’re an adult, you need repeated exposure to the same groups of people before you can be comfortable enough to start a real relationship. One way to do this is by going to the events that are hosted around your student housing.

You may not be inclined to go to these events due to social nervousness, but just showing up — even if you don’t talk to someone while you’re there — can be valuable. Of course, if you’re feeling miserably sick or absolutely anti-social, there’s no reason to subject yourself to an event that will make it worse. But if you’re just feeling a little bored and disengaged and don’t have anything else to do, it can’t hurt to put in some face time with a group of people who live near you.

Don’t Get Discouraged — It Takes Time

There’s no magical formula to making new friends, but eventually you’ll stumble into a good group. The most important thing to remember is that you aren’t alone. Everyone else is fumbling around, making mistakes and eventually succeeding in their college social lives. Once you realize that everyone else is just guessing, the process will become much easier for you too.
