Life is Beautiful Music and Art festival helps give back to downtown community

9:10 AM, Sep 17, 2016
9:10 AM, Sep 17, 2016
Share Article

Life is Beautiful is bringing some of music and art's biggest stars to Downtown Las Vegas next weekend. 

To kick off the festival, organizers are hosting a slew of events to give back to the community.

Life is Beautiful and the Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department DTAC will join forces to relocate the homeless population from the downtown festival grid to the Corridor of Hope, where the Lighting Hope Project will provide them with a beautiful morning, featuring meals, performances and conveniences to help them back on their feet.
 
In the days leading up to the festival, the local homeless population were given the opportunity to receive “Golden Tickets” in exchange for cleaning up the streets of downtown Las Vegas.
 
The tickets grant access to a variety of services at the event, provided by HELP of Southern Nevada; St. Joseph Catholic Church; CARE; Right at Home; Youth Outdoor Unity; Panacea; Deblanc Entertainment; Care Stream; Nevada Partnership for Homeless Youth; Veterans Relief; E320; Giving Project; Captain Andrew Walsh and the Metropolitan Police Department Downtown Area Command; Annie Wilson, homeless liaison; Anderson Dairy; the Salvation Army and West Care, on property donated by Mr. and Mrs. Thrower. 
 
Beginning the weekend prior to the event, Chase Carter, The Stencil Artista, Gabe Gault, John McNichol, Alex Huerta and Phenominal Mark will design and create a mural within the Corridor of Hope to inspire those who look at it to strive for a better life.
 
For more information and tickets to Life is Beautiful visit www.lifeisbeautiful.com
