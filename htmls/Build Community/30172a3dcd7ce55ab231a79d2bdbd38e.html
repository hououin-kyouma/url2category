Local woman who lost her child is giving back to the community

By: Alex Fisher

Posted: Nov 29, 2016 05:55 AM PST

Updated: Nov 29, 2016 05:55 AM PST

BAKERSFIELD, Calif. - A local woman who lost her child 33 days after giving birth is giving back to the community and she's asking for your help today.

Amber Bullard is on a mission to ease the emotional pain for parents and other children who are facing dark days in the hospital. She's partnering with Barnes and Noble Bookstore today to provide books to children in the hospital, hoping to bring a sense of joy.

It was a moment Amber Bullard anxiously waited for, giving birth to her first child, Addison Rose.

But Addison suffered from congenital diaphragmatic hernia, or CDH, a hole in the diaphragm which separates the chest and abdomen.
    

The hole allows the intestine to go into the baby's chest and occupy the space where lungs need to grow, but the newborn also had internal bleeding. That's when Bullard was faced with the devastating reality, there was nothing else doctors could do to save Addison.
    

So Bullard held her daughter and read a book out loud. It was the first and last time. Addison was 33 days old.
    

Now, nearly two years have passed and Bullard is diligently working to keep the memory of Addison alive. She's collecting books for a new library at the Lauren Small Children Center at Memorial Hospital.
    

The library would provide a new book for each of the nearly 100 children admitted, at any given time, continuing Addison's legacy, hoping to leave an impact on other people's lives and expand to every hospital in Kern County.

Barnes and Noble Bookstore on California Avenue is accepting book donations until the end of the year.
