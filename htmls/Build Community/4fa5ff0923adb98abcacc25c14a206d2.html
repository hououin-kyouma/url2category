The Clarion-Ledger announced on Thursday an initiative focusing on the outstanding community service occurring every day by kind Mississippians.

"Give Back Like Dak" is an ongoing project the newspaper hopes will inspire others to go out and make a positive impact on their own communities, said Nathan Edwards, president of The Clarion-Ledger.

Sponsored by Sanderson Farms, the initiative bears the name of current Dallas quarterback and beloved former Mississippi State star — and the guest speaker for The Clarion-Ledger's upcoming 2017 Sports Awards — Dak Prescott, a man equally known for his good deeds off the football field as much as his athletic talents in the professional league.

Playing off Prescott's charitable example, "Give Back Like Dak" asks readers to submit videos of themselves or others performing good deeds that uplift their surrounding communities. Once the deadline for submissions arrives, the videos received by the newspaper will be displayed online and readers will be asked to vote for the ones they believe exemplify community service.

GIVE BACK LIKE DAK: Submit your videos, see full rules and regulations here

The people who submit the top five videos, as determined by votes, will each win two VIP tickets to the Sports Awards, set for May 25, and attend a private meet-and-greet with Prescott before the event starts.

"There are so many Mississippians who work hard each and every day to give back to their communities. We want to not only recognize those already giving back, but encourage others to do the same, as each act makes an impact," Edwards said. "This promotion will highlight these individuals online, via videos they submit, and will allow our readers to pick their favorites."

"Our role in covering communities around the state gives us the opportunity to learn about so many selfless Mississippians," said Sam Hall, executive editor at the newspaper.

"Look at all the good Dak Prescott has done through various community service projects, and think about how his example has inspired others to pitch in. With "Give Back Like Dak," we have the opportunity to share even more examples of our readers' good deeds and to motivate others to join them in helping make our state a better place."

Added Edwards: "Not everyone will be a winner to meet Dak Prescott, but everyone who contributes are winners in the eyes of those they help."

Contact Dustin Barnes at 601-961-7249 or dbarnes2@gannett.com. Follow him on Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat and Twitter.

DETAILS

WHAT: Readers can submit their videos, as well as read full rules and regulations, at clarionledger.com/givebacklikedak

WHEN: Deadline to receive submissions is March 22. Winners will be announced on or around April 12. 

HOW: Voting will run from March 29-April 11. During that time, voting will be limited to a maximum of one vote per person each day of the voting period.
