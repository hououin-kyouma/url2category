Green County tractor pull focuses on giving back to community

Posted: Sep 25, 2016 09:05 AM CDT

Updated: Nov 14, 2016 01:21 AM CST

MONROE, Wis. - In rural communities across south-central Wisconsin, nothing brings people together like a tractor pull.

But at the Green County Fall Nationals in Monroe, it's not about the smoke, the growl of the engines, or the speed; it's about raising money for the community.

At Saturday's event, organizers said over the past 8 years, the Fall Nationals have raised more than $300,000 for local people in need.

For example, in 2013, Dawson Henry, of Belleville, ended up in the hospital, needing both of his feet amputated above the ankle.

Soon after Dawson got sick, the Fall Nationals was there to help.

"They brought us gift cards to help us with the transportation back to the hospital," Beth Henry, Dawson's mother, said. "Dawson also got a Nintendo DS and some games."

Also benefiting from the event over the years was the Buehler family from Monroe.

In August 2015, the family lost its dog, "Buddy," to a pit bull attack.

"We were devastated, of course," Donna Buehler said.

That devastation, however, soon turned into joy.

"The day after it happened the Nationals showed up on our door and offered to buy us any dog that we wanted," Buehler said.

After the Fall Nationals offered help, the family adopted a new dog, named "Max."

"They paid for vet bills for a year for Max., like checkups and shots and everything," Buehler said.

Both the Buehlers and the Henrys said they were thankful for the help.

"It just touched our hearts," Henry said.

To learn more about the Green County Fall Nationals, visit its website.
