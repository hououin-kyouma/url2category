Lakeshore realtors give back to local community

Wednesday
Jun 7, 2017 at 5:02 PM Jun 7, 2017 at 5:02 PM

The group, which is a 501(c) charitable organization, has also raised over $5,000 to be given to families in need and other charitable outreach programs.

Austin. Metz @hollandsentinel.com, (616) 546-4290

REALTORS Who Care, the charitable arm of the West Michigan Lakeshore Association of Realtors has distributed baskets filled with personal goods as part of its ‘May Baskets’ program.

The group, which is a 501(c) charitable organization, has also raised over $5,000 this year to be given to families in need and other charitable outreach programs.

Dale Zahn is the CEO of the West Michigan Lakeshore Association of Realtors and he has overseen the charitable program in recent years.

“REALTORS Who Care goes back to the beginning of the West Michigan Lakeshore Association of Realtors 17 years ago,” Zahn said. “It started rather small with a small group of realtors that wanted to get involved in the community and give back to the communities that they served in the interest of goodwill and helping people out.”

At first, the program, which includes realtors from along the lakeshore, focused on helping realtors and their families who had specific needs such as a child getting sick or financial hardship due to illness or an accident. From there, it also aimed to help with natural disasters or national tragedy.

“The main focus is still local, in West Michigan and it is not just dollars,” Zahn said. “It is also goods and services.”

On the local level, the ‘May Baskets’ program continues to grow and was again a big success.

For the program, baskets are placed all along the lakeshore in real estate offices, lender offices, title companies and other places. At the end, the personal products are collected and sorted at a central location in Grand Haven and are then delivered along the lakeshore.

“It is all done in the interest of goodwill,” Zahn said. “It is realtors donating their time and there is nothing in it for them other than feeling good about giving back to the communities that they work in.”

For those interested in donating to the program, Zahn said checks can be made out to REALTORS Who Care and sent to the West Michigan Lakeshore Association of Realtors or individuals can call and receive more information.
