ARIZONA COLLEGE STUDENTS CREATE APP FOR GROUPS OF FRIENDS TO MEET

Long gone are the days of meeting a new friend in person. An increasing number of college students are developing new relationships online.  A couple of U of A students are now capitalizing on the trend and hoping to cash in.

Author: Emma Jade
Published: 06/13/16
  • MORE

Long gone are the days of meeting a new friend in person. An increasing number of college students are developing new relationships online. A couple of U of A students are now capitalizing on the trend and hoping to cash in.

"You meet people with your friends, and that's when you feel most comfortable and that's what we're creating," Ryan Zettle, a student from the University of Arizona, said. He's hoping to get groups of students to hang out. "In college, people have this misconception that it's so easy to go to a new club and meet a new friend."

Ryan says that's not true: Nowadays, relationships spark online. But he wanted to take it a step further.

"There's no app on the market that incorporated groups of friends meeting other groups of friends," he said.

So, last July, Ryan went to work and teamed up with another college student to build an app. It's called Clique Meet.

"We went back to the Core foundation of how you meet new people, and that is always with your friends because you feel most comfortable, you have less anxiety and it's just really the foundation," he said.

When two people "match" on the app by showing interest in one another it opens up a group chat with those two people and their friends.

"[They] might go to the movies -- they can do whatever they want with it, it just depends on both parties," he said.

Ryan and his team rolled the app out at ASU this week, hoping to keep up the momentum and launch it at U of A next week.

"Once you get to that group chat," Zettle said, "you're going to feel so comfotable and you're with your friend, it's really going to turn into, 'why not? why not do this, let's go hang out.'"

Making new friends the new way, because now, there's an app for that.

Right now Ryan and his team are just focusing on college students to really get the app going.

However, you can download Clique Meet for free from the app store or at cliquemeet.com.
