Jeff Demps looks to give back to community

Thursday
Nov 10, 2016 at 2:01 AM

Frank Jolley @DC_Sports

Jeff Demps received a lot of assistance as grew up and developed into a world-class athlete.

And he never forgot that.

When he was a student athlete at the University of Florida, Demps received national recognition for visiting hospitals in Gainesville and spending time with children who had been admitted for a variety of illnesses. During his high school career at South Lake, Demps would often turn up at events involving the Special Olympics and “race” against the athletes.

After he graduated from Florida and moved into careers in the National Football League and professional track, Demps maintained his charitable nature, often stepping up to help area residents in need.

Now, Demps and longtime friend Logan Brown are looking to expand on that generosity.

The pair recently created the SPEED, a nonprofit foundation which they hope becomes a vehicle that will allow them to expand on Demps’ charitable nature. SPEED, which stand for Serving People Eagerly and Elevating Dreams, is already working on its first project — providing turkeys to needy area families.

“Jeff has always believed in giving back,” said Brown, who attended South Lake High School with Demps and was certified on Oct. 1 by the National Football League Players Association as a contract adviser — a player agent. “We’ve been in contact with the guidance departments at local public schools and with area churches to find families that might need a turkey for Thanksgiving. Jeff came from meager beginnings and he’s always expressed a desire to help others who might be having a rough time and need a hand.

“He was taught to respect others a very young age and he has simply never forgotten the lessons he was taught.”

Demps’ athletic accomplishments are part of local lore. He gained more than 3,100 yards and scored 37 touchdowns in his final two seasons at South Lake and was a state champion sprinter at 100 meters in 2007 and 2008.

After high school, Demps moved to play football and run track at the University of Florida, helping the Gators win the 2008 Bowl Championship Series National Championship. From there, he competed in the 2012 Summer Olympics in London, where he won a silver medal in the 4x100 relay — a medal was later stripped when teammate Tyson Gay failed a drug test — and played briefly with the NFL’s Tampa Bay Buccaneers in 2013.

Currently, Demps competes professionally on the international track circuit.

“We didn’t have much when I was growing up,” Demps said. “I got a lot of help from a lot of people to accomplish the things I did, and since I have the ability now, I want to do things to make others happy, especially kids and families. In the past, I’ve done things like giving away turkeys and school supplies and donated bicycles.

“But, I want to do more and I hope we can do that through our SPEED Foundation.”

In addition to SPEED’s turkey giveaway, Demps and Brown are planning to hold a sports camp in the winter or spring, which will give youngsters an opportunity to train with Olympic-caliber athletes. A number of details still need by be ironed out, but he hopes to hold the camp at Montverde Academy, where longtime coach Dennis Mitchell works with his Star Athletics club.

Mitchell also has competed in the Olympics, winning the gold medal in the 4x100 relay at the 1992 Summer Games in Barcelona, Spain.

“We hope to invite all youngsters between third grade and through high school to the camp,” Brown said. “Hopefully, it will serve as a fundraiser for SPEED and allow us to do even more throughout community. We want to do more things on a year-round basis and the camp could go a long way toward providing us with the resources to do that.”

Brown added that SPEED is accepting donations from residents and businesses interested in helping the foundation achieve its goals. Anyone wishing to help can contact him at 859-338-2257.

Anything donated, according to Brown, whether it be financial or material, will go back to the community.

“In the long run, we want to help make south Lake, west Orange and Sumter counties a better place to live,” Brown said. “We want to give kids the same chances Jeff had when he was younger. Without the help he got, he might not have gotten the chances he got.

“If we can help families and open doors for kids through SPEED, then we will have achieved our dreams.”
