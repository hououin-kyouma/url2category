This App Removes the Awkward Factor From Finding Friends in College

Have you joined Knock Knock yet?

Kelly Schwarze Dec 18, 2015 1:35PM EST

This scenario has played out way too many times in our lives: you’re at a party, meet someone awesome, swear to hang out with them all the time in the future, and then . . . totally forget to get their number, Instagram account, Snapchat name, anything.

Instead of doing some casual new friend (and crush) stalking on every social channel, or worse yet, hoping you run into them again soon, a new app called Knock Knock helps you save all of the other person’s digital contact info with a — you guessed it — “knock” of the app. All the information the other person has listed in their Knock Knock profile that they choose to share will be added to your Knock Knock database.

The app uses something called Beacon technology to create a Bluetooth connection with other phones it discovers nearby. Say you’re waiting for a lecture to start in a huge auditorium-sized classroom, just open the app to discover other users in the same room. Knock Knock will tell you which mutual friends you have in common and you can start chatting with them instantly or exchange contact info from across the room, without ever talking IRL. Pro tip: you should probably formally introduce yourself after class!

Knock Knock was created to make meeting people on campus and remembering all those faces so much easier. "Thinking back to my first week of college, I remember wishing there was a way to keep track and remember all of the new people I met," the app’s CEO, Ankur Jain told TechCrunch .

Knock Knock will even keep track of where and when you met someone so that you’ll never have that awkward “Oh, hey. Were we both in Intro to Chemistry together last semester?” again. The app is super high tech in that you don't even have to click to open it as you would a usual app. Try knocking on your iPhone or Android phone when the screen is asleep, and the app will open, ready to communicate.

How we’ll be using Knock Knock the most, however, is for group chatting. Since you don’t necessarily need to exchange phone numbers to use the app, group chats can be a great way to keep conversations going with your crew or anyone you meet nearby. Imagine finding people on other dorm floors and adding them to a message about campus events. With the magic of a knock, you can meet cool new people and find even cooler things to do in your off time.
