How to make friends in college at http://www.idealfriendships.com - Jeff Greco new college friends at: http://youtu.be/yK0-NJMaaFc

Be yourself, introverts and extroverts both have huge capacity to have meaningful relationships, just be aware that your approach to making friends college
may be slightly different.

Introverts or sometimes labeled "shy" by other people, typically like to have fewer friends, but have the capacity to have much deeper relationships. The question of 'is it hard to make friends in college' becomes a 'no' when you follow the action steps below.

Extroverts typically like to have tons of friends, but may not go quite as deep with each connection so not making friends in college is usually not a challenge for them.

If you truly wish to make new friends in college, then there are things that you can do which will not require you to dramatically transform yourself into a social butterfly.

Opt to participate more in volunteer work. It is hard to make new friends in college -- or anywhere for that matter -- when the situation expects you to find a partner or companion for yourself. With volunteer work, however, new members are often automatically grouped with others to complete a particular task. With that, you have an instant set of new friends.

Participate in other activities that you truly love and you will thus have an easier time fitting in. It is easier to make new friends in college when you are in a situation that you know like the back of your hand. If you love reading, then spend a bit of time in the library. The people you will eventually get to know there are likely to enjoy the same things you do.
