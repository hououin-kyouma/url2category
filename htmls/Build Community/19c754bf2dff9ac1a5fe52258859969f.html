Charity Fundraising How To: Choosing Your Cause

July 23rd, 2013  
  Tweet

If you type the word “charity” into Google, your search will come up with approximately 260 million results. If you try to lighten the search by typing in “charity causes,” you will still receive 65 million results on Google. No one has time to search through over 65 million results to find a cause that’s just right for them to support. So how do you choose a cause or a charity? Here are four simple steps to finding the charity that’s perfect for you.

Step 1: Find Your Passion

The first step is to find a cause that you’re passionate about. You want to donate your time to a charity that represents a cause you truly believe in. Tap into your inner emotions and think about what it is that makes you tick. What is it that makes your heart ache or race? Some people have the easiest time picking a cause that is very personal to them. For example, if a family member or friend survived cancer, people will tend to support specific charities that donate their profits to cancer research. It’s important to understand there’s more than likely a nonprofit organization somewhere in the world for whatever cause you decide to dedicate your time to. Specific causes that charities raise money for can range from cancer research to rescue animal shelters. The best part about our society today is that if you care about a specific cause, chances are someone else already does too; which means it’s very likely your cause will already have a charity in its name doing fundraising.

Step 2: Do Your Research

Once you’ve determined your cause, now is the time to use the Internet to your advantage and do a little research. If you do a simple Google search for your cause with the word “charity” afterward (e.g.: breast cancer charity), you will find a Web sites for one or more specific charities. If there is more than one Web site for a charity supporting your cause, search around on each site to see which most fits what you’d like to be involved in. Is one of the charities donating money to survivors? Is one donating their money to research? Do you believe in one charity’s message or goal over the other? Web sites also allow you to research further into what a charity has done for its cause, past, present and future. Web sites are a great tool used by charities in order to provide an opportunity for almost anyone to learn about a charity’s goals, successes, and aspirations. Also, if you’re just looking to quickly donate money to a charity, most charity Web sites will have an option where you can simply click and be lead to a payment page where you can instantly donate money to their cause. Most of these  sites also have calendars of events, which can allow you to potentially get involved with a charity fundraiser by donating your time or any supplies they may need. Charities want the public to know what they’ve done to raise money, how much money they’ve successfully donated etc., therefore a Web site can allow a new-comer (such as yourself) to research how much the charity itself has done to support your cause.

Step 3: Find Your Local Chapter

Many charities have multiple branches and local chapters. They typically have a national Web site (one which is accessible to everyone), but these national Web sites may allow you to search for your local chapter. You never know, maybe your area doesn’t have a chapter yet, and you may be the perfect person to start one! Local chapters give you the ability to find out events that are being held in your area where you can volunteer to help out. There also may be an opportunity to continue working with your local chapter on a regular basis. The greatest part about a local chapter is it gives the community the ability to get involved, as well. This can provide the community with the benefit of strengthening relationships, while working on a great cause at the same time. If you find out your cause does not have a local chapter in your community, it may be worthwhile to reach out and make contact with the charity to propose a chapter in your area.

Step 4: Get Involved!

No good charity will ever turn down a person who genuinely wants to help and be part of their organization. Charities rely on people from the general public who share in their beliefs. Not only is the public what will help fund their organization, but also, when they need help, it’s the public they reach out to. Getting involved with a cause once you decide on one really can be the simplest of tasks. Getting involved doesn’t necessarily mean donating money to the cause; volunteering your time at a local fundraiser will help out any organization. If you find a cause that you’re passionate about the limits are endless on what can be done to help. You may just be surprised how many people share your beliefs in the cause, and how many people will be willing to help.

Selecting a cause can be extremely beneficial. Not only does it help others, but it also helps personal character building and can allow for you to really think outside of your normal realm. Charities rely on creativity from their public involvement, so belonging to a cause may tap into inner creativity you never knew you had. Get involved today!

By J’nelle Oxford, Charity Fundraising Specialist
