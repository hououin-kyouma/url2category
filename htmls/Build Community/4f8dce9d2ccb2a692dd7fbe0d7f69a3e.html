How to Choose The Right Charity

By
The Road Trip Guy
-
March 6, 2017
253
0
Share on Facebook
Tweet on Twitter

Now that The Trip 2017 has been confirmed and my 7 goals have been set, it is time to start working on finding 12 interesting and suitable charities. During the road trip through Europe I plan to involve these charities by trying to raise funds, write blogposts about their activities and of course visit them. So, how do I choose the right charity for my project? What aspects should I be looking for in a charity and how does it relate to the theme and goals of The Road Trip Guy? Many questions that must be answered before making a selection. By following the 3 steps below it will become more clear on how to choose the right charity for this great adventure.

1. Where do I start? 

When you try to find out on how to choose the right charity for either work or personal reasons, it is very important to decide what charity you are passioned about. There are more than thousands of different charities in each country alone so instead of checking out all of them, select the ones with a cause you are interested in. For example; I want to include a wildlife & nature conservation charity so I started looking for these projects in the countries that are included in The Trip. For marine life rescue & rehabilitation, I know to look into countries connected to oceans & seas and with forest preservation in countries with a rich flora. An afternoon on Google and Youtube will give you great information and insight on how to choose the right charity that matches your passion.

 

2. Visit the place

For my project I won’t be possible to visit each charity beforehand, but if you get the opportunity to do so then by all means go for it. Make an appointment and ask for a tour and information and see how the charity is managed and what kind of volunteers and managers are involved in the daily operations. After the visit you can decide for yourself if you want to commit your name and donations to the charity and support their cause.

 

3. Public reviews and publications

You did your research online and planned a possible visit, now it is time to explore to future plans of your charity and get more in depth information on their activities. What are they planning to do for the upcoming year? Do they have any financial year reports? And what comments are posted by other visitors/contributors? These are all questions that would give a clear answer on the smoothness of the daily operations and what the future plans of the charities are.

When you find a charity you are passioned about, did your research, visited the charity and checked their future plans, you should be on the right track on how to find the right charity. Once the outcome of the above is all positive, keep following your charity for updates to see if even 6 months or 1 year from now they are still in line with your motivation. Good luck!

For the next coming weeks I will post updates on how the search for the 12 charities is coming along by following these steps. If you have any recommendations please feel free to contact me anytime!
