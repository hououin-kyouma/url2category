7 Ways To Give Back To Your Mountain Bike Community

Staff Mountain Bike Tips
Posted by: Meredith Wires mountain bike community, giving back to mountain biking, mountain bike tips, Mountain bike inspiration
September 9th, 2016

Here at Sacred Rides, we think it’s important to give back to the communities we explore via mountain bike. We continually strive to give back through trail building programs, community projects or donating to local mountain bike clubs. As a mountain biker yourself, there are a lot of ways you can give back to the mountain bike community you love OH-SO-MUCH! You can think on a local level and give back to your mountain biking neighbours, or think global and help international communities gain access to more bikes and better trail systems.

 

Here are 7 Ways To Give Back To Your Mountain Bike Community...

1. grab a shovel -->  trail building

You ride em’… but do you ever think about all the work that goes into building mountain bike trails? One of the best ways to give back to your local mountain bike community is to lend a helping hand with a little manual labor.  It’s usually the local mountain bike club that will be maintaining existing trails and building new trails – so connect and learn different trail building techniques from them. CLICK HERE and check out the International Mountain Bike Association’s website for tips and tricks for trail building.

2. share your passion --> become a teacher

If you’re confident with your mountain bike skills, share your passion with someone else. A lot of people are hesitant to get into mountain biking because they think there are too many barriers to entry and find it intimidating. Introduce a child, friend or sibling to the sport. If you have multiple bikes (I know a lot of you do!), share the wealth and let a friend test ride one of your bikes. Start off on easy double track trails and slowly progress to more difficult terrain. Walk them through some basic mountain bike tips for climbing, descending and going over small obstacles. Put up a poster at your local bike shop to advertise your local weekly or monthly mountain bike ride clinics… and offer them for free!

Like this post? CLICK HERE  to join Nate's Singletrack Mailing List, packed full of tips, inspiration and good times for passionate mountain bikers and adventurers.

3. give a little --> donate (not just money)

It’s an easy way to give back, and there will always be organizations and people that will accept your monetary donations.  But, don’t forget about donating mountain bike gear, accessories and bikes themselves! If you can afford it, the ultimate way to give back is to provide someone with the tools to mountain bike, who might not be able to afford all the gear themselves.

4. surprise someone --> random acts of mountain bike kindness

Sometimes the best ways to give back are the simplest. Here are some ideas for  random acts of mountain biker kindness:

  • Provide a post-ride beverage for your riding crew.

  • Offer to drive everyone to the trailhead.

  • Leave a kind note for other riders at the trailhead.

  • Make homemade trailside snacks for your riding pals.

Do you have any other random act of kindness ideas? Let us know in the comments below!

  5. share your skills --> host a "you can fix it" workshop

If you have basic bike mechanic skills host a workshop at your house or in your garage. Invite your riding pals over, and learn new bike mechanics skills together. Share your knowledge! Think about starting a bike co-op amongst friends and community members, where you can share tools and lend a hand to people that need their bikes fixed. Check to see if a bike co-op already exists in your town and offer to donate your time to help fix bikes!

6. gather the troupes --> organize a charity ride

If you’re passionate about a specific cause, think about organizing a charity fundraising ride or race to support the cause. Start small and over the years, it may become a huge event with a big following and a big impact.

7. think about your impact - be a considerate rider

One way to give back to the local community is to be an overall responsible rider. How do you become a responsible rider? Well… start with these tips:

  • Yield to hikers on the trail – don’t sneak up on hikers and be friendly when they pass by!

  • Leave No Trace – pack out your garbage and don’t litter. Keep your trails pristine.

  • Don’t ride trails that are muddy or sensitive to erosion.

  • Maintain the Trail – if you notice branches all other the trail from a recent windstorm, pause your ride and clear the trail.

how else do you give back to the mountain bike community? Let us know in the comments below!
