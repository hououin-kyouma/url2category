Ed Walsh Foundation continues tradition of giving back to Watertown community

Friday
Mar 17, 2017 at 12:04 PM Mar 17, 2017 at 12:49 PM

Dana Forsythe dforsythe@wickedlocal.com @watertowntab

After Watertown native Ed Walsh was killed battling an intense nine-alarm apartment fire in the Back Bay on Mar. 26 in 2014, his family, left with the intense urge to give back to the community in Walsh’s name, started the Ed Walsh Foundation.

Walsh, 43, a father of three young children, lived with his family in West Roxbury, but he and his family are well known in Watertown. He grew up in town, and graduated from Watertown High School in 1989.

His mother Joan still lives in town and worked for the Watertown Public Schools as a Title 1 tutor, and also worked at a number of schools, including the Hosmer School and West Junior High School (now Watertown Middle School).

For several years, Walsh worked in finance, but he "had a calling," and soon after took the firefighter's exam, aced it and waited until he found a spot on the Boston Fire Department, eventually rising to become a lieutenant in Engine Company 33 at the department's fire house at the corner of Boylston and Hereford Streets.

Kathy Malone, Walsh’s sister, said the foundation was established to “keep Ed's spirit alive by giving back to the community where he lived and worked.”
The Foundation is spear headed by Ed's amazing wife Kristen and is comprised of Ed's family and friends.

Next week, the three-year-old foundation will be hosting it’s spring fundraiser on March 24.

“Ed was a devoted husband, loving father and a friend to everyone who met him,” she said. “He had a warm smile and quick wit that put everyone at ease.  He will forever be remembered as a person who brought out the best in all of us and who brightened our days with his up-beat personality and positive attitude.”

Malone said whether coaching baseball, volunteering, or working at the YMCA, her brother was always helping children, families and his community.  

To that end, the goal of the foundation,a 501 (c)(3) charitable nonprofit, is to carry on Walsh’s dedication to helping people in need. Over the last few years the Foundation has given to causes including Cambridge School Volunteers, the Greater Boston YMCA, the Watertown Youth Baseball & Softball, Watertown Challenger League and Watertown Boys & Girls Club.

“Watertown is where Ed grew up and played,” Malone said. “He loved sports and remained active in sports his entire life. Watertown held a special place in Ed's heart.”

Since the beginning, the foundation has recognized this, Malone said, and has donated to kids and families in need, as well as the Lt. Paul J. Sullivan Scholarship Fund whom Ed was a recipient of when he graduated from Watertown High School.

This year, the foundation will be marking its first time participating in the Boston Marathon, a step Malone called thrilling.

“This will be the first year that the Foundation has been represented in the race and we are thrilled to have Emily Casey as our inaugural runner,” she said.

In addition, she said, the foundation will be represented by runner Brendan McCarthy.

At first, Malone points out, the foundation didn't receive any bibs when they applied to the BAA and John Hancock. But, as always, the Boston Fire Deptartment was there to help and graciously shared some of their bibs. 

“I wish we had enough to take all of the wonderful applications that we received, but unfortunately we don't,” she said. “The presence of the Ed Walsh Foundation in the marathon this year is an amazing honor for Ed's legacy. His giving spirit is emulated in our marathon team's efforts as they train and gain supporters.”

Three years later after Walsh’s death, Malone said his family has put their energy into remembering his sacrifice and ramping up their efforts to help others.

“This date will be a reminder of when we lost Ed but his tremendous loss is felt by all of us each day,” Malone said. “Ed was a remarkable father, husband, son, brother, and friend. He was a hero to us long before his loss was felt by the world. Anyone that had a chance to meet and know Ed, knew he made this universe a better place being in it.”

Walsh’s birthday is Mar. 21, so, Malone said, the family choose to continue to celebrate his life with the annual spring fundraiser. 

This year, to promote reading, organizers have asked attendees to bring a book a book that children ages 6-12 would enjoy. This book drive benefits students at Boston Public Schools who don't have access to books at home.  The books will help build libraries in the schools, and provide books to children to use after-school and at home, Malone said.

Over the last three years, Malone said there have been many amazing memories from Foundation events.

“From great times for dear friends and family of Ed's from grade school, high school, college, work and life all getting together to share their memories and carrying on Ed's giving spirit in a positive way and reconnecting with one another, to people that have been touched by Ed's life, it gives us all great strength to see that such good can come from the loss of Ed here on earth with us,” she said.

“We truly feel his presence with us as we gather together and work to make life a more fun and a little easier for children in his name,” she added.

This year's spring fundraiser will be held on Friday March 24 at American Legion Post 440, located at 295 California St. in Newton. Doors open at 7 p.m. 
Tickets are $20 in advance or $25 at the door. For more information, visit http://edwalshfoundation.org/spring-fundraiser-2017/
