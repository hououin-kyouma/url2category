7 Great Ways to Give Back by Promoting Literacy at Your Business

Did you know that today, September 8, 2016, marks the 50th anniversary of International Literacy Day? Reading can be a life-changing skill. A good book can expand your world, be a great escape, and make you think. Using your business to help promote literacy is not only a nice thing for socially responsible companies to do, it can also help make you a valuable part of the community and boost your visibility. Read on for some tips on ways to give back to your community by promoting literacy at your business:

Host a book club

Host a local book club for regular meetings, or start a book club of your own. Work with your local library to come up with book titles for kids’, teens’, and adult clubs. Promote the idea at local schools, the library, bookstores, and reading associations. Give out bookmarks with your logo.

Host readings by local authors

Name an Author of the Month and invite a local writer in to do readings from his or her book. Co-promote it with the author using email and social media to target groups that may be interested in that month’s topic. Sell books at the reading and offer the titles in your shop all month long.

Encourage little readers

Use placemats with word games, word searches, and reading puzzles in your restaurant. Make up little bingo cards or a small scavenger hunt to entertain kids while mom or dad shops. Set up a small reading corner with pillows and picture books.

Help a nonprofit

Here’s one way your business can give back, literally: support a local nonprofit that promotes reading. Ask at schools and the library about ways your business can help, or raffle off a basket of books and donate the proceeds. Check out national nonprofits like Milk and Bookies, which helps kids get books to underserved children in their community; United Through Reading, which shares videos of active-duty military personnel reading to their kids back home; or Pajama Program, which donates books and pajamas to kids living in group homes and shelters.

Start a Little Free Library

Little Free Library was started by a teacher’s son to honor his mother’s love of reading. He built a model of a schoolhouse, filled it with books, and put it in front of his house for neighbors to exchange reading materials. Today, there are more than 40,000 Little Free Libraries around the world. Build one and send a press release to local media to promote it.

Donate a percentage of sales

Pick a specific nonprofit, decide on the percentage of each sale to be donated, and pick an end date. Jointly promote the sale with the charity and create a hashtag to share news about the sale.

Hold a book drive

Pick a recipient, such as a school, a nursing home, or an assisted living facility. Decorate a large box to hold donations. Promote the drive on social media, and share a picture when you make the donation. It’s an easy way to put smiles on a lot of faces.

Using your business to promote reading and literacy in your community gives customers another reason to value you as a socially responsible company. Looking for more ways to give back to the community? Check out more ideas here and be sure to let us know your suggestions in the comments.
