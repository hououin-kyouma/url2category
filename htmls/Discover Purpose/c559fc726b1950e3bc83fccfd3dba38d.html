The One Thing People Don’t Tell You About Finding Your Purpose

by Alissa Carpenter | All Posts, Career Development, Leveraging Your Strengths

Everyone keeps throwing the phrases “finding your purpose” and “discovering your passions” out there like they are some kind of magic words that everyone wants to hear.   Don’t get me wrong, I use them too but it wasn’t until the past few years that I actually even knew what they meant.  I mean what’s a purpose anyways? 

I used to think people were talking about some kind of spiritual experience and then this magical life changing moment would happen.  Almost like the sky would part and a sign would appear.

 

Well, that never happened.  There was no sky writing moment or light bulb experience where it all clicked. 

I just assumed after a few years working and by trial and error some sort of shift would happen and then BAM… my purpose would be shown to me.

It wasn’t until I took a serious step back and spent some time reflecting that I was able to come up with my purpose. 

Before then I was going through a serious Quarter Life Crisis because I had no idea what I wanted out of life and there weren’t any signs pointing me to a direction.  I felt like I was in the middle of the ocean without a life raft. I lacked direction and even if I knew my direction I didn’t know how I was going to get there.

So friends, I don’t want you to go through life thinking that this is something that will pop up out of thin air. 

 

Finding your purpose is something you have to work on. Once you find it you can’t expect one thing in your life to be sole keeper of your purpose.

 

Let me break that down for a minute since it’s a little confusing.

Once you discover your purpose one avenue (personal or professional) cannot be the entire way you live it out.  You will not get all of your life enjoyment from your job, friends or family.

It will come from various aspects of your life and should.  The minute you put everything into one “basket”, like your job, you will be completely disappointed.  Don’t get me wrong liking your job is important BUT it can’t be your whole life and every part of your purpose can’t be lived out through it.

Let’s break down what a purpose is to better illustrate my point.  There are ton of venn diagrams that reference purpose but this one is my favorite

 

Let’s look at each section together:

Things you like to do

This is where you can find your passions.  The things you love to do on a daily basis (personally or professionally)?  The reason why so many of us have that side hustle is because there are other things we love to do that aren’t part of our work day.  These are things that you love to do and pretty much can’t live without.

Your Strengths

What are you good at? What comes naturally to you that you don’t even need to think about and it just happens.  Are you good at solving problems?  Talking with people? Analyzing data? What uniqueness do you bring to the table at work and at home? 

Things the world needs

What is the world looking for and what can you provide?  Think about what is going on in the world around you and what you may be able to or already bringing to the table in your own way.  This doesn’t have to be something mind blowing just something where you provide value.

 

The intersection between all of these areas is your purpose.

 

Some diagrams put in “What You Can Get Paid to Do” but I’m not a huge fan.  There are places in the world where people don’t get paid to live out their purpose and not everything you do will have a monetary value to it.  Just because it doesn’t bring in income doesn’t mean it won’t provide some type of value to your life or someone else’s.

So, my friends I want you to really reflect on your purpose.  Download my Life Vision document to help you answer a few questions about yourself to get one MAJOR step closer to living out your purpose.

Have you struggled with finding your purpose? If so, what have you done to work through the process?  Let us know in a comment below and send us a tweet #notokthatsok.
