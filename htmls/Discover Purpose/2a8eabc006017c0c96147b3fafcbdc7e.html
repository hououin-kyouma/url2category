FIRE: Season of Creation Worship Series

Posted on September 5, 2017 by James

Today’s Scripture: Exodus 3:1-15

 

A Fire Inside

Moses was keeping the flock of his father-in-law. He and his flock came to Horeb, the mountain of God. There he saw a fire. There he saw a fire burning a bush, but the bush was not being burned up. Moses saw this and marveled! He didn’t know what to think so he moved closer to investigate. That is when he heard the voice of God calling out his name, “Moses, Moses!” Moses said, “Here I am.” Then God said, “Remove the sandals from your feet, for the place on which you are standing is holy ground.”

Fire. If you were to shout the word in a crowded place, the word may inspire panic and all havoc might break loose. It can be scary.

Fire. It can be heard after “Ready…” and “Aim…,” and then heard next is a solemn salute of rifle shots at a funeral being held with military honors. It can bring sadness.

But then, when you are hungry, or you are out when it gets cold at night, the light and heat of a fire brings hope, comfort, and even happiness.

For Moses, who was by this time around 80 years old, the fire he saw that day brought something else. Here, on the mountain of God, Moses was fascinated by what he was seeing and he had to turn away from watching the sheep and get a closer look at this mysterious fire. Once he made himself open to learning more, God spoke to him saying that this was holy ground. He must have trembled at the presence of God!

The Fire was a Sign

Fire was more than just something God used to get Moses’ attention. It was more than just a miracle. The fire that day was a sign. The fire that day may have been a momentary fire. But it was to rekindle within the heart of Moses a long lost flame; a flame that would become an enduring flame.

As the story goes, Moses was away from his home in Egypt for over 40 years.

Something happened in his life that changed the course of his life. Being hidden in a basket and placed in the Nile River, he was raised by the Pharaoh’s daughter. This set the course for his youngest years. He lived a life of affluence. He was protected. His family associations gave him prestige.

But he was removed from his people: the Jewish people. He lived among them, but he was not a part of them. His identity was separate. In the first half of his life, on one fateful day he witnessed a slave master beating one of the Jewish slaves. Something in him must have snapped. It was the decision he made on that day that changed the course of his life. When he chose to take the life of the slave master that day, he became a murderer.

The first life he was leading came to an abrupt end. It was his action that led to the ending of his first life. When he lost everything, the second stage of his life began. He fled to the wilderness and found a new home with the people away from both the Egyptians and the Jews.

Second Stage of Moses’ Life

Sometimes the life we are leading comes to an end because of something we can’t control. For example, there are thousands of people in the Gulf area of Texas and Louisiana who will lose everything they have in their current lives. Possibly, many of these people will go on to a second stage of their life – one that is forced.

Sometimes, like in the case of Moses’ first life change, it is because of our own actions.

It is FOUR decades into this second life that God comes to Moses and it is here in this passage of Exodus we find Moses, a simple shepherd. He is living a life that is nothing like the one he started off with. He must have all but forgotten the life he started with. Life had become redefined, it had become simple. But he was still the person God made him to be.

So God came to him within a flame. A flame to brighten the long, hope-crushing night of suffering, slavery, and oppression that had fallen upon the descendants of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, Moses’s kin, and the people of the Holy One’s calling.

God had not forgotten Moses or his people. Even though his choices in life took him away from the place he once called home, he had never lived into the greatest reason that God made him – to be an instrument of God’s salvation.

God Brings Us Home

Home is most often where the fire inside of us comes from. When we step back into where we came from, life finds new meaning. His life was interrupted because of the fire of God burning before his eyes in a way he couldn’t ignore. It was in a way that he never could have guessed.

He may want you to notice that those things that bring you the most fulfillment, you simply aren’t doing them anymore. He may be telling you that something in your life is missing. Maybe that something is as simple as having a friend. Maybe that something is as serious as having purpose in your life.

If you have been living your life the same way for a long time and you can’t remember the last time you had a passion for life, maybe it is time to think about that. Do you have a passion for the life you are living? Even on the days you are dog-tired and collapse in a chair when you walk in the door, do you feel fulfilled?

If you can answer with a yes, then you are doing alright!

That answer may be a prominent “Yes!” If that is true, then I’m not calling to mind anything that you aren’t already aware of.

If that answer is “I think so…,” then you may be in exactly where God wants you; you may just be feeling uncertain. That’s okay to feel uncertain! Praying about the direction you have in life can connect you with God’s calling on your life. It may just take some time.

A New Path… or Renewing the Old One

Create a new path for yourself. The path you’re on may have its benefits (there is a good reason for still being on it), but what part of your life did you really get excited about? Last week we talked about spiritual gifts. This included gifts such as teaching, leading, generosity, and kindness. One of these or another of your personal gifts may have been tapped into years ago but now it is going unused.

My wife, Crystal is a good example of someone who spent years at a time teaching in different contexts. While now, she is beginning to find fulfillment through a children’s moment here during service, she has been teaching through her programs at the library through her new job as the children’s program director. But, before that when we lived in Missouri, she taught children’s church for years! We could go even farther before that. While there has been a store manager career and her college years in between, for years she taught dance classes to little girls at her dance studio. But those years in between while she was in college and then working to support our family, she strayed from her calling to teach children. It wasn’t until God called her to that path again did she find fulfillment.

Maybe you need fulfillment from some part of your life that has gone untapped. It isn’t that you need an entire life shift, but maybe just a sidebar to life. If you do, you may have thought about it for a long time.

Do you have a calling on your life that you haven’t followed? If you know there is something, why are you feeling uncertain? Search these reasons. God will most likely tell you to trust in his majesty and power.

“Who am I?”

Verse 11, Moses’ answered God with a question. Moses said to God, “Who am I that I should go to Pharaoh, and bring the Israelites out of Egypt?”

God said, “I will be with you; and this shall be the sign for you that it is I who sent you: when you have brought the people out of Egypt, you shall worship God on this mountain.”

God’s famous response was to say to Moses, “I am that I am”

God simply IS. His existence is the greatest presence above all creation! We will never know the vastness of his greatness! We can only come to recognize his existence, and that there is nothing greater. All other things of this world either were or are to be, but God always IS.

That is often all we need to know to begin to realize the importance of God’s calling on our life. For Moses, it seemed that a lifetime had passed, but finally those memories, those stirrings of regret caught up with him in that fire – that mysterious fire. That fascinating, awe inducing holy fire.

From that fire he couldn’t ignore, the Holy One spoke to him. Is there a fire for your life that God speaks to you through?

Moses left behind his former life and the people in it, but the Holy One called him back to it. Maybe you have left behind a former life and the people in it. Is there something God is using to speak to you about that life that you left behind? Maybe it is through a fire – the fire that burns within you. Or possibly God weighs on your conscience to give that loving nudge you need to hear. Perhaps you can actually hear God’s voice calling in your ear, “I have seen the affliction of my people who are in Egypt, and have heard their cry, [and] I know their sufferings.”

There’s a Fire in My Soul

For many of you there was a day when you were captivated in your hearts by the fiery intensity of the Holy Spirit. You felt the love of God for you in Jesus Christ, and you answered a call to follow Jesus. You were baptized and have since committed to lead your own children and invite many others to follow the way of Jesus led by the fires of the Spirit.

And perhaps this day, here and now, maybe in a hymn or a song we have sung, maybe in the reading, maybe even in this sermon, the fire of the Holy One has caught your eye. This fire of God has you turning aside to witness it, to better know it. The passion of the Holy One is moving to ignite your heart.

God’s presence illuminates a vision for all of us, but sometimes it is in a way we least expect it. Our Holy God brings deliverance from every form of evil, injustice, and oppression. His power conquers your fears, shame, and doubts.

The new life God calls us to, as we stand before His Holy Fire, has been provided to us. As the Spirit calls your name, all it takes to begin this new journey is to say the same words Moses said to God,

“Here I am.”
