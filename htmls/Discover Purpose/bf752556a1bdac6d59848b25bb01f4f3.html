Published on Jan 21, 2016

Prathama talks about how failing at a pivotal moment can create a new path of self-exploration and open unexpected doors. She reflects on how her own experiences across Bangladesh, Bulgaria, and the United States led to her work with social justice and what she has learned from serving these communities.

Prathama manages the Campaign for Black Male Achievement's Accelerator - the nation's only accelerator supporting high performing organizations working to improve the life outcomes of black men and boys. She leads the Accelerator's operations and program implementation, cultivates external relationships and collaborates with funders and major stakeholders in the field of black male achievement. Her other work has involved building a life outcomes dashboard for black men and boys, authoring social impact case studies, and providing strategic recommendations to consulting clients on performance measurement and financial sustainability strategies.
Prathama is a Bangladesh transplant living in Boston since 2007. She came for college, fell in love with the city, and never left.

This talk was given at a TEDx event using the TED conference format but independently organized by a local community. Learn more at http://ted.com/tedx
