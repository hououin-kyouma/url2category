Finding Your Purpose Through Your College Degree

by Krista Jennings
10/17/2014

If someone came up to you and asked, what’s your purpose? Would you hesitate for 3 seconds and start to mutter out what it is that you think your purpose is? If that’s you, you aren’t alone. I probably would have, about a year ago.

 

Thankfully, I have become clear on what my purpose is. I wake up every morning to inspire and to be inspired, by sharing knowledge that can allow for others to be the best that they can be. Hence, my committed work to my blog, Free From Perfect.

 

As a young woman, it’s a very daunting task to figure out what your purpose is. The interesting thing is that we don’t really focus as much attention and energy to finding and living our purpose until we finish college/university. So, I would say somewhere between 21 and 25.

 

After, I graduated from university. I thought to myself. Well what now? I suppose I get a job. So that is exactly what I did and that job, as much as I wasn’t passionate about doing the work I was doing, it taught me a few things.

 

1. No matter how unrelated your first job out of school is to your degree, you can learn a lot.

This job, allows you to continue building relationships and helps build more confidence in yourself. These are key pieces in finding your purpose. It will allow you to see what you do like and what you don’t like.

 

2. Your first job, is your first job.

Very few people really like their first job out of school. But be grateful that you can continue to be productive and start to build a grown up life routine. Having a routine is important in creating and building the balance that you want in your life. In order to have balance you first need to be happy. Be sure to make time for yourself. It allows for you to be happier.

 

3. It’s ok if you decide to change career paths from your degree.

If your purpose is unrelated to your line of work, that’s ok. The point is that you find something that truly ignites you.

 

4. Your degree provides you with the tools to think critically, develop analytical skills and continue to build your confidence.

These are all transferable skills. You will have a more global perspective that you most likely wouldn't be exposed to otherwise. If you know your purpose, great! And if you don’t that’s also great, I suggest you commit and dedicate time to truly finding what ignites you. While you’re on that quest, you will learn a lot about yourself.

 

Krista lives to inspire and to be inspired, where she shares her journey as a recovering perfectionist.
