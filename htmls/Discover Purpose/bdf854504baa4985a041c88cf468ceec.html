How much time did you waste in search for life purpose? There is a better thing.

Would you like to know your life purpose? Do you think your life would be much easier if you find it? Happier? Just better? I found so many internet people claiming they will help me to find my life purpose, my passion, my meaning of life, but there is one little thing – you don’t need any of these to live a happy life.

Let me explain in detail.

I am that kind of person, who always follows instructions, so when I find an instruction how to find life purpose in a weekend, or passion in one workshop – I follow those instructions thoroughly and often I see what is wrong with them and why they do not work.

Sometimes it is because they rely on emotion – enthusiasm or excitement and call it passion. (Passion is something you can do even if it is boring) Sometimes they simply lie to you, that your vocation is to do what you absolutely love to do. (Vocation is something you are going to do even if it is unpleasant) Sometimes they encourage you to be just more talkative (Go to 5 random people and tell them what are you excited about! – Sorry, suddenly I need to the toilet), and sometimes for them having business is the only answer (Einstein, Gandhi, Kafka and even famous CEO Jack Welch didn’t have their own businesses). I wasted lots of time on such things. Are you wasting too?

All those teachers claim that they have found their passion, vocation, purpose and so on. But most of them are total misfits, who for one reason or another cannot live in usual society. It is nothing wrong about that, just they naturally have a strong sense of some passion and they think this is the answer, and everyone should be the same.

But we are different.

Let me review all the common “ultimate life things” and explain each of them – why it could be misleading and how we will find it. At the end, I will explain what is the universal way to finally find these things. I call it the “Life Direction”.

So let’s fight against fake motivation and celebrate the critical thinking.

“Ultimate life things” that might be misleading:

  • Life purpose
  • The passion
  • Life mission
  • Life goal
  • The meaning of life
  • True self
  • Life direction (yep, this one might be misleading too)

I want you to understand me correctly. It is nothing wrong to have any of these. These are great things that helped many people to live more fulfilling, successful and happy lives. The problem is when someone doesn’t have one and dedicates the life to find one – he or she is wasting the time because most of these things are found indirectly (like the Chinese emperor already explained).

Let’s see them one by one.

Life purpose – the single answer to all your questions?

What is it?

Life purpose is a great thing – if we know it, it puts all the dots on i’s. This is the single thing that we can aim our life direction at. This is the reason why do we wake up every morning. This is an almost magical fit of our inner values, our skills and the needs of the world.

However, rare are people who know their life purpose, can tell it in words. What I observed, that more often there are people who do not even think about purpose, but their purpose is obvious in their actions. Two examples from real life: A woman who works in a boring job for the government but all her time is dedicated to bird ringing and she seems to be leading bird ringer in the whole country. Or another, who works in a kindergarten but all her time is dedicated to caving (a special kind of climbing mountains), and she holds the world women caving depth record. None of them thinks or talks about purpose, they just do what they feel they need to do.

And there are no people without purpose. Because it is about inner values and each of us has inner values. It is about the needs of the world and each of us gets money for some work from that world. Each of us has some skills. Values, skills, and needs meet at some point – what we can do is to make those meetings occur more often. To use our skills to do the thing both we and the world find valuable. This will be the purpose of life.

How can it be misleading?

First of all, internet people will claim that we can find life purpose in a one-weekend intensive workshop. Or after taking a 3 weeks course. Life purpose is found working with our values, skills, and needs of the world, adjusting them to fit together. It takes a long time and maybe even a lifetime. You don’t need purpose right now. It is OK if you realize your purpose before death.

They are telling us that life purpose is a decision. We need to think about it very very deeply, analyze benefits and drawbacks of each option and after a weekend of deep thinking, we decide that our life purpose is to help people. Or to help homeless dogs. Or to be a businessman. Or to write a blog. And then we start forcing ourselves to do what we decided. But if our actions start to contradict our inner values (which we often forget to consider while analyzing) we will lose our inner harmony (defined here) and this artificial purpose will not bring any happiness.

And the biggest problem is when they try to convince us to think big. Aim for BIG GOALS! But this totally contradicts the concept of purpose. If you feel that your life is about small things, why to force yourself to think big? If you have a simple dream – pursue it, no matter how simple and small it is!

What if someone whose purpose is to be wife decides to be a world famous singer? If someone whose purpose is to be an accountant decides to be a well-paid painter? And if someone whose purpose is to be assistant decides to start a multi-million business. Isn’t that nonsense? It is OK to think simple. If you want to find your purpose – do not force your mind, just listen what you think. Listen.

How is it found?

I guess you already got the idea. You already have a purpose, but you haven’t realized it. However, this does not stop you from fulfilling it spontaneously. If you want to realize your purpose – prepare for a lifelong journey:

  • Know yourself very well – your values are important.
  • Develop your skills in the fields that are important to you.
  • Listen to your thoughts and ideas.
  • Convert your ideas into actions.
  • Share what you have done with the world to see if they need it or not.
  • Don’t force yourself to aim for higher aims or think big. Think as big as you want to.

And one day you will find, that the world needs the skills you have developed in the field that is important to you. No magic. No mystery. But takes time.

The passion – in pursuit of positive emotions?

What is it?

To have a passion is a great thing – it is waking every morning and knowing what to do next, and, the best part – having lots of energy to do this!

Passion is more about how you feel doing one or other thing than what you think about it. It is just believing that what you do is important, useful and interesting. It is doing something without any need of affirmation, supervision and without comparing to others.

How can it be misleading?

First of all, we might have a problem if we try to think what is our passion. If we try to find it using intellect. As I said – passion is about feeling, so if we decide that this or that thing will be our passion, we might get disappointed. We might be excited about the idea of doing something, but actual doing might make us feeling not that nice. Or it becomes boring after some time. We can find passion only by trying things, not thinking about them.

Then we have people, who might not be that good at feeling excitement and passion (so-called thinkers). Like me. They always tell me – work passionately, do this and that passionately! Can I just work consistently, thoroughly, mindfully, wholeheartedly? Why arouse those emotions? Leave them for extroverts ;)

Also, we might be of those people who have many passions! (they have a community here) We may feel that there is something wrong with us because we cannot choose one true passion. But this is something like being an introvert – at first, we think that there is something wrong with us, but then we find some blogs on the internet and understand that everything is OK. The same is when you have many passions. It is not that you cannot choose one true passion, just you have several passions and they all are true. Your task is to manage your attention to them.

How is it found?

Passion is to be found very simply. If you do something and you feel passionate about it – this is it. Most of the people who have found their passion tell me stories how they tried something and hurray! This is it! So, if you haven’t found your passion yet, you need to:

  • Try new things.
  • Observe how you feel about doing them.

A way to passion is simple as it is, but you might need to try lots of things until you find one. My suggestion is to choose what to try wisely:

  • Know yourself very well.
  • Develop your skills in the fields that are important to you.
  • Listen to your thoughts and ideas.
  • Try those ideas.

And don’t forget to observe how you feel about those ideas that you try. Passion is felt by heart and not thought by the brain.

Life mission – change the world one more time?

What is it?

Life mission is also a great thing. To know what is the best thing you can contribute to this world and be sure, that the world needs it!

But the mission is something like purpose. Everyone have it, but not everyone has put it in words. Professions like teachers, doctors, artists, and firefighters have a clear and nice mission – to educate people, to save people, to entertain people. Some people might have a mission that does not sound that nice – what is the mission of a bank accountant? Definitely, he has one, but it does not sound that exalted.

How can it be misleading?

The problems begin when they try to convince us that the mission is always to change the world (I encourage you not to do that because I like this world). There are people whose mission is to keep the world constant, to maintain the structure, the foundation, to support it. But internet people say: “no, there is no mission in keeping the world constant, you must change it!“Again, this is like introversion – at first, you might feel bad about it, but later you realize that it is OK not to want to change the world.

And another problem. The word “mission” sounds exalted by itself. A mission! Something big and important! Like changing the world! And then people having simple occupations like the bank accountant or teacher begin to think that they are not following their missions in this life. I am meant for something bigger! That only causes disappointment and maybe a pursuit for some fake bigger mission. A simple mission is also a mission like a simple dream is also a dream.

Accomplish the simple mission first, and then you will be promoted with a bigger one.

How is it found?

How do big companies develop their mission statements? Some smart guys just sit down and think a while what are they doing. And then they put it in words.

You can do the same – think what are you doing right now? This is your mission right now. But if you want a bigger mission you can get one too, by following these steps:

  • Develop your skills in the fields that are important to you.
  • Try new things.
  • Share what you have done with the world to see if they need it or not.
  • Don’t force yourself to aim for higher aims or think big. Think as big as you want to.

And one day you will find that some skills you developed are really needed in this world. But you don’t need a bigger mission to live a happy life. You can be as happy as you want right now.

Here is a test to find whether your mission on earth is finished: If you’re alive it isn’t.
Richard Bach (a great writer)

Life goal – the event after which you can die?

What is it?

I am not sure if life goal is a great thing. It is a trick to keep yourself motivated and follow the direction of your choice. You may set up a life goal like “to teach my grandchild to read” and then you know that you must stay healthy enough to be able to teach and to convince your own children to reproduce quickly. But such is the nature of goals – they are always artificial.

How can it be misleading?

If you set up a goal for your life have in mind that the world is changing. If the goal is very specific, after some time the goal might not be relevant anymore. You are changing too. If the goal is set up based on your inner values – those values are changing too. If you pursue a single life goal – after some time you might find yourself doing something you are not interested about. And it is not that you set the wrong goal. It is because you have changed.

And also, setting a life goal takes away all the other opportunities you might meet along the way. I believe that a lifetime goal should be set only if you are absolutely passionate about some specific thing – then the goal will help you to focus on that direction and move forward. Otherwise, it is just a distraction.

How is it found?

The goal is not found it is set by you.

Setting goals for projects is actually a great thing. Like I said, you need a direction first, and then you can set small or big goals, to keep yourself focused and moving in that direction. You can do the same with the whole life – set a direction, and then set a goal to keep moving.

But if you want to have a lifetime goal – better think about the moment of dying. How do you want to feel that moment? Here are some examples of lifetime goals:

  • To not regret anything you did in life
  • To not regret anything you haven’t done in life
  • To be grateful for your life
  • To be happy
  • To feel inner peace
  • To be in harmony with the universe

With or without goals – don’t forget to enjoy the life.

The meaning of life – without it the life is meaningless?

What is it?

The meaning of your life is a simple thing. It is the difference between what the life looks like with you and without you.

This means the meaning of your life is not to be found. It is what you create. And your life always has a meaning, because the world would be just different without you.

How can it be misleading?

The whole concept of finding the meaning of life is misleading because the meaning is something you create with your actions. Each action changes something in this world, at least, a little bit. And this bit is your meaning. Bit by bit you can build a big meaning if you want to.

How is it found?

Your action is your meaning. You don’t need to find anything. The only thing you can do is to change the meaning of your life by changing what you do – your actions.

True self – because this one is fake?

What is it?

This is getting rid of the need to fake our actions. People might be faking their actions for many reasons – it might be a wish to please others, it might be a demanding job, or even not suitable goals. People might be faking themselves consciously and unconsciously.

Some psychologists or philosophers might find a deeper meaning of this term, but it is enough for us now. This definition is actionable and simple enough.

How can it be misleading?

It might become misleading when they make you think that there is some perfect version of you hiding in … you. The search for this perfect version is the act of not accepting yourself. Denying living with your true personality and wanting to be perfect. This is a harmful approach because you will direct your attention not to a possibly solvable problem, not to dealing with your drawbacks, not to nurturing your best traits, but to an empty pursuit of an unrealistic image. (Is there extrovert hiding in you?)

How is it found?

The true self is revealed when we stop faking. We stop faking when we stop lying to ourselves. However, this is not that straightforward procedure because often we are lying unconsciously – this has become a habit, a very ugly habit.

How to stop lying to yourself? A simple plan would look like this:

  • Learn about yourself and know yourself very well. Then you will know what is true about you and what is not.
  • Listen to your thoughts and ideas without forcing yourself to think a specific way. Then you will know what is really happening in your mind.
  • Convert your ideas into actions. When you initiate an action that was born in your mind you will make a step towards your true self.
  • Observe how do you feel about doing things that you do. You unconsciously know when you are unconsciously lying.
  • Practice mindfulness. Because it clears your mind – with a clear mind you can see your true self more clearly.

Life direction – step by step.

What is it?

This is my approach to deal with life – setting a direction.

The direction is some specific actions to keep doing or goals to pursue for next few years.

This is not the purpose – but I can set a direction before I found the purpose, and find it on the way.
This is not the passion – but the actions that I do to move towards that direction might become my passion.
This is not the mission – however, I might discover that it eventually is.
This is not the life goal – but it might be a smaller goal.
This is the meaning of life – because direction is nothing but actions I want to do next. And meaning is created by actions.
This is a direct way to true self – because if you set your direction wisely, then it is the direction towards true self.

Seems great thing, but can it be misleading too?

How can it be misleading?

It can be misleading when it becomes aimless wandering. Traveling the world just to escape yourself or your duties. Changing jobs or even life-partners with a hope to find the right one at last. The direction is not about finding. Just like the meaning of life, the direction is about creating your life, creating your way with the footsteps you leave behind. Not about running from yourself, but about mobilizing all your true and fake selves for some actions.

You need to define it and keep it. If you feel it must be changed – you can change it then.

The better the direction is defined, the closer it leads you to the purpose, passion, mission, meaning and true self.

How is it found?

It is super easy. You just need to decide what are you going to do tomorrow. Or right now after reading this article.

But to have a well-defined direction there are some steps to consider:

  • Know yourself very well – your values are important.
  • Listen to your thoughts and ideas without forcing yourself to think a specific way.
  • Observe how do you feel about doing what you do.

Same steps again? Yes the same, again and again. Because all those nice words like “passion” and “mission” are just different sides of one and the same thing, I’d call it the Way.

One thing – if you already have a sense of purpose, passion or have a good life goal, do not abandon them – you are lucky that you have them. But if not – try setting the direction first – you will find your passion on the way.

 

Let’s combine the steps mentioned in this article to make an ultimate list for finding ultimate life things:

  1. Learn about yourself and know yourself very well.
  2. Develop your skills in the fields that are important to you.
  3. Listen to your thoughts and ideas without forcing yourself to think a specific way.
  4. Convert your ideas into actions.
  5. Try new things.
  6. Observe how do you feel about doing what you do.
  7. Share what you have done with the world to see if they need it or not.
  8. Don’t force yourself to aim for higher aims or think big. Think as big as you want to.
  9. Practice mindfulness.

That, in my humble opinion, is all you need to do. Do you have anything to add?
