Looking for a Purpose

Sitting at a quaint café,

In a small village of Iceland,

I see the blue ocean, that makes the clouds,

I see the clouds, that make the rains,

I see the rains, that give fresh water,

I see the fresh water, that grows the grass,

I see the grass, that dwells on the mountains,

I see the mountains, that sheep graze on,

I see the sheep, that give warm wool,

I see the birds, that pollinate the plants,

I see the plants, that provide us food.

Every creature made by God, does something,

Every creature has a purpose,

A place,

A function.

But, who am I?

What is my purpose, my place & my function?

What am I sent to this world for?

Am I born to exist?

Am I born to make a difference?

Am I born to travel & write?

Am I born to explore beauty?

Am I born to help clients through my work?

Am I born to pro-create?

Am I born to do good deeds, to have a better life in the next birth?

But that cannot be my purpose,

For they are my own personal goals,

For the upliftment of my soul.

A purpose is meant to benefit,

The people, the world,

Just like the clouds, the water, the sheep and the birds?

But, then again,

What is my definition of world?

Am I my world?

Or is my partner my world?

Or is my family included?

Or does my world expand to all people around me?

Is my world my city, my state or my country?

Or does it include all the humans around the globe?

Or like Lord Krishna said,

Our world includes all the living beings that exist in the universe?

But, the bigger my world, the more difficult it is for me,

To find my purpose,

To make a difference.

If the universe is my world, it frustrates me,

For I am only a miniscule part,

And very little difference can I achieve!

But if I make a difference, find a purpose,

For the few surrounding me,

If the millions like me, around the world,

Make a difference to the few hundreds surrounding them,

We, together make a difference,

To billions and trillions!

Now, when I question what is my purpose of life?

I am able to find the answer,

In the little things that I do.

Another question comes to my mind,

Just like the sheep, are destined to be shaved for wool,

Just like the clouds, are destined to dissipate with rain,

Just like the plants, are destined to be consumed,

Is our destiny pre-written?

Or, do we create our own destiny by our actions?

Are we just puppets in the hands of god?

Or do we decide where our life goes?

Do we only exist till our purpose remains?

Do we die once we fulfill the purpose?

Or do we die for the purpose?

In the end,

One question that stays with me,

More than any other is,

Are we sent on earth for a purpose?

Or to create a purpose of our own?
