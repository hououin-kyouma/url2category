5 Tips in Finding Your Soul’s Purpose

Posted on November 13, 2013 by Tara Mullarkey

There were years when I knew I just wanted to help people.

I was interested in psychology and self-help.  I think this came from my personal pain of growing up in an alcoholic family.  

I started going to teen support groups, reading religious and inspirational texts and literature related to the effects of such family — I was learning how to survive. 

And I had a deep longing to share what I was learning with those who didn’t know.

But those interests were tempered by this need to survive, financially. I grew up in Connecticut, a predominantly affluent place, and money seemed like the important thing to pursue. 

I would hear frequently, “Money doesn’t grow on trees” and “Money’s not everything, but it’s right up there with oxygen.”

My mom struggled, however successfully, to raise us as a single parent.  My parents were immigrants from Ireland and the most thing they wanted was for us to have a better life — which meant more financial security.
 
Through the confusion of deciding what to study in college, I was also working part-time in office jobs — real estate, financial trading houses, and internet companies.  After months of deliberation, I decided that studying psychology or social work wouldn’t get me to money.  And money is where I had learned I should be, and where I wanted to be at that time.  

So I studied Economics. I struggled to get through my major.  There was a day that a tenured professor looked at me in a private tutor session of an advanced Econ class and said that maybe I should change my major.  

If I had known about intuition or “signs” then, I would’ve listened.  I would’ve known that this clearly wasn’t the path for me. 

Struggling is not the path of your heart.

But I was determined to make it work. And I did.  Can we say full steam ahead?  Essentially I “turned off” my inner voice, the deep inner desires of my heart.

I graduated with that Econ degree.  And very soon after graduation I landed a well paid job at a Fortune 500 institution.  Impressive, maybe. It allowed me to buy my first condo at 22 years old. Also impressive, maybe.  

Looking back, it placed me right in the system.  The one that needs you to need your job to pay your mortgage.  

Freedom gone.

I did that for 7 years, all the while ignoring the tug in my heart that said this wasn’t the life for me.  

I longed to do something creative.  I dreamed of helping other people overcome their pain. I desperately wanted to serve. And play big in the world.

Pushing papers didn’t serve.  Analyzing financials didn’t fulfill my heart. Fitting into company values felt like a betrayal of my soul.

I knew all along that it wasn’t for me, but I couldn’t (or didn’t) listen. In retrospect, I needed those years to be exactly where I am.

In some months in the later part of my corporate career and while finishing up my MBA in Finance, my spirit started to awaken.  I’m sure it was many years coming and the culmination of all of my life experiences thus far (especially the suffering), but it seemed like it happened suddenly and rapidly.

I took my self-help interest deeper and stumbled upon spiritual books.  They opened me up to my soul, the inner voice that had been nudging me all along.

Instantaneously, I knew I had a different path to take. Relief.

But I didn’t know what. Petrified.

In that time what I learned was this:

The spiritual seeker trusts even when the answers aren’t visible. Click to tweet it!

So I began developing trust, following love, and believing in something greater than me.

I believe that each of us are here for a very specific reason.  Each one of us has a very beautiful offering for the world. This is called our soul’s purpose.  

It can be anything from creating the most beautiful canvas, writing a story, to hairdressing or cleaning houses.  The point is that you feel it deep down in your soul that THIS is YOUR work in the world.

You do it with such love that your life becomes an offering.

I believe that if we all live our soul’s purpose, we would all be a lot happier, less stressed, less diseased, and more peaceful.

Your life experiences are what your soul chose way before you arrived.  They are to shape you so that you can share your unique offering and make this world a better place.

Here are some tips for accessing your heart space so that you can start to really listen to your soul and find your purpose here on earth:

1) Start to listen to your heart.
    When your heart is saying no, you may feel: drained, tired, without energy, sick or diseased, panic attacks, anxious, addicted, moody.

    When your heart says yes, you will feel: more relaxed, empathetic, motivated, inspired, excited, a magnetic pull towards someone or something, a natural high, like time hasn’t passed (time warp).

2) Meditation. Sitting in silence, breathing in the present moment, brings stillness to the fearful overactive mind so we can hear our true inner voice.

3) Suffering. What have been some of the major turning / pain points in your life? Think about what you’ve learned from them and how your empathy can be a catalyst for change in another.

4) Ask yourself: What would i do if i didn’t need the money? Concerns about money was the single most driver away from my soul’s purpose. I hadn’t come to know yet that if I follow my heart, prosperity follows. It’s the Law of Attraction.

5) Resist the self-doubt. I know, there’s something you love but you don’t feel your good enough or qualified enough to do it. Trust me, you know more than you think you know about what you love. And there are people waiting to receive this gift only the way you will offer it. Your passion will evolve as you do. Start now!

My path unfolded once I left everything that defined me until then. I went on an inner pilgrimage around the world and trusted every opportunity, person, and place that came to me.  Pretty soon, I knew exactly what I had to do. 

I kept returning to my inner voice every time the fear enveloped me. And I still do. It’s a life-long journey.

“There has never been and never will be another you.  You have a purpose, a very special gift that only you can bring to the world.” ~Marie Forleo

Start living your soul’s purpose today!  It can be as simple (and sometimes challenging) as sharing a loving smile with a stranger…that’s your purpose too!

Now, I want to hear from YOU! Are you struggling in your current work and feel that same nudge at your heart that you’re meant to do something else? Do you have fears about how to financially survive if you actually follow your heart?  Are you still trying to figure out what your soul’s purpose is?  Share your thoughts and questions here.  They help every reader here know that they are not alone.  And I love to hear from you!
