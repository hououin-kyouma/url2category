Note: Jump to the end of this answer if you’re sensitive, it’s a lot happier.

29 years ago I didn’t even exist. Now, what am I doing here on top of a giant ball floating in the middle of nowhere surrounded by other giant balls?

It’s the question that I’ve been pondering over for years. And it’s only now, that I’ve come to realize how often we underestimate how insignificant our existence is, in the universe. It will be worthwhile to get over some facts before we dive into the gist of the answer.

The reason why we ask such a question in the first place is because, we humans, have an inborn tendency or a need to find a purpose or a meaning in everything we do or experience, to make our engine revved up and keep on running. Without it, nothing that we do makes sense and we breakdown.

I call it the “Evolutionary Lenses of Optimism (ELO), <more about this when I release my book>” it’s a lie, but a necessary one. The same reason why a widow of a soldier who died in a bomb blast leaving no traces, still believes her husband is hiding somewhere and one day he’ll return to her and stand in front of the door with a bouquet of flowers. She’s not crazy, she’s just wearing ELO.

You probably already know, that we are made up of stardust, the same matter which were formed during the Big Bang. No more significant than a speck of dust currently orbiting some random planet somewhere in a far away galaxy. Living constantly under the mercy of the incredible phenomena (plural) that occurs in the universe.

A Gamma Ray Burst which took place millions of years ago, that high energy beam travelling across space for millions of light years, can silently wipe us all out without warning. And the fact that we consider it has never happened and doubt it’ll ever happen is because the duration of human existence is not even analogous to a nano second with respect to our sense of time as compared with the scheme of things of enormous proportions that occurs in the universe (in non-scientific terms).

But then, why do we still ask?

A dog doesn’t ponder over the purpose of life! It gets born, does what it was programmed to do by evolution, transfers its genes, links the cycle, then goes.

Now, let’s slide the scale a little bit to make it more relevant. So, what about Homo Erectus? One of our close ancestors who’s estimated to have an IQ of around 53. Not sure.

We mull over the purpose of life as our brain is more developed. Even today, some might not even ask this because of futility or ignorance or immaturity, which makes it even more irrelevant.

We ask these type of questions because we have seen enough and there are chemical/electrical impulses occurring inside our more developed prefrontal cortex triggering our existential awareness, not necessarily that there needs to be a purpose at all or the purpose of our existence appears magically.

This also explains why one might disagree while not realizing that he/she is proving my point.

Unless we develop an efficient lifespan extension technique or an immortality formula, just a 150 years from now, no one here in this world who are alive now would be alive then. We would be completely flushed over by a new generation just like we flushed the previous. Or better, from now if someone started pushing the red button once every 100 years, we will be flushed manually. Maybe only for some royal bloodline who carries a superior human codex will have the right to reproduce and repopulate, like those people we see today, whom everyone worships for a reason(sarcasm). Our generation is one of many tiny links in this endless meaningless chain. We are not even analogous to a flash in time.

It doesn’t matter if it’s a bacteria of today or it’s humans becoming super evolved to the extent that we can become half cyborg-half human if we wanted. Or if in the future, there’s a pre-planned life insurance program to revive ourselves into a new clone body by transplanting backed up memories, after death. Even then, life will still be pointless.

From an evolutionary standpoint, “We're protein robots controlled by our genes. Our only purpose in life is to create as many copies of these genes as possible to ensure the continuum - Richard Dawkins”. We are slaves to reproduction. And it’s a continuous meaningless cycle.

Perhaps, if you were an intelligent immortal higher dimensional being, eternally floating inside a bubble throughout space-time, who saw countless life forms like us come and go. Then I didn’t have to explain to you how pointless life is.

We are not as great as we think we are. It’s our ego that makes us seem great.

Here comes the bright side.

However, if you want a spiritually figurative and fabricated answer, the closest answer I can give you is: The purpose of life is to live a life of purpose.

It’s your one shot to live life. And you don’t need to try too hard to become something or to be like someone. And certainly, no one needs to die trying to achieve a dream.

We are wired to think that way because all of us were conditioned by the social environment from a very young age to believe that life is completely worthless without a purpose, which ironically, only serves the evolutionary purpose to improve our species, turning you into a victim of reckless evolutionary competition. And this system is even more pointless than life itself being pointless. It’s poisoning all of us to the degree that we won’t feel content or happy with what we are. So for once, screw the system. Break free.

Instead, do what you love to sustain yourself. Learn to be content with what you end up with. Try to cherish every moment with your loved ones rather than staring at the ceiling and pondering over the pointless purpose of life and wasting your life away.

Give it your own purpose and live the way you want to live. The sooner you accept all of what I’ve written here, you’ll free yourself from your own prison. I find it liberating instead of being degrading.

All the very best.

Do visit my profile here Grizzly Thiyam sometime, if you’re really interested in this crucial topic.

Now it came to pass, that the little soul yearned and yearned to know itself. And so great was its yearning that "I" one day said, "Do you know little one, what you must do to satisfy this yearning of yours?" "Oh what, God? what? I'll do anything!? The little soul said.

"You must separate yourself from the rest of us," I answered, "and then you must call upon yourself the darkness."

"You may choose to be any Part of God you wish to be," I said to the little soul.

"You mean I have a choice," ask the little soul.

I answered, "Yes." You may choose to experience any Aspect of Divinity in, as, and through you."

"Okay," said the little soul, then I choose forgiveness. I want to experience my Self as that Aspect of God called Complete Forgiveness."

Well, this created a little challenge, as you can imagine.

There was no one to forgive. All I have created is Perfection and Love

"No one to forgive?" asked the Little Soul, somewhat incredulously.

"No one," I repeated. "Look around you. Do you see any souls less perfect, less wonderful than you?"

"I see none less perfect than I," the Little Soul exclaimed." Who then, shall I have to forgive?"

Just then another soul stepped forward from the crowd. "You may forgive me," said the Friendly Soul.

"For what?" the little soul asked.

"I will come onto your next physical lifetime and do something for you to forgive," replied the Friendly Soul.

"But what? What could you, a being of such Perfect Light, do to make me forgive you?" the Little Soul wanted to know.

"Oh," smiled the Friendly Soul, I'm sure we can think of

something."

"But why would you want to do this?" The Little Soul could not figure out why a being of such perfection would want to slow down its vibration so much that it could actually do something "bad."

"Simple," the Friendly Soul explained," I would do it because I love you. You want to experience your Self as Forgiving, don't you? Besides, you have done the same for me."

"I have?" asked the Little Soul.

"Of course. Don't you remember? We've been All Of It, you and I.

We've been the Up and the Down of it, and the Left and Right of it.

We've been the Here and Now of it, and the Now and Then of it.

We've been the Big and Small of it, the Male and Female of it, the Good and Bad of it. We've been the All of it.
