That's a question that you have to answer for yourself. However, perhaps you should question why you're asking this question first. Do you feel like you -need- an explicit purpose in life in order to live it? Personally, I don't live life with -purpose-; I have goals and aspirations, but I don't have a singular purpose. I enjoy living for the sake of living because life itself is a beautiful thing; what a wondrous opportunity to exist on this planet for a century or so, standing upon the shoulders of an untold number of people that became before me, and gazing out into an unknown and uncertain future. That excites me, and invigorates me. I live for my morning cup of tea, for my afternoon walks, and my after-dinner coffees, but my life is also so much more than these things. Ask yourself: who says that a life needs a "purpose?" Consider living for life.
