Student Money Saving Tips

Student Money Saving Tips

The term ‘student’ has become synonymous with the word ‘skint’ – even with the student loans that are granted, many students find themselves struggling for money and juggling a variety of side jobs on top of studying to stay afloat.

When you head off to University, it will most likely be the first time that you live on your own without your parents to help you – which can be a massive reality shock if you are not fully prepared for it. Before you even head off to University, asking your parents to show you how they manage the household will be a helpful perspective – budgeting, paying bills, food shopping, cooking, cleaning and more. It may not sound like it would be very difficult, but without proper planning it is easy to get overwhelmed with all of the studying that you are doing as well as the socialising!

Now I’m not about to suggest that you don’t go out with your friends because that would quite frankly, be as useful as telling a cat to not miaow! Have fun with your new friends and enjoy Uni life, but be sensible with your student loan and you will thank yourself for it in the future. After all, on this blog I am an advocate of having as much fun as you possibly can as long as you have solid financial goals that you meet every month – don’t blow the budget out of the water!

Here are some of my top money saving tips for students:

Save Money on Food

Meal plan, meal plan, meal plan. Meal planning will save you a ton of money in your life, let alone in your student years! If you don’t plan out what you are going to eat, you can end up throwing away a lot of out of date food and popping to the shops every day because you have nothing in the cupboards. Check out my post about meal planning if you are unsure where to start.

Yellow-stickered food products will be something that will keep food costs down – all of the supermarkets tend to reduce at different times throughout the day so you will have to check when your local supermarket reduces theirs. Check out Be Clever With Your Cash’s guide to yellow stickered items.

Learn how to cook. This will be one of the most important life skills that you can learn that will keep you healthy and also keep your food bills low. There are a variety of different staple foods that you can eat – and make sure you keep eating your veggies! Have a look for ideas on fantastic frugal food blogs Thrifty Lesley and Reduced Grub.

Share food shopping costs with your housemates. If you meal plan together then you will save money on some of your meals – plus it’s good to be sociable!

Drink water instead of buying fizzy drinks as not only is it better for you, but it will keep your grocery bill much lower – also you will have more energy for studying (or partying!).

Use Approved Food. Approved Food is a website where you can get a large variety of food that you usually purchase, for up to 70% off. I did a post about them, and I have a free delivery code on there too.

If you need more information, then please read my post on how to save money on your food/grocery bill as hopefully it can help you out further – I’ve also included on there a link to a fab student cookbook.

Use Your Student Discount

All students (including Open University students) are eligible for NUS Extra cards which cost £12 per year which can be used both in-store and online in over 200 stores including 10% off at the Co-op which will help save money on your food shopping! In my first year of studying I didn’t think I would be spending any money to need to buy an NUS card, but in my second year I got one and used it much more than I thought I would. Save money on food, travel, clothes, gifts, beauty and more. There are some places that don’t widely advertise that they offer student discount on certain items such as McDonalds.

If you are a full-time student living either on your own in your house or with other students then you will be exempt from council tax payments, no matter how many of you are living there.

Transport

If you don’t need to use a car then don’t! Cars are very expensive – there’s petrol, tax, insurance, maintenance and repairs to contend with. Car insurance for young drivers is notoriously high, so it’s worth avoiding purchasing a car if you can. Walk as much as you can, get the bus (student discount available) or the train.

You can get cheap train tickets with a railcard which is only £30 per year (if you are aged between 16-25 years old). I also found a discount code on the Student Beans website for a further 12% discount off the £30 price. 

Save Money on Going Out

As tempting as it is to go out every night with your new Uni mates, it would be in your best interests to not do this – try and stick to the student nights as these will be much cheaper.

Drink at home before you go out as this will work out much cheaper and you can split the costs with your friends.

Challenge yourself and your housemates to have a Dryathalon for charity – these are usually done in September or October but you could do it any month that you want as it will be great for your health, wallet and also for fundraising money. If you all do it, you are much more likely to stick it out for the month too!

Laptops

Look at your course to see what kind of laptop will be required – if you just need it for typing up essays and doing research, you will probably not need an all singing, all dancing laptop that will cost a crazy amount to buy.

Apple offer excellent discounts on their laptops – 10% student discount which is amazing! You can purchase online through UniDays, or if you don’t want to sign up with UniDays then you can either go into store with your I.D. or ring up Apple with your information so that they can verify you.

Don’t discount purchasing a second hand laptop – many people find themselves in money problems and the laptop is one of the first things that can be sold to get them some cash because they are high value and of high demand. We purchased my husbands Mac off Gumtree from a guy who repairs laptops and then sells them on – we had no problems but obviously make sure you do some research if you are going to go with this option.

Clothes

It’s going to be tempting to buy a whole new wardrobe for your new adventure and all of the nights out that you will have – but this doesn’t need to cost a fortune. I am the Queen of frugal shopping, and no-one is ever the wiser that my outfits cost me £10 instead of £100+.

Use your student discount – most stores offer a student discount such as Topshop, New Look, Missguided, ASOS, Warehouse, Miss Selfridge – need I go on?!

Buy second hand. Don’t worry, not all second hand clothes look as though they have been worn a million times and never washed. In fact, most second hand clothes that I see tend to be worn once and then immediately listed on eBay – because we live in a generation now where nobody likes to be seen in the same outfit more than once. Head over to eBay or a similar site, and search for a label that you like e.g. Missguided, with your size – this will bring up a list of items from that brand in your size that you can search for. Most things on there are second hand but you can find an amazing bargain that has never been worn if you are a keen buyer.

Downgrade your brands. If you are living off your student loan, you would be wise to downgrade your brands to something more affordable. If you pick good items, then no-one would know that they are cheap. I always remember my friend saying that she had more compliments about her Primark shoes than her Kurt Geiger pair. And don’t worry – it won’t just be you that will be doing this either.

Swap with your friends. You have a new friendship circle of (hopefully!) people who are similar to you – which means of course, that you can swap and borrow each others clothes.

Have a No Spend Day

A ‘no spend day’ means simply, a day where you do not spend any money. This doesn’t mean that you need to stay inside your room with your nose stuck in a book all day, but just to be sensible and find some fun things to do where you don’t need to spend any money. I’ve done a post with free or cheap date night activities that can definitely be used for friends as well.

If you become conscious about not spending money on a particular day, this can help you stick to a budget and a financial plan – and can extend into a no spend week once you are used to it. If you pop to the shop every day to buy something, this is a habit that you can break, which a no spend day will help you with.

Keep Bills Low

You don’t have your parents to help you with bills, so it’s up to you and your housemates to find the cheapest ways to pay. Of course, you could ask your parents for advice and I’m sure that they would help you! Make sure you go on comparison sites and find the best deals for your house.

Try and keep the bills as low as possible by turning lights off when you aren’t in a room, taking quick showers…and a whole bunch of other money saving tips are mentioned in my posts: Water saving ideas and save money on electricity and gas bills.

Textbooks

Another term, another list of textbooks that you are given to purchase for your studies. The cost of these can quickly add up, which is why it is wise to look into the cheapest options.

  • Check in your library to see if they have them available. You could also check in the Uni library although these may get checked out quickly.
  • Buy second hand. Check eBay, Gumtree, Amazon, Facebook – ask around and see if anyone has one going, especially if you know someone who has been doing the same degree as you.
  • Rent textbooks. You can rent for a whole year with sites such as Vitalsource for up to 50% less than the full price, or Alibris.

Does anyone have any helpful money saving tips for students?
