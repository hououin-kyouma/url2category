5 Easy Money Saving Tips and Life Hacks for College Students

March 22, 2015/in Blog, Society /by University of the Potomac
Financing your education is costly. Without scholarships and grants, most people who attend college are strapped with massive amounts of debt after they graduate. So while going to college is money well-spent for receiving an education, saving every penny is worth your extra effort and time. Here are some money savings tips and life hacks for college students are who trying to minimize their debt burden. – Budgets are your best friend. Once you make a budget, stick to it! Unless, of course, you find extra money in the couch. Try free websites like Mint.com that keep you up-to-date on your finances and spending so you never run out of money, and squirrel away some in the process. – Textbooks are costly, find alternatives. As if college wasn’t expensive enough, sometimes a single textbook can cost $200, and you might not even use it. Discuss with your instructor beforehand two things: Is it required? Are there online alternatives? – Rent textbooks if possible. There are many alternatives for student who need textbooks for a discount, or for a temporary basis. Search around for ways to minimize your book cost by renting from reputable websites like Amazon, or checking out textbook boards around campus. – Travel doesn’t come cheap. But is also doesn’t have to cost an arm and a leg. If you live on campus, you likely won’t need a car. Instead, take public transportation (DC has many forms of mass transit). Check out student discounts for these services and save big each term. – Shop for food, don’t waste it on fast food. Food is a necessity, but fast food is filler. Don’t waste your money on fast food that is high in sugar and carbs, which will leave you hungry in a few hours. Hit the grocery store and stock up! Ramen and a bag of frozen vegetables has saved many people’s lives. College is hands down the best years of your life! Enjoy it while it lasts, and come out with as little debt as you possibly can. For more information on how we can help you, please  contact us any time.
