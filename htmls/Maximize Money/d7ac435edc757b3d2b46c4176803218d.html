For those not studying finance this year we thought we’d give you a few tips and links to places that will enable you to budget more effectively and get discounts when your new academic year commences.


Don’t buy new stuff for Uni

Most universities have book exchange schemes and second hand book shops on site for students to use. This website claims to be the largest second hand marketplace for students so worth taking a look.


Discounts and free stuff


For just £12 a year the NUS extra card gets you discounts on lots of things. Other sites to take a look at for deals and discounts are: myunidays.com and savethestudent.org

 

Gas and electric bills


You can save a lot of money by doing simple things such as:


  • Always turning the light off when you leave a room

  • Study at the library rather than at home (use their heating and lights)

  • Don’t overfill kettles as boiling just the water you need uses less electricity

  • Turn the temperature down. If the boiler isn’t working as hard it won’t use as much energy. By reducing the temp by just 1 deg C could save around £60 a year

 

Get yourself a job


Your student union usually advertise jobs locally and on campus so they should be checked out. Print off copies of your CV and spend some time visiting local businesses, introducing yourself and handing out your CV. Studentjob is a great site for finding tips on writing effective CV’s and tracking down local jobs on social media.
