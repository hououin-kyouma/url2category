Published on Sep 26, 2017

Brown University has launched a campaign to raise $120 million to replace student loans with grants that don't need to be repaid. The initiative aims to take the financial burden of repayment off of aid recipients. The policy should substantially reduce the average debt burden of its graduates. Brown President Christina Paxson said in a press release, "We're committed to making a Brown education accessible to students from all income groups, so we can continue to accept the very best and brightest students from around the world." Brown must raise $30 million before implementation of the initiative. It aims to accomplish that goal by December 2017.
