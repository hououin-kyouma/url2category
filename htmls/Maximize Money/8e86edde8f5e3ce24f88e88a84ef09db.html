College Earning: How to Make Money at School

  • Apply Online Back to School CreditBox CreditBox Loans Education Educational Graduation Save Cash Save Money School Books School Budget
  • Educational
  • 8/29/2017

While you’re at college or university, studying and earning your degree should be your top priority. However, if you have some extra time and would like to make some extra cash, there are a few ways you can make money while you’re at school. Plus, if you’re making a little extra money and putting it directly toward your student loans, you’ll graduate with less debt to worry about.

Pick Up a Campus Job

Many part-time campus jobs are just like regular part-time jobs. They can range from working the counter at a convenience store to helping out in the dining hall to working as a librarian. Chances are, you’ll be able to find an on-campus job that will fit your interest and skill set. The advantage to having a job on campus instead of one in the outside world is that campus jobs have hours that cater to your classes, so you’ll never have a conflict or miss out on studying because of extra work. Getting a campus job is a balanced way to make money at school.

You can also pick up a part-time job in the community surrounding your college or university. Though these employers aren’t obligated to respect your class schedule, most jobs in college towns will keep your class and study time in mind, while allowing you to make some extra money.

Become a Tutor

Are you fluent in a foreign language? Or maybe you’re a brilliant mathematician or savvy grammar-hound? You can use your specialized skill set to make money while you’re at school by becoming a tutor. There are lots of people at colleges and universities who need a little extra help learning the course material. You can advertise your services to them through Craigslist, online campus forums, or even on information boards in the school’s common areas. When you become a tutor, you’ll get to help someone, hone your own skills, and make extra money on the side.

Sell Your Wares

If you’re an artist, designer, or writer, you can use sites like Etsy and EBay to sell things that you’ve made in your spare time. When you’re selling your own crafts, you have all the flexibility you need to work once you’re done with class and studying. While your business may not turn into a big success overnight, you’ll still earn extra money every now and then to put towards school debt or spending.

If you’re making a little extra money, but it isn’t enough, turn to CreditBox. We can get you the cash you need, fast, for whatever you need it for. Just apply online today to get started.
