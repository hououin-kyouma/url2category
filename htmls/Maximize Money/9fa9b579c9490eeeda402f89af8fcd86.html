Tips to Save Money in College

0 Comments 23 August 2017

Just because you’ve made it to college doesn’t mean you should stop saving money. Here are some tips to save money in college.

  1. Rent second-hand textbooks or buy them used. Always sell your books back to a store once you are done with that class.
  2. Rent things such as a mini fridge and a loft for your dorm room.
  3. Research what businesses have student discounts and shop there.
  4. Invest in a coffee pot. Money spent at coffee shops adds up, so brewing your own coffee is cheaper.
  5. Instead of spending your free time off campus, keep an eye on free or cheap on-campus events. You can see a movie or live music on campus.
  6. Consider becoming a resident assistant (RA). This job often comes with major perks, such as free housing and meal plans.
  7. Keep applying for scholarships. The search shouldn’t end once you get to college. Keep your College Greenlight profile updated and we can continue to match you to scholarships.
  8. Limit eating out. If you have a meal plan, utilize every meal. If not, cook at home as much as you can.
  9. Once it is time to live off-campus, find a few roommates to split rent and other bills.
  10. Always walk, ride a bike or take public transportation whenever possible. Taking Ubers or paying for gas adds up.
  11. Save spare change in a jar. Count and roll the spare change once the jar is full. The amount adds up before you know it.
  12. Create a monthly budget to avoid overspending.
  13. Work each summer to have additional money while you are in school.
  14. Forgo a TV and watch movies and shows on your laptop or tablet.
  15. Cut coupons and download apps like Flipp, which clip digital coupons for you. You also can make a grocery list and find deals on items via the app.
