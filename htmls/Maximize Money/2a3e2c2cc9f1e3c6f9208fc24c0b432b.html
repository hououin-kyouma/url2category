How to Save and Make Money While You are In College

It’s no new discovery that college is expensive. Many students are financing student loans and graduating, according to Forbes, with an average of $40,000 in debt.  According to 30daystox, 60% of all college students receive no financial help from their parents. Between balancing long school hours, extracurricular activities and a social life, making money on the side is virtually impossible. Student loans and crippling debt are the thoughts that keep college students up at night even longer.

Life on campus can offer a whole new world for students filled with plenty of obligations and events for years to come, that usually also come with a price tag. A new outfit for that frat party you just HAVE to go to, tickets to the championship basketball game or a midnight pizza delivery. There are many ways to blow through your grad party money before you realize you can't afford guac at Chipotle. But don’t worry. There are also plenty of ways to save and make money without having to miss out on any of the fun college has to offer.

Here are a few school-hour friendly ideas to save some money while maintaining a full schedule.

1. Room and board can be expensive, sometimes even more than the tuition itself. A great idea to save money as well as contribute to your school is by being a Resident Advisor. These positions are often given free housing or at least housing at a discounted rate. According to College Data, the average housing for a public college is $10,440, so that is a lot of money you will be saving! Not to mention gives you a great highlight on your resume.

2. Learn how to cook! Some schools require you to purchase a meal plan, and if this is the case you can opt for a smaller option and meal prep your own meals at home. According to Money for College Project, the average meal plan for a semester is $1,600. Companies like Blue Apron and Plated offer step by step food instruction and healthy meals delivered to your doorstep. Just don't be THAT kid who sets off the dorm fire alarm in the middle of the night. 

3. During the summer months no one wants to think about school, but colleges usually offer online classes that you can take on your own schedule for the same amount of credits. This may give you the opportunity to graduate early aka saving you a lot on another semester of room and board. 

4. Cool the credit card costs. It can be easy to swipe this magical card and have no idea how much you are actually racking up. A night out there, a nice dinner with friends there and one too many fireball shots last Friday. Add that to the cost of books and other school related expenses and you have a stressed out student ballin' on a budget. According to Debt.org the average college student graduates with $3,200 in credit card debt. You can use a debit card that way you are only spending the money you have currently and not start the "I'll worry about it next month" trend.  

Now that you know some tips on how to save money, how can you make money so you can graduate ahead of the game? 

1. Take to the internet

The age of modern technology is in full force. If you find yourself receiving more than your fair share of retweets or favorites on social media or you’re constantly receiving compliments on how well you do your makeup, why not try making money off of your talents?

Social media websites like Twitter, Instagram and YouTube have been known to pay for the people who become popular on their platforms. For example, many makeup companies will pay popular YouTube bloggers to review their products . Similarly, Twitter and Instagram users will also get paid by various companies to post about their products. Its almost effortless, you're getting paid to be you, not a bad deal. 

If you already have a social media account with a healthy number of followers, flaunt your talents!

2. Sell your stuff

Hosting a yard sale may prove to be quite difficult when you don’t have your own yard, but you can always use the Internet as your own marketplace. There’s a good chance you own a lot of things you no longer want or need. You can sell practically anything on the Internet. Isn’t one man’s trash another man’s treasure? If you’re on the creative side, you can sell homemade crafts. Cafepress and Etsy offer an easy way to sell poster, t-shirts, hats, cups and tons of other items.

3. Teach what you already know

If you pride yourself on doing well in school and don’t have a problem with teaching others, put your skills to good use. Could you be a tutor?

You can broadcast  your qualifications, availability and contact information online,  tell your friends, family and other students in your classes that you’re ready to save their GPA. Let your professors know that you are willing to tutor any students who are struggling in their class as well. They might even start recommending your services and you could make some great friends along the way.

One of the great things about tutoring is that the hours can be pretty flexible. You can even tutor online through UniversalClass.com,  tutoronline.com, mytutoronline.com or many of the other sites that offer online classes and tutoring.

Not only is tutoring a great way to earn some extra cash but you can also brush up on your own knowledge in the process.

Are you a modern day Mozart? Maybe becoming a music tutor is in your future. Used to play soccer in high school? Try organizing a few willing younger kids into a team and coach them during your available hours.  Nearly any skill or passion you have can become marketable. Don’t be afraid to turn those skills into a profit. 

4. Get an internship

Internships do not pay a lot, but if you find a paid one you can work it in with your school schedule and also build your resume helping you in the long run! There are also many virtual internships, so you will not even have to leave the comfort of your bed! Not to mention a great resume builder and a chance to improve your future career options.

College is supposed to be a once in a lifetime experience, so do not stress out to much. If you plan ahead and manage exactly what you are spending money on you will be able to still go out with your friends without racking up bills that you will be stressed about the next morning when your bank calls you. 

Every college is different and every student has a different college experience. That being said, virtually everything good in the world costs money-there’s just no getting around it. If there’s one thing that can bring people together, it’s the shared experience of a constant shortage of money. These are easy ways to make money to help avoid the constant panic of an empty bank account.

Not only are these all great ways to save and make money in college, but they're also great opportunity to network. Market your skills and your personality and you may not only have a full wallet but a nice boost to your potential career path.
