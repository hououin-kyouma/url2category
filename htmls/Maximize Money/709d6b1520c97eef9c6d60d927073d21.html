Montana making money magic

High school students investing in their future

Student Assistance Foundation

Nonprofit Student Assistance Foundation (SAF) is seeking student candidates from six regions across the state to participate in its “Montana Money Magic” program.

Montana Money Magic is an effort that will help qualifying low-income students save for postsecondary edu- cation and improve their financial literacy.

“This is a great opportunity for Montana students,” said Melissa Huntington, program manager. “Students earn a 3-to-1 match on their savings, and gain useful money management skills.”

According to Huntington, students who earn the maximum match receive $1,350 to help them gain access to higher education. That means the funds can be used to pay for dual enrollment classes, tutoring, computers, welding supplies, books and more.

“Doing all the activities the in- structor assigned to us was the best part,” one graduate from the program said. “They really tied everyday life into what we were talking about.”

In fact, students who enrolled in Montana Money Magic last year in- creased their level of financial literacy by 42 percent from the beginning of the program to the end. Participants are given a pre-test and post-test.

GETTY IMAGES/ISTOCKPHOTO

To apply, students must be 16-18 years of age, in high school and from a family with an income of less than 185 percent of the federal poverty level.

Counties that are included in this project include: Gallatin, Park, Meagher, Glacier, Toole, Pondera, Cascade, Chouteau, Teton, Big Horn, Carbon, Stillwater, Sweet Grass, Yellowstone, Broadwater, Jefferson, Lewis and Clark, Missoula, Ravalli, Mineral, Silver Bow, Deer Lodge, Beaverhead, Madison, Powell and Granite.

To learn more, contact the Student Assistance Foundation outreach manager in your area at 877-COL-G4ME. For more information, visit Smart AboutCollege.org.

Student Snapshot

Terry Bradley

School: Montana State University

Year in school: Senior

Age: 23

Hometown: Huntley

Major: Elementary education with a science option

Why did you choose MSU?
It offered a quality teacher education program while not too far from home.

What was your biggest challenge your freshman year?
Being a first-generation college student, I was working multiple jobs and my academics suffered. I had to navigate an entirely new academic system all by myself.
