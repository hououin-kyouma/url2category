5 ways to be a savvy money-saving student

Written By Good Housekeeping Money | 25 August 2017

Be financially prepared as you fly the nest and set up on your own...

With today’s students expected to come out of university with an estimated debt of £57,000, it’s hard to imagine how they will cope financially in the coming weeks. But with careful planning, students can actually save significant money. Money expert Kalpana Fitzpatrick explains how:


1) Get the right student bank account
If you’re going to need an overdraft, go for a student bank account that offers the best 0% overdraft deal. Most students find an overdraft facility handy, but be sure to always pay in the minimum amount and stay within the agreed overdraft limit to avoid hefty penalty charges.
As well as interest-free overdrafts, some banks offer freebies; it could be that you find the freebies more valuable, so decide what suits you best.
 
Our top picks for the best student bank accounts:
Nationwide Flexstudent– – £1,000 0% interest-free overdraft limit in year one, which you can request to increase to £2,000 in year two of your studies and to £3,000 in year three
HSBC Student Account – a free £60 Amazon gift card, free 12 months Amazon Prime, and up to £1,000 interest-free overdraft in the first year
Santander 123 Student Current Account – a free 16-25 railcard for four years, which gives 1/3 off rail fares, plus get up to £1,500 interest-free overdraft.
 


2 ) Council tax discount
If everyone in the household is a full-time student, then you do not have to pay council tax. If you are sent a bill, then apply for an exemption.
If you are a student but live with non-students, then council tax is payable, but it may be discounted.
It is then up to the individuals as to how to then split the council tax bill.
See gov.uk to see what discount you can get.

3) TV licence fee refund
It’s likely that students will come back ‘home’ for the summer term – if so, then part of the TV licence fee is refundable.
According to TV Licensing, if you don’t need your licence again before it expires and you have at least one complete month left on it, you can claim a refund for part of it.
You can avoid paying the £147 annual fee altogether if your parents have a TV licence and you live with them out of term time. But this only applies if you watch TV on a gadget that doesn’t require an aerial and isn’t plugged into the mains at the time of watching.
If you are eligible, let TV Licensing know that you don’t need to pay. Fore more information, click HERE.
 
 
4) Bag the discounts
We all hate paying full-price for things, but luckily as a student there are plenty of discounts. Here’s are top ways to get them:
 
NUS Extra card – it costs £12 a year but can unlock 200+ discounts.Top discounts include 10% off at the Co-op, 20% off at National Express, 40% off at Pizza Express, 10% off at Superdrug and 20% off at WHSmith.
 
Unidays – a student discount website to help save on essentials, whether it’s technology, beauty, or fashion. It’s similar to NUS, but may feature different stores where you can get a discount.
It’s free to join, but you have to verify your student identity

Student Beans – another website that also offers discounts – alongside useful resources, including jobs – to help with student life. 

Apple Store – as well as a student discount, Apple is currently giving away free Beats wireless headphones if you buy an eligible Mac or iPad pro for university. Visit the Apple Education store. Microsoft also offers discounts – visit its education store. 

Take advantage of  Amazon Prime, which is free for students for six months, and then priced at £39 a year (usually £79). Once signed up, students can also get exclusive offers such as 10% off textbooks, Echo Dot and Fire TV Sticks, up to 20% off Amazon Basics products. Amazon is also offering those with a Prime Student subscription 6 months of its Music Unlimited streaming service for just £6 if they join now, and then £4.99 per month instead of the full price of £7.99 a month.   Find out more at Amazon's dedicated website. 

Virgin Media offers a nine-month broadband contract – it’s unlikely students will need broadband for a full year, so only pay for what you need with a short contract. Always shop around to get the best deal. 

5) Don’t overpay tax
Most students take on some kind of part-time work to help with living costs. Anyone earning less than £11,500 a year shouldn’t have to pay tax.
If you are automatically charged tax via pay-as-you-earn (PAYE) then you can reclaim it via HMRC.
