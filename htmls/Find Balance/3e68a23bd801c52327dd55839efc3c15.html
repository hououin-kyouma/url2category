Why a good work/ life balance is important

Our work can take up a large amount of our adult lives so it’s important to find a career and a workplace where you feel you belong.

After all, there’s nothing worse than being in a job where you aren’t happy, so it’s vital that not only are you right for the role you’re doing, but that the job and the employer is right for you.

The same applies to making sure that your work and personal lives are well balanced and this doesn’t just mean having so much fun that it begins to negatively affect your work.

On the opposite end of the scale, if work has begun to take over and you’re finding it’s taking up most of your time, then it can have a negative effect on the quality of work you provide, not to mention your health and over all well-being.

The biggest risk to not getting the balance between work and play right, is the potential for burn out, when people are working so hard that they’re not giving their mind or body enough time to rest and recover.

The effects of a burn out will leave you exhausted and affect the quality of your work, not to mention relationships with people both at work and at home. Such a problem can reduce levels of concentration and leave you more likely to make mistakes – something which can be detrimental in industries where accuracy is key.

Overloading yourself when it comes to work can also lead to stress which, as well as being detrimental to the quality and quantity of your productivity can also cause long term ill health – not a good thing for either the employee or the employer, who will have to invest time and money in finding a temporary cover or replacement.

Studies show that a happy work force can lead to greater productivity, which is why employers look to put in place schemes such as flexible working options that help employees achieve a good work/ life balance. The idea is also that it makes the organisation more attractive as an employer and therefore allow it to attract the best candidates in the process.

Successful and productive workplaces will also encourage a team to get to know each other and work well together. The employer might actively look to organise activities that are less about work and more about enjoying time together as a team and by doing so encourage a more harmonious and productive workplace.

Workers can also ensure that they are giving themselves the right amount of rest and relaxation, by taking time out from work on their lunch hour for example, or making the most of annual leave. People can prioritise their workloads by being realistic about when things need to be done and not taking on too much all at once. Another technique is to create boundaries between work and play so that, for example, work isn’t taking over the time when you should be relaxing, which will allow your mind and body to make the most of the down time.

At Wade Macdonald, we value the importance of a good work life balance, and we believe very strongly in finding the right candidate for a role. This means one which will make both employee and employer happy and ultimately lead to a long term successful working relationship. For us a positive workforce is a productive workforce and it’s this ethos which will ultimately drive success.
