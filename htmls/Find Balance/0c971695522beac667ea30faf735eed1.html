2 ways to find balance in your life

Sunday
Jul 9, 2017 at 7:59 PM Jul 9, 2017 at 8:41 PM

For most of us, life is moving at a rapid pace. We believe there is so much to do and so little time to do it.

Many move through life going through the motions and rarely take time to relax and do exactly what they want to do. Instead, we might focus on working too much or following goals that other people have set for us. Finding true balance in our life can be a daunting task that takes planning and follow though to keep us from falling back into our unhealthy habits.

If you’re feeling like you’re on a hamster wheel and can’t get off, try these suggestions:

* Don’t wait until your life’s imbalance has caused health problems. Start creating balance today.

If we don’t listen to our body, it has a way of letting us know that it’s time to slow down, take better care of ourselves and regroup. To have true balance we must feel happiness in all aspects of our life.

When we are feeling stress in one area of our life — maybe it’s caring for a loved one or looking for a new job after being laid off — it affects all other areas. When life is too busy, we don’t always take the time to make healthy food choices, move our bodies, spend time relaxing and having fun.

But doing those very things actually is what can bring us back to balance. There’s a reason why the message given on airplanes in the event of an emergency is to put on your own oxygen mask before helping others. Why is it that many of us ignore taking care of ourselves, but spend countless hours focusing on helping others?

Let today be the day that you start a new chapter of self-care. Start small, like making a healthy lunch instead of buying fast food or taking a walk with a friend. Small changes over time yield big results.

* Set goals for every aspect of your life now.

Just like women begin nesting in the final trimester of their pregnancy, when we are trying to find and create balance in our lives we need to tidy up everything around us. Is your house a place to relax, rest and rejuvenate or is clutter taking over making it difficult to be calm? If so, set a goal to clean up and organize the area that is causing the most stress first, then move to other areas. Getting started is the hardest part.

Are you as healthy as you could be? Evaluate what you are eating and if necessary make changes. Keep in mind that food is fuel. What we put into our bodies is what we get out. The same also applies for exercise — a body at rest stays at rest and a body in motion stays in motion.

Rest is important, but doing some form of exercise every day to move our bodies is just as important. Set health goals, write them down and look at them every day. Studies show that we are more likely to achieve goals if we do these things.

Are your finances in chaos? If so, this can also cause stress and imbalance. Seek help from a financial planner or credit counseling expert and create a budget and stick with it. We all need to know where our money is going. Do you?

And now work. Is your work fulfilling? Do you want to do something different but are afraid to make a move? Without risk, there can be no gain. Don’t let fear keep you from trying something new. Set goals and create change.

The most important goals you can set should be ones that deal with the important relationships in your life. Are you spending enough time with those you love or is work cutting too much into your family time? Having healthy relationships are important for life balance.

The process of setting goals lets us thoroughly evaluate all aspects of our life and be able to see clearly where we need to make adjustments.
