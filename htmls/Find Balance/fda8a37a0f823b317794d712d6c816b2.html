Published on May 22, 2016

In this video you'll learn ideas on how to find balance in life while focusing on self-improvement and which area to prioritize.

►If you found this video helpful hit Subscribe to support the channel and share the video with your friends to spread the word◄

It's often that I get asked "how do you find balance between fitness and business" or "how do I improve my social skills while simultaneously working on my health".

These are very important questions that everyone on the journey of self-improvement will eventually ask themselves.

Truth is we have only 24 hours a day, and whether we like it or not certain areas of our lives will have to be prioritizes.

An easy way to look at your journey of becoming the best version of yourself is if you look at 4 areas:

1 - Your spirituality and understanding of the world around you
2 - Business and the thing where you contribute value to the world
3 - Health, both physical and mental
4 - Social skills and relationships with other people

Now, each one of these areas requires a lot of time and effort to improve.

Each one is also a big journey, so now the question is which one to prioritize.

And the way I look at this is simply by analyzing each area on a scale of 0 - 10 and see which ones are lagging behind the most.

In my own life when I first started setting goals and moving forward all 4 of these were pretty bad.

I choose initially to focus primarily on health because I felt like I could leverage the fact that I'll have more energy to advance in the other 3 areas.

And that's how it went down. After handling my health through exercise, nutrition and good sleeping habits I focused more on business.

Then after I got some financial stability I started working on my social skills. It took me more than a year to be able to say that I'm happy with the way my social skills are growing.

A few years back I'd be too shy and anxious to say "Hi" to a person I didn't already know. And I was trapped in the social conditioning that was holding me back from meeting new people.

We are social creatures after all, and it's necessary to have the skills to be able to meet and connect with the people you want to have in your life.

Once I got pretty happy with my social skills I decided then it was time to invest more into business and finding my purpose on this planet.

Throughout all this time I was still working on my health and fitness goals so I decided to mix business with health. Because I realized I can make a difference in the world with the skills I've cultivated over the years by mastering my own health.

And soon after I started coaching others in the area of health, then this YouTube channel was born and the rest is history.

The point of the whole story is that you have to prioritize at some point.

There's no way to be immersed in all 4 areas of your life at once, it just doesn't work like that.

Immersion into one area will take months of momentum and time.

And then there's the diminishing returns. Getting from 8/10 to 9/10 is exponentially harder than just getting from 1/10 to 8/10.

That's just the way it is. To reach mastery you need A TON more hours and practice.

So at the end of the day you want to choose which area you want to commit to for mastery. And choose wisely.

Once you make that choice don't look back, go all-in and you'll make a difference in the world.

Balance at the end of the day is really about being happy with where you're at in all 4 areas of life and not from a stand-point of completion but from the simple fact that you're making progress in each area.

Or that you've consciously made a choice to maintain one area while working on the other.

As a human being that's really all you can do, time will pass anyway so why not commit to something great.

Hope you enjoyed!
