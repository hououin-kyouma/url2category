Back to school: healthy habits for students

Published 11:00 pm, Saturday, August 17, 2013

It’s that time again. Both parents and kids are getting back into their routines for a new school year.

Summer can be very hectic and often there is no set schedule for meals or activity. Forming a meal schedule for your family is an effective way to get kids back on track with healthy habits.

A good meal plan includes a healthy breakfast. Many kids will skip this meal, or eat pre-packaged sugary food because it is more convenient and fast. Waking up a little earlier to enjoy a healthy breakfast at the table is a great idea, however there are also healthy choices if you are short on time. Fruit and nut bars, fresh fruit and smoothies are healthy foods to grab when heading out the door. Make your own healthy breakfast choices ahead of time like whole grain pancakes or muffins and put them in freezer to heat and eat.

Packing a lunch for your kids is a healthy alternative to the school cafeteria. If your child is a picky eater, take him or her grocery shopping and let them pick out healthy foods they would like to eat. Kids are more likely to eat what they pick out for themselves. Make sure to pack a healthy variety of foods including milk or dairy, vegetables, fruit, whole grains and protein. Feel free to mix it up with healthy recipes so lunch doesn’t get boring.

The change from summer to fall can also lead to a decrease in physical activity. After school, kids are likely to come home and spend hours watching TV or playing on the computer. Limit these activities by signing them up for after-school programs or activities like sports, dance or martial arts. In the evening, take a walk or bike ride as a family.

Eating dinner together as a family is part of maintaining a healthy routine. When family members eat separately they are more likely to choose unhealthy foods and eat at odd, inconsistent hours each night. This can also contribute to an unhealthy sleep schedule. Kids who do not maintain a good sleep schedule are more prone to bad behavior and trouble in school. A healthy routine needs to be consistent throughout the day.

Too much time in front of the computer or television screen can be considered unhealthy, but there is such a thing as healthy screen time. There are now apps that reinforce the healthy habits you teach your kids in the form of games. Apps like “Pick Chow” allow kids to create healthy meals and email them to their parents with recipes and shopping lists included. Apps like “Work it off” teach kids about the calories they eat and burn.

Consistency plays a huge role in maintaining a healthy routine. Reinforcing good habits in your kids will lead to a happy and healthy school year.

For additional resources, apps, recipes, and more visit www.healthylivingmc.com.

Emma Cooper is a senior Mass Communication Major at Sam Houston State University. She is a summer intern for the Montgomery county United Way Healthy Living Alliance.
