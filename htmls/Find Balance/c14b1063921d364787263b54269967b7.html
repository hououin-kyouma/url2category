How to find balance in life

NATALIA & BRIANNA O’TOOLE, Townsville Bulletin
April 22, 2017 12:00am

It is when a passion becomes an obsession that issues arise. This is not limited to the bounds of exercise and eating, rather it is relevant in all things.

When something begins to consume a person and affect the way daily tasks are carried out, or the way a person thinks, this is a red flag. Obsessions can give rise to relationships issues, mental and physical health problems and can be costly. For these reasons, it is important to ensure we have balance in all things we do, both work and leisure.

Sometimes people are not aware of this certain issue of imbalance and obsession in their own life. Perhaps you are being consumed by work and forgetting about the importance of relaxing and unwinding when coming home.

This can put a strain on relationships, raise stress levels, cause health issues and bring tension into relationships.

Likewise, while it is great for Australians to develop a passion for health and fitness, this too can go too far. Particularly for women, who feel pressure to look a certain way, eating disorders can develop due to a fixation on becoming aesthetically pleasing.

When it gets to a point when you are no longer able to enjoy a meal with a friend due to questions playing over in your mind – “What oils did the chef use?”, or “How many calories are in this?” – it becomes clear an obsession has gone too far.

How do you maintain balance? Here are a few tips to help you enjoy a healthy balanced lifestyle.

1. NEVER COMPROMISE SLEEP

When life is busy, whether it be with work, kids, university or social events, never compromise sleep. Sleep is so important and often people are willing to sacrifice a full night’s sleep to stay later at the office, get to the gym before the kids wake up or cram in some last-minute study. Sleep is the most imperative element to ensure balance and wellbeing.

2. 5X5 RULE

If it won’t matter in five years, don’t spend more than five minutes worrying about it. Never skip catching up with friends or your partner because of worries about finding something healthy when eating out. In five years’ time, having had salad dressing on your salad, or a light spread of butter on your toast won’t matter. So while you should make an effort to have a healthy diet, it’s important to not let it consume you.

3. GIVE SOCIAL MEDIA THE FLICK

For females, in particular, it is the constant stream of images of unrealistic figures which can bring people’s self-image down, by comparing yourself with edited and deceiving photographs. Ditch social media and realise your own worth. You will be amazed at how much better you feel when you have a social media detox.
