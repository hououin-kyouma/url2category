Work/Life Balance- How to Reduce Stress

Stress not only affects us psychologically, it can cause physical health problems as well, including high blood pressure, headaches, stroke, lack of sleep and weight gain. Everyone can reduce some of the stresses we experience each week by creating a personal plan to do so that builds a better work/life balance.

List Your Sources of Stress

The first step to take on the journey to improving your emotional health is to write down the things that cause you stress each week. These can include:

  • Finances
  • Heavy activity schedule
  • Personal relationships
  • Job insecurity
  • Heavy workloads
  • Children
  • Lack of sleep
  • Caregiving
  • Neighbor problems

Identifying stressors at work can help you plan your workweek better, improve your time-management skills, determine if you need to drop some responsibilities, decide whether or not you need to improve some specific skills, or look for another job. Identifying stressors in your personal life can help you reduce your activities and avoid unhealthy relationships.

Review Your Work Goals

If you work, your stress increases if you feel as if you’re in a rut, getting passed over for promotion or falling behind schedule in your career. This happens when you have either unrealistic career goals (which can be caused by the dates you have for achieving them), or no plan to achieve them. Write down where you want to be in your career next year, three years from now and toward the end of your career. List the titles you want to hold, salary you want to make, the skills you want to have and the dates you want to achieve these goals.

Next, write down exactly what it will take to achieve those goals and put together a plan (including deadlines) to achieve them. Drop unrealistic goals or modify them. Set new goals if things have changed during the past few years.

Review Your Work Schedules

In addition to looking at your work goals, look at the amount of work you do each week. Are Thursdays and Fridays typically more hectic as you play catch up? You can reduce stress by getting more work done earlier in the week, working more efficiently and finishing early instead of on deadline. Decide if you need to delegate more, take less work home, and stop sending emails and texts at night and on the weekends. Determine if you need more software, equipment or skills to reduce your workload.

Review Your Life Goals

If you haven’t thought about your personal hopes and dreams lately, take time to do so. They might be simple, such as taking up gardening, learning to play tennis or traveling. They might be more complex, such as opening a business, starting a family, adopting your first pet or moving to a new area. Use a separate sheet of paper for each goal and write down the steps you’ll need to achieve your life goals. Include deadlines, and identify people who can help you get there.

Review Your Weekly Schedule

Now that you’ve looked at your work and life goals, examine your weekly schedule, both at work and at home. Are you trying to cram too much in? Are you trying to reach important goals too soon, or attempting to reach too many at once? Are you cramming too many kids’ activities into your days and weekends? Can you spread your activities out evenly during the week to reduce those one or two days that stress you each week? Make an effort to create work and life to-do lists each week to help you manage your time better and reduce your stress.

Re-Evaluate the People in Your Life

Unfortunately, friends, co-workers, neighbors and family members can create serious stress in our lives. They might be great people, but they have their own problems and frequently lean on you, bringing you down. They may be negative people who gossip and complain each day. They might pick at you, criticizing you without even realizing it. List the 10 most important people in your life, then the next 10. Determine if you need to cut ties with any of them, call them less frequently, or sit down and have a talk with them.

Take a Wellness Checkup

Examine your health by reading our blog post, Wellness Resolutions for 2017. You’ll find numerous, simple ways to help you improve your physical health and reduce stress. If you haven’t scrolled through the library of Flatout blog posts, do so to find lots of articles that can increase your enjoyment of life and improve your quality of living.

Volunteer

It can be as simple as walking dogs on Saturday morning at your local animal shelter or playing checkers with seniors at your local assisted living facility. Giving back helps you feel good about yourself as your focus on others. Flatout founders Stacey and Mike Marsh knew that as their restaurant, bakery and national business grew, they wanted to give back to community organizations and they made sure they put their employees and customers first. Thinking of others instead of focusing only on their goals helped them create a multi-million dollar business that continues to grow.

Get Away form it All

When was the last time you took a vacation – even a long weekend? I mean a real vacation – one where you’re not checking in with the family, boss or co-workers every few hours via phone, email or texts? Schedule the time and budget the money for a true vacation that lets you step back and see the world through a different set of eyes. This can significantly alter your perceptions of the importance (and lack of importance) of things that are causing your stress.
