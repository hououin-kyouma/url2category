10 TIPS TO REDUCE STRESS NATURALLY:

1. Deep breathing exercises are an excellent tool to calm the nervous system and induce relaxation, but to be effective has to be done correctly and regularly with concentration.

2. Aromatherapy has been used to aid relaxation for hundreds of years. There are many essential oils that are well known for their calming qualities. Lavender, Geranium and Chamomile being the most popular ones.

3. Massage is one of the most enjoyable and indulgent ways of reducing stress, everyone loves a relaxing massage. Massage is always more effective and enjoyable when performed by someone else, but if this is not possible self- treatment can also be beneficial.

4. Herbal teas, this is one of the quickest and most convenient methods of stress reduction. The most popular one being Chamomile.

5. Smile, even if you think you have little to smile about, smiling will make you feel better. According to some experts smiling releases hormones called endorphins, or the feel good hormones which help reduce stress levels The added benefit of smiling is that a smile is not only good for you but all those around you as well. Looking at a smiley face instantly makes everyone more relaxed, so spread the good chi and smile!

6. Reduce foods that stress, the biggest culprits are caffeinated drinks, chocolate and most processed foods.

7. Have some me- time. Always being on the go, and always putting the needs of others can equate to self neglect. Just a simple manicure or a relaxing facial can be a really good psychological pick me up during times of stress.



8. Listen to calming music, the most calming music seems to be nature sounds rather than music with instruments,

9. Get quality sleep, our ability to handle even basic every day activities can be greatly compromised when we do not sleep well, let alone stressful situations. Going to sleep at the same time each day, and not consuming stimulating foods and drinks in the evenings can help greatly.

10. Count your blessings. This is an ancient technique that is, and has been used in stressful situations by saints, prophets and healers worldwide. The idea is that no matter how awful the situation is, or unfair the scenario, there is always someone worse off than you. So by focusing on those less fortunate than ourselves and appreciating all the things that we are blessed with, we won't feel so negative.
