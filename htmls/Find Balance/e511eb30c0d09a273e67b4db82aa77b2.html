    • Financial Stress and How to Reduce It

    Financial stress can have a great impact on students. Students are overwhelmed with stress every day. Whether it’s academic, work, social, personal or financial, there may be some lingering worry, anxiety and stress in your life that does not seem to wither away. If stress is not managed properly, it may lead to serious health issues in the future. UC Davis Student Health and Counseling Services is dedicated to reducing an individual’s stress and increasing their overall well-being. The information provided here is aimed to assist students in reducing their financial stress and offer possible solutions for the future.

    Financial Stress: How Bad is it Really?

    According to a 2012 research study provided by Inceptia (pdf), one third of students reported that financial stressors have had a negative impact on their academic performance. The study also found that those students who work more than 20 hours per week were more likely to report that financial stress has negatively impacted their academic performance. Students found that the more they worked, the worse they did academically. In turn, students also reported that because they were under an intense amount of financial stress, they reduced their academic course load in order to reduce their overall stress. This seems a little backwards doesn’t it? Students are feeling like they need to lessen their academic load in order to keep their work schedule consistent, so they can pay for tuition and college essentials. However, research is showing that this trend is harmful to mental health. Students should be spending their time advancing their studies, but because they are under intense financial stress, they cannot dedicate as much time to academics as they do to work.

    What Can Be Done to Reduce Financial Stress?

    A great way to reduce financial stress is to learn how to budget your finances and to stick to the budget you create for yourself. CashCourse is a great online resource that helps you plan out your finances. CashCourse was created by the National Endowment for Financial Education (NEFE) as a resource available at no charge to students who are seeking financial education. The website provides a customizable set of personal finance tools to help you manage your money while teaching you real-life financial skills. When you sign up for CashCourse you are able to choose which tools you want to use—this way you are not bombarded with a surplus of tools that you may not be interested in. This online resource also allows students to understand how much of their money they should dedicate to rent, bills, tuition, groceries and fun activities. Students can make money plans in order to pave the way for a great financial future. CashCourse enables students to make a budget that fits their financial needs. It also encourages students to stick to the financial path that they create for themselves so that they feel financially secure.

    Financial Security for the Future: Start Saving. Start Saving Now.

    As a college student it can be hard to think about long-term financial planning due to the high financial demands you face every day (such as tuition, rent, bills, groceries and social events). However, it is extremely important to always be planning ahead financially—especially if anything unexpected should happen. The best thing to do to plan ahead financially is to start saving. Start saving now. You may be thinking that you are young and full of energy and have plenty of time to start saving. This is true; however, there is no time like the present! With that being said, it is crucial that you start saving money as soon as you can. This does not mean that you need to start putting away large percentages of your paychecks into savings accounts right away. But do think about allocating a certain percentage of your funds (regardless of how little) into a savings account in order to be better prepared for the future. Life happens. And since no one can predict the future it is essential to have something saved up just in case the unexpected should happen and you need to use that money.

    Dedicated to a Healthy Student Body

    The mission of Student Health and Counseling Services is to enhance the physical and mental health of students in order to help them achieve academic success, personal development and lifelong wellness by providing an integrated program of quality, accessible, cost sensitive and confidential healthcare services, tailored to their unique and diverse needs and to assist the University community, through consultation and education, to develop a healthy campus environment consistent with UC Davis "Principles of Community".
