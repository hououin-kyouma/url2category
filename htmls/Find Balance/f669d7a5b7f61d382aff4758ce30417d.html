Breakfast Games Fuel Healthy Habits for Students

03/31/2016

Tags: Breakfast, Fuel Up to Play 60

A whole team of celebrities and community leaders came together to celebrate Dairy MAX’s 2nd Annual Breakfast Games at three locations across Texas and Oklahoma last March. 

With the support of the Dallas Cowboys, Houston Texans and Fuel Up to Play 60, the nation’s largest in-school wellness program, Dairy MAX partnered with three school systems to bring this interactive, breakfast-centric event to schools in Leander ISD, Aldine ISD and Oklahoma City Public Schools (OKCPS). 

Each event kicked off with the schools’ FUTP60 teams sharing breakfast in the cafeteria with an NFL star and other special guests, like local dairy farmers. Then the NFL player led the teams into the gym, Breakfast Games torch held high, to meet hundreds of their classmates.

After that, the games began! The FUTP60 student teams competed in a three-stage indoor obstacle course for gold, silver and bronze medals as classmates cheered them on.

Two of the 2016 Breakfast Games took place during National School Breakfast Week. Leander hosted the games at Pleasant Hill Elementary on March 8, with celebrity guests including Dallas Cowboys strong safety Barry Church, dairyman Bryan Lanting and Texas Department of Agriculture Commissioner Sid Miller. The next games happened in Aldine at Orange Grove Elementary School on March 10, where students met former Houston Texans player J.J. Moses, dairyman Brennan Ribiero and USDA Deputy Under Secretary for Rural Development Lillian Salerno.

The third event capped off National Nutrition Month on March 22 after spring break in OKCPS at Southern Hills Elementary. Students had breakfast with Dallas Cowboys center Travis Frederick and former Miss Oklahoma 2011 (and first runner-up to Miss America 2012) Betty Thompson.

Looks like fun, right? It’s also a great way to show your students why breakfast is important. Learn more about running Fuel Up to Play 60 in your school or district.
