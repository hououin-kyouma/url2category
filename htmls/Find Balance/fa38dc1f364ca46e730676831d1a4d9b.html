Healthy Habits
Healthy Habits will aim to help college students make healthy choice while eating on campus.

Project Description

Our objective is to help college students make the healthier choices while eating at the campus diner. We will enroll students in a team challenge program, and post daily incremental challenges, such as eating 1 cup of fruit a day. The participants will then post a picture of the food described in the challenge and a subsequent picture of the emptied plate to prove they completed the challenge. Points will be assigned to teams for every challenge completed, and teams will compete to be on a leaderboard and receive the final prize. We will reward the participants with food from health-oriented establishments and opportunities to engage in fitness activities. College students lack the incentive to maintain healthy eating habits and be educated about dietary needs. Once students leave home, they have to start making important decisions about how to care for their own health and nutrition. If a student starts developing poor eating habits, those habits can hinder academic performance, physical health, and mental health. Conditions such as diabetes and obesity are linked to poor eating habits. Developing health habits is crucial to lifelong health, and college is an opportunity to shape these habits in a positive way. Our reach will consist of students at UMD who are on a meal plan and frequent the university diners. We hope to focus on gathering groups with an investment in nutrition, such as sports clubs and health organizations, as well as everyday students who want to improve their diet. Moving forward, we would be able to expand to other campuses and areas where people feel as though they are not motivated enough by their peers and surroundings to eat healthy. In the short-term we hope to motivate students to make incremental changes in their diet and shift towards consciously eating healthier every day. In order to do so, we also hope to build a support network of peers in our Facebook group to motivate one another and keep participants dedicated to the challenge. In the long-term we hope to educate students about the consequences of poor diet and the benefits of making healthy choices, and instill healthy eating habits that they will carry throughout life.
