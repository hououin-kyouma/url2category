5 Healthy Habits Students Should Keep

Student life is best remembered as years of memorable adventure. The chapter of life where you develop your skills, talents, and your character, being a student is not all fun. You juggle multiple things at once, feeling as if nothing can be worse. And it may feel that way because you’re leaving out an important aspect of your student life: your wellness. Keeping yourself primed and ready with a few health habits is key in keeping collected despite the demands of student life.

  1. Schedule

Time management and organization are important when you’re tackling multiple tasks. And multi-tasking is not an option for most. Create your own schedule and make sure you stick to it. Start with scheduling and prioritizing your activities after school. Should you go straight to studying and doing homework? Maybe have a short leisure break with neighborhood friends? Or take a nap for a little while?

 

  1. Sleep

Speaking of naps, we can never stress enough how important sleep is to the growing body. It’s more than growing out your bones to its best length. Getting plenty of Z’s feeds oxygen to the brain and allows for our body to function properly. So instead of pulling an all-nighter for a chance to perform well in class, get a shut-eye and get up early to prepare.

 

  1. Exercise

Your P.E. classes aside, doing simple exercises in the morning or before going to bed has its perks. Countless researches and studies are consistent with a roster of benefits that include improving your mood, your focus, concentration and memory retention.

 

  1. Eat

Improving your academic excellence shouldn’t just end with physical activities. Aside from being central to growth and development, proper nutrition works well with exercises to optimize how your body moves and how your mind functions. Make it a priority, and be picky about what you eat!

 

  1. Breaks

Yes, taking breaks once in a while can help boost your school performance? Take your mind off of school for a bit and go for a 5-minute walk! Again, movement stimulates the brain to perform better. Brain breaks or what others call “sanity breaks” are short but efficient in charging you up with a pump to your blood flow and a kick of oxygen to your brain.
