Having fun learning healthy habits

Listen
Gateway health students teach children at wellness event

By Nathan Mack

Gateway Barbering students let Boys and Girls Club members try their hand at hair cutting and trimming in the Barbering Studio.

The Boys and Girls Club – a youth guidance organization promoting health, education and other developmental skills – teamed up with Gateway’s Dental Assistant program to bring the club’s elementary members to Gateway to receive a free dental screening in partnership with area dentists and to learn about healthy living. The Physical Therapist Assistant, Nursing, Dental Assistant and Barbering programs all provided young visitors with games and activities to teach things like how to exercise, eat healthy and take good care of their hygiene.

Nursing students taught kids how to maintain a healthy lifestyle through exercising with everything from jump ropes to cans and other everyday objects from around the house. Another group of Nursing students taught its Boys and Girls student groups about the importance of eating fruits, vegetables and all-around nutritious foods from the food pyramid, or “My Plate,” categories.

Gateway Cosmetology students offered free manicures and haircuts to help teach the children about the importance of taking care of themselves. The Physical Therapist Assistant program set up a fun obstacle course spanning over half of Academic building’s atrium to get kids to moving and having fun. The Dental Assistant program taught kids how to brush and floss their teeth while even offering free teeth cleanings, and the Surgical Technology program focused on teaching kids the basic biological aspects of what kinds of germs get on their hands and how to clean them thoroughly.

“If kids are going to take anything away from (the wellness event), it’s that they should be more active instead of sitting on their electronics and realizing exercising doesn’t have to be boring, it can be fun,” said Amy Lumley, a Nursing student who taught the children about exercise and heart health.

Physical Therapist Assistant students described the goal of her obstacle course in a similar light, saying “we want the students to know they can (exercise) on their own, at home, and they don’t have to have any expensive equipment.”

Overall, the Boys and Girls Wellness event was a great way to teach and inspire kids to take better care of themselves, and the event was a great way for Gateway students to get hands-on practice with the career they hope to be entering into once they have graduated.
