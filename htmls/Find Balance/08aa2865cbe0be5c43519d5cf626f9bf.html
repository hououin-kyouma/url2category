            Healthy Habits | Everyday Learning

            This video demonstrates two of the most important habits we can teach children: covering your mouth when you cough or sneeze and washing your hands. In this video, a young girl demonstrates the correct procedure for performing these two simple tasks. It’s important to introduce preschool-age children to healthy habits and to reinforce these habits while they are young so that they become automatic.

            This resource is part of the KET Healthy Me collection.

            Keeping My Body Healthy: Hand Washing
            In this lesson, students will learn about dirty hands and how to clean them.

            Vocabulary: germs, clean, dirty, wash, soap

            For the following lesson, you will need:

            • Glitter
            • Hand lotion
            • Soap and water
            • Flashlight

            Step-by-Step Procedure:

            1. Introduce the lesson by asking the children to look at their hands. Are they clean or dirty? How can you tell?
            2. Encourage the children to wash their hands.
            3. After the children have washed their hands, ask them to sit in a circle and apply hand lotion.
            4. Sprinkle glitter on hands and talk about germs.
            5. Use the flashlight to shine on “germs.”
            6. Rewash hands with soap.

            Background Essay

            Print

            “Cover your mouth!” “Wash your hands!” To young children, these may seem like the words of a nagging grown-up, but as adults, we know that those are two of the most important things you can do to keep yourself and those around you healthy.

            Every morning, children walk into classrooms carrying backpacks, lunches, and lots of germs. All it takes is for one child to cough or sneeze into her hand and touch door handles, tables, pencils, crayons, books, blocks, etc., and pretty soon you have a whole classroom full of sick children. Or worse, you have an empty classroom because everyone is home with the latest bug. Spreading germs that cause the flu, colds, stomach viruses, and other illnesses can easily be avoided by properly covering your mouth and washing your hands.

            The Global Public Private Partnership for Hand Washing indicates that the simple act of washing your hands can reduce your chances of getting sick by 50 percent. In a recent study, BioMed Central Pediatrics found that promoting hand washing in schools decreased the amount of illness related absences by 26 percent. This information about hand washing probably is not new to you. You are also probably aware that covering your mouth when you cough or sneeze significantly reduces the risk of infecting those around you. In fact, studies have shown that one uncovered sneeze has the potential to give up to 150 people a cold in just five minutes. Many people know the importance of these actions; however, many people are not aware that there is a correct and incorrect way to do them.

            In the original KET video, Healthy Habits, preschoolers are shown the correct way to cover their mouths and wash their hands so that they can provide a healthier environment for themselves and those around them.

            Discussion Questions

            Print
            1. Why is it important to cover your mouth when you cough or sneeze?
            2. What is the correct way to cover your mouth when you cough or sneeze? Why shouldn’t you use your hands?
            3. Why is it important to wash your hands?
            4. Should you only wash your hands when you can see the dirt on them? Why?
