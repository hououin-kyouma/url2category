  Five Ways to Keep a High GPA in College

Keeping a college GPA or grade point average high takes some concentration and effort, even for students who always did well in high school. The expectations are different for university students than for those in high school and university coeds who find ways to maintain their grade point average set themselves up for success in school.

Don't Suffer in Silence

Most professors offer students office hours. It's during these times that a student can connect one-on-one with the instructor and clarify concepts that still remain unclear. The professor can additionally give the student study tips that he tailors to the student's strengths. As well, because the instructor knows the subject matter better than the student, he can also help the student identify potential pitfalls long before the student realizes they are coming. This can stave off something that occurs far too often in university classes, especially at the freshman level; students often don't realize they are heading for trouble until it's too late.(ref1,3)

Determine How Much Each Class Requires

According to the Cornell University website, students should put in two hours of study time for each hour they attend class. However, some classes require even more study time than the normal average. Students who have classes they find particularly challenging should identify them at the outset of the semester and keep a log of how much time they spend doing homework, studying for tests and completing projects. After the first several weeks of school, they'll gain an understanding of what they need to do to keep their grades up in that class and make adjustments to their study schedule based on their findings. (ref1,2)

Attend All Your Classes

College life represents freedom from many of the restraints that students felt living at home and attending high school. That being the case, the lure of missing class can take hold. However, students who want a high GPA need to avoid this practice. Not only can they get behind very quickly, but as EduChoices.org points out, some teachers assign grades in part based on attendance and participation, so not going to class can knock critical points off a student's grade point average. (ref1,4)

Study Where It's Quiet

There seems to be a constant stream of stimuli when a person goes away to college, and that can seriously affect your ability to study in a negative way. One simple way to sidestep this issue is to spend time studying where it's quiet. Going to the library or even a coffee shop that's not busy can help students concentrate better and retain more information as they study. (ref3,4)

Take Good Notes

Being able to take good notes is a critical skill that most students must possess in order to achieve a high GPA. While there are those students who remember everything they hear, most of the time that counts as the exception rather than the rule. Much of what a student learns in college comes during the lectures; good notes allow the student to access this information later as he studies. (ref1,4)
