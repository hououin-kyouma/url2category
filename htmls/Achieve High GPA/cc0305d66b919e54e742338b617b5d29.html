Create A Daily Study Routine That Works For You In College

What is a study plan?

A study plan is an organized schedule which students create to outline study times and learning goals. College students should create a study schedule where they can dedicate specific days of the calendar to studying. Creating a study plan holds you accountable for your own learning outcomes. Online students need a study plan to succeed. This is because you need to have self-discipline and determination to complete your studies without the constant reminders of an instructor.

Why do I need a study plan?

A study plan is an effective way to help you navigate through your college education in an organized way. Every student develops a different kind of study plan. Do some self-evaluation on your current schedule and time management while creating your personalized study plan. Find days that you have less personal commitments and squeeze some study time. Always create a plan that works for you since each student studies differently. Some students find that studying nightly for thirty minutes is more realistic than studying a few times a week for a longer period.

Identify your learning goals for each study session in order to maximize these scheduled study times. Determine why you are studying, and develop a plan that can help you achieve those goals. Consider upcoming tests, your average in certain courses, and projects that you anticipate will take more time than others will.

Lastly, create a reasonable study plan. You need enough time to study but you also need to rest or set yourself up for failure. You can spend less time studying if you do it correctly.

Use these four steps to create your study plan

Step #1: Develop a time chart of your current activities. This will allow you to see how much time you spend from day to. Record things like when you are at work, school, or home with family for a week. Then look for extra hours to slip in some dedicated studying. This chart will help you determine days and times that are consistent to devote your studying.

Step #2: Develop a schedule – Make a mark in your planner or calendar. Use detailed notes to block out times on your calendar so that you can notice every time you look at it. Have a schedule written down so you do not forget. In addition, it is helpful to write down which subjects you plan to study so that you can be sure devote enough time to each of your classes.

 Step #3: Determine your study goals – determine why you need to study and what you plan to accomplish in each class at the beginning of each week. Do you want to raise your average in a particular class in order to maintain a certain GPA? Are you preparing for a big exam? For example, if you are studying for an important midterm exam, alter your study plan two weeks prior to the test to incorporate review of old tests and notes in your sessions. On the other hand, when you do not have an upcoming test, use your study time to read ahead one chapter in order to grasp the next lecture.

Step #4: Stick to your schedule – A study plan works best if followed on a consistent basis. Try developing a study plan that will work for you throughout the semester. Re-evaluate your study plan at the start of every semester since you will have different classes.

Lastly, some students study better with a partner. Studying with a classmate allows for collaboration and discussion. When creating your study plan, check with other students in your class to determine if you can coordinate study sessions. However, stick to an independent study plan if you tend to socialize more than study when you are around others. If you do choose to study with a partner, choose someone with whom you are likely to stay on task.
