Stoughton High valedictorian has a 4.88 GPA – and turned down Harvard

Wednesday
Jun 1, 2016 at 4:21 PM Jun 2, 2016 at 12:03 PM

The graduating senior challenged himself with honors and AP classes, while performing in two bands, playing two sports and participating in many clubs.

Cody Shepard The Enterprise @cshepard_ENT

STOUGHTON – Adam Potter’s high school record was more than perfect.

He took advanced classes, performed in two bands, played on two sports teams, participated in several clubs and was the senior class vice president.

Oh – and he finished with a 4.8859 grade point average.

The 18-year-old Stoughton High School valedictorian set his sights on being at the top of his class – and he accomplished the goal.

“I just took what my school would let me,” said Potter, who began Advanced Placement classes his sophomore year after honors his freshman year. “I felt like I wanted to challenge myself.”

Potter will graduate Thursday evening with an impeccable transcript – A's across the board. And it wasn’t that he pulled all-nighters or had no social life to get himself there.

Potter was in the marching and jazz bands, becoming the conductor of the marching band for his junior and senior years; he played on the ice hockey team in the winter and tennis in the fall; he participated in student council, Junior Statesmen of America and the National Honors Society; and he was the class officer and vice president of his class senior year.

“I feel like you have the time to get everything done, it’s just a matter of how effectively you use it,” Potter said. “If you get everything done when you have the time to, you’re not always pressed to get everything done late at night. I very rarely stayed up past midnight.”

With his higher-than-the-ceiling grade point average, Potter applied to several colleges, including Harvard University.

The Stoughton student was accepted, but decided to go elsewhere, committing to Georgetown University in Washington, D.C.

“When I was a freshman, the goal was getting into the best school – people think of it as the end-all be-all,” he said. “But the more you think about it, it’s less about a checkbox and more about the place you’ll be spending the next four years of your life.”

Besides, Potter said he didn’t like the feel of the Ivy League school as much and wanted to move a little further away from home.

And Georgetown caters to what he hopes to do in the future.

“The more you think about it, the more you realize you need to pick where you’re going to be happy,” Potter said. “I’m going into the School of Foreign Service. There’s so many like-minded people. For the next four years of my life, that’s the best option to explore what I want to do.”

 

Potter will walk across the stage Thursday evening knowing he has one of the highest GPAs in the region.

“I was a little scared going into high school because middle school guidance counselors would say, ‘If you take all honors, homework will dominate your life,’” he said. “It freaked me out a little bit at first. But you wouldn’t know until you actually try it. I was glad to push myself a little bit. And you get more out of it the more you put into it.”

Part of a series of stories profiling the region’s high school valedictorians.
