NORFOLK, Va. (WVEC) – Many parents and students are looking beyond high school to the time they or their children will be in college.

Admissions officials at Old Dominion University say getting accepted takes a lot of planning.

Besides getting good grades and test scores, they say many schools look at what students are involved in outside the classroom. They're looking for students with a variety of backgrounds and interests.

"We also look for students that might have been involved in their school or community and how those involvements might lead to leadership opportunities in other service," says Jane H. Dan'e, the associate vice president for Enrollment Management.

Back to School - 13NewsNow.com

She also says school look at how students approach their studies because it may signal their future success.

"I look for a student that has continually challenged themselves and has taken the more advanced courses. As a matter of fact, I'd rather see a student that earns a B in an advanced course than all As in courses that are less challenging for them," she adds.

Dan'e says the most desirable college applicants are well-rounded, with experience and expertise nurtured beyond classroom walls.
