How to Make a Studying Schedule (and actually stick to it)

Hello lovely!
All too often I see students who know they need to study for an exam, but who just don’t know where, when, or even how to start! This can be a really stressful and frustrating feeling! To help you get past this frustration, this post will teach you how to create a studying schedule and actually stick to it!

1. Fill in all tests and assignment due dates 

The first thing that you are going to need to do in order to create a study schedule is to know when all of your exams are and when your papers are due. This will let you know if you have more than one test in the same week and how much time you need to study for all of them. You can either do this in your planner or in a task scheduling app like Trello. Simply write the class title, exam date, and exam time.

2. Find out what will be on the test 

Once you know when all of your exams are, you will be able to have a head start on preparing for them. Usually, a week before the exam, the professor will mention that it’s coming up and give you a heads up on what information you should expect. Sometimes, they are even nice enough to make a study guide that you need to fill out. Be sure to listen in class for any clues about what might be on the test. If you have any questions, feel free to ask them in class or stop by the professor’s office during his office hours.

3. Choose your studying methods

Since each class, student, and professor is different, it is difficult to give a one size fits all study plan. Instead, here are a few things that you can do. Just pick a combination of these that you think would work best for you and the amount of time you have to study.

a. Read through and highlight notes

b. Fill out the study guide

c. Create notecards by hand or on Quizlet (here is a post on how I do this)

d. Study in a group

e. Work out problems

f. Memorize in small chunks

g. Test yourself

My personal favorite study plan is to fill out the study guide (if given), create notecards on Quizlet, memorize them in chunks of twenty-five, and test myself in a study group and/or using Quizlet’s features. I would recommend playing around with these studying methods until you find what works for you and your schedule!

4. Start early

Once you’ve chosen your preferred combination of studying methods, you will need to figure out when to start preparing. You can do this by estimating how difficult the test will be, looking at how much information you have to cover, and guessing how long it will take you to complete your specific studying tasks from step three. Here is an outline of my studying routine to give you a better idea of what I mean:

DAY 1 STUDYING CYCLE: FIRST POMODORO (TWENTY-FIVE MINUTES)

-Quickly read through notes and highlight most important information

Rest for five minutes

DAY 1 STUDYING CYCLE: SECOND POMODORO

-If desired, create notecards out of your highlighted information

Rest for five minutes

DAY 1 STUDYING CYCLE: THIRD POMODORO

-Continue making notecards if you are not finished

Rest for ten minutes

DAY 2 STUDYING CYCLE: MAY REQUIRE ALL THREE POMODOROS

-Memorize notecards in increments of twenty-five (this can also be spread out into twenty-five notecards a day if you start studying early enough)

Rest for five minutes between each twenty-five-minute cycle

DAY 3 STUDYING CYCLE: MAY REQUIRE ALL THREE POMODOROS

-You can use this cycle to read back through your notes or to test yourself using your notecards
Rest for five minutes between each twenty-five-minute cycle

5. Get to work

Now that you have your study plan thought out, it’s time to actually get started! You can add these to your to-do list or create cards for them on Trello! You can also grab our bonus worksheet to help you apply what you’ve learned in this post and create your study plan in one place!
I hope you enjoyed this post on how to create a study plan! I know that this can be a little overwhelming, but trust me when I say that having a plan first will help your studying go much smoother! Best of luck on those exams, my friend, and don’t forget to grab your study plan worksheet below!
