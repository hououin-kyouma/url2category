Cheerleaders rise to challenge of higher GPA, community service

  • BY TREVOR METCALFE
    tmetcalfe@registerbee.com
    (434) 791-7983
  • Oct 7, 2015
  • (…)

To be on the basketball cheerleading squad at George Washington High School, a student needs to maintain both a GPA of 2.0 and complete 40 hours of community service every year.

“Being in the city now for 10 years, I see that we have great athletes,” coach Trina McLaughlin said. “I have seen some that have gone off to college and could not compete academically, so I decided when I became the coach that I would raise the standards.”

However, McLaughlin — in her second year leading the squad — said her students are exceeding those expectations, boasting an average GPA of 3.5 and an average of 72 hours of community service.

McLaughlin said she wanted to see a 100 percent college entry rates for her cheerleaders. She changed the GPA requirement with approval from then-GWHS Principal Withers Jackson.

“So far, so good,” she said. “All of my folks have had a 2.0.”

The Virginia High School League says student athletes must pass five of the seven core classes to be eligible to play. McLaughlin said straight D’s for those classes would be a GPA of 1.0. However, she said many school districts had taken steps to raise their eligibility requirements.

Cheerleaders also must complete community service hours with the squad. McLaughlin said many girls want to pursue a Graduate of Distinction honor, which has community service requirements. She had some seniors had even completed more than 120 hours this year.

“The more we have given back, the more this community has come out and supported us,” she said.

Team captain and GWHS senior Le’Riece Miller said she was attempting the complete the Graduate of Distinction honor with her service.

“We do a lot of it,” she said.

She said one of her favorite projects was helping out younger residents of the Cardinal Village development with homework and games in the afternoon.

Freshman Faith Odom said the service aspect helped her practice her routines in public before having to perform at games.

“It’s a great experience for me,” she said.

Junior Nadia Ragin added that the experience let her go out in the community and learn and meet new people.

The squad will perform Oct. 18 as part of a Pop Warner cheerleading exhibition at the GWHS gymnasium.

Metcalfe reports for the Danville Register & Bee.
