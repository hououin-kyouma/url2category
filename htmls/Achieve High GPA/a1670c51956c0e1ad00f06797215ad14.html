SO the question is....is going to a prestigious school and getting a lower gpa better than going to a mediocre school and getting a high gpa in order to get to a good medical school?

    name brand is really not much of a help (store brand is really as good). And you run the serious risk of transferring then getting a downward trend in your GPA.....

    Go to the school that best fits your needs in terms of majors, costs, scholarship and how much you like being there. Dont go to a school solely based onthe incorrect perception that the name alone will improve your chances in getting into medical school. Four years from now, you may have a completely different view of what you want to do.

    While I am on the subject, dont major in biology under the perception that is the "correct" major for premed. Go to school, major in what you both like and what you find yourself doing in your life. Take the prereq courses as well but you can major in anything. Across all majors, GPA and MCAT scores are what matter
