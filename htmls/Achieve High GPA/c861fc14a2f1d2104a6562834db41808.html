FLORENCE, S.C. – West Florence High School freshman Karis Wymbs was thrust into the world of modeling when she was 10 years old after she entered a raffle at Magnolia Mall on a whim.

The winner of the raffle would receive an opportunity to strut the catwalk during a fashion show for MyStarKid, a talent casting agency for children.

“I wasn’t really expecting anything to happen,” Karis said. “Then they called my mom, and they said they wanted me to model for them on the runway.”

Karis’ impromptu raffle entry earned her a spot in the show, and since then she has modeled on runways in New York, Texas, Georgia and the Carolinas. She also belongs to three talents agencies, the first being DeAbreu Modeling and Talent Agency in Columbia.

Karis signed with the agency in 2015 where she was required not only to model but learn to act as well. After six months of training and a week at the New York Film Academy, Karis was cast to be an in a film shot in New Hampshire.

Karis said she was originally supposed to be an extra in the film until a featured role required a skill only Karis had.

“I was the only one that could cry on the spot, so I was able to have a speaking role in the film,” Karis said.

From there, Karis has performed in two music videos and was recently selected to perform as a supporting actress in a film titled “The Parent Class.” The movie will begin shooting in December in Philadelphia. With Karis’ acting and modeling career taking off at just 14 years old, her mother, Linda Parnell, admitted she had some concerns going into it.

“I was a little worried at the beginning,” Parnell said. “Everyone was saying she needs to be homeschooled, because all the castings take place Monday through Friday.”

Parnell said her daughter was insistent on pursuing her dreams of becoming a model and actress but even more insistent on attending public school and getting her education.

“She said she’d rather go to school and act less than to act all the time and be in the house and have no socialization,” Parnell said. “I like that.”

Karis and her family have worked together with her teachers and administrators so she can attend school at West Florence, maintain a good GPA and continue to audition for fashion shows and acting roles. Karis is permitted to miss 10 days of school each semester and has earned a straight-A average thus far.

“I thought it was going to be really hard, but the Florence School District really helped me,” Karis said. “All my teachers, they help me out where I can go places and they still catch me up on my work, and they email me my work so I won’t miss many days.”

Parnell and Karis’ father, Kelvin Wymbs, said their daughter has matured over the past four years since she discovered her modeling and acting talents. Wymbs said Karis has become more driven and motivated to achieve her goals.

“She’s really focusing on what she wants to do in life,” Wymbs said. “She’s empowering herself, and that’s always good to see.”

When Karis looks back at who she was as a 10-year-old girl spontaneously entering a raffle at the mall, she also has noticed drastic changes in herself and how her experiences have transformed her outlook on life.

“I have a bigger mindset,” Karis said. “Then 10-year-old me focused a lot on what other people said. But I see that now I really like myself. I don’t really worry about what other people say, because I know if I try, I will do better and I will succeed.”
