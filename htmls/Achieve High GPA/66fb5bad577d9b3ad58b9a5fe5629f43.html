What's a Grade Point Average (GPA)?
   
Advanced Search

Search Tips

QUT uses a seven-point scale where seven (7) is the highest grade awarded and one (1) is the lowest.

 

The Grade Point Average or GPA is calculated as follows:


GPA =

sum of (credit points of unit X numeric value of grade for that unit)

sum of credit points of unit(s)


The highest GPA you can achieve is 7 if you have HD (High Distinction) grades only. A GPA of 6 is for D (Distinction) grades, a GPA of 5 for C (Credit) grades etc. Most students have a combination of grades which results in a fractional GPA for example 5.83

 

For more information, including normal percentage ranges associated with grades, refer to QUT's grading system policy.

 

The 'Results' portlet located on the 'Study' page of HiQ displays available final grades for each of your unit enrolments in the current teaching period, as well as your GPA.
