UMass Amherst Welcomes High-Achieving First-Year Class as Students Return to Flagship Campus

August 29, 2016
Contact: Daniel J. Fitzgibbons 413/545-0444

AMHERST, Mass. – The University of Massachusetts Amherst will welcome another outstanding academically accomplished entering class when students return to the Commonwealth’s flagship campus over Labor Day weekend.

UMass Amherst Chancellor Kumble R. Subbaswamy says, “Once again our campus has attracted an outstanding incoming class of talented students from throughout Massachusetts, across the nation and around the world. The impressive academic credentials of the Class of 2020 exemplify UMass Amherst’s rising reputation as one of the nation’s leading public universities.”

The size of the entering class, approximately 4,650 to 4,700 students, is similar to last year. Despite a continued decline in the college-going demographic and an increasingly more competitive marketplace for those students, the academic profile of the entering class maintains a record of excellence and accomplishment.

Their average SAT score is 1225 and high school grade point average is 3.82. On average, students in the incoming class rank in the top fifth of their high school class. 

This year’s incoming class is more diverse. The ALANA (African-American, Latino, Asian/Pacific Islander and Native American) population will increase from 24 percent to approximately 29 percent and the underrepresented minority population will increase from 12 percent to 13 percent. Men represent 52 percent of the class and women 48 percent.

Massachusetts residents will again comprise nearly three-fourths of the first-year class. Domestic out-of-state students decreased by 129 students to 870 while the number of international students increased by 48 to 350. In-state enrollment increased by 70 students. Total undergraduate enrollment is estimated at 21,575, up from last year’s 21,308. In-state enrollment will be about 16,665 or 77 percent of the student body.

Commonwealth Honors College will welcome an entering class of 600. Their average SAT score is 1343 and their high school GPA is 4.27.

This is the 13th year of consecutive growth and another record for first-year applications for admission. About 41,000 students applied for admission, a 2 percent increase over the prior year, including larger numbers of out-of-state and international students. In-state applications have increased by half over the last 10 years while the number of high school graduates in the Northeast has been declining in recent years.

Although there was no change in the number of out-of-state applications, international student applications increased by 18 percent. Admission to UMass Amherst continues to be selective—only 59 percent of students who applied were admitted for the fall, substantially lower than the 71 percent acceptance rate in fall 2006. 

This is the 11th year that the campus offered an Early Action (EA) admission program whereby students apply early for admission and receive notification of their acceptance by mid-December. The number of students using the EA option has increased annually from about 3,000 applicants in 2006 to 16,400 this year. About two-fifths of students apply through EA, 71 percent were admitted, and more than 2,000 will enroll in the fall, representing 40 percent of the entering class.

Along with the first-year class, about 1,200 transfer students will be arriving on campus this fall, slightly more than last year. The transfers include 335 community college graduates enrolling through the MassTransfer program. MassTransfer guarantees admission to Massachusetts community college students who complete a designated program with a GPA of 2.5 or above. The overall GPA for the entering transfer students is 3.32.
