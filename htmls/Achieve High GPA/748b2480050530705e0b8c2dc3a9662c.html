10 Useful Websites To Help College Students Study More Efficiently And Be More Productive

Published April 27th, 2016 by SFEC Admin

SFEC Blog

Comments

As a college student, we don’t have to tell you how rigorous college homework assignments are because you already know that. But what if we could tell you which websites and tools could help you manage your workload, prep you for tests, improve research, help you with writing and save you a few bucks on books, would we have your attention? We thought so. In this blog you’ll find a list of 10 websites we think will help you work smarter and be more productive.

Websites to Help You Save

Half – College textbooks are so expensive. You may save a small fortune when you buy your books online at Half.com. Although the company is owned by eBay, the inventory is listed by private sellers. And just like eBay, the prices are all fixed. Once you find the item you like, at the price you want, simply pay for it and the seller ships it to you.

StudentRate – If you’re a college student living on a budget, you’re going to love StudentRate.com. This website aggregates student deals and discounts on items ranging from clothes to textbooks, to travel, to technology and a lot more.

Websites to Help You Study

Quizlet – This free study tool can be used on any device and makes learning new material, dare we say fun. Quizlet.com offers flashcards, quizzes and practice tests on just about any subject.

Koofers – This all-in-one website not only helps college students study by providing access to flash cards and practice exams, Koofer.com also gives you information on professor ratings, helps you generate the ideal class schedule, the site also posts job and internship openings.

Websites to Help You Conduct Research

Infoplease – This repository of information lets you search encyclopedias, atlases, thesauruses and other reference material. Infoplease.com is extremely useful.

Google Scholar – Find research articles relevant to any project or paper quickly and easily with Google Scholar.

A Research Guide – We know how frustrating it can be when each professor wants a paper written in a different format and that is why we think you're going to love Aresearchguide.com. The site collates some of the best resources for literary research and has the latest style guides so you can find the format your professor requires.

Websites to Help You Write Bibliographies

EasyBib – Actually, this little gem of a website helps you do more than generate citations correctly. Easybib.com has tools to help with research, title pages, and comes with an essay checker to catch writing mistakes.

Bibme - Whether your professor wants you to use APA, MLA, Chicago or any other citation format, Bibme.com helps you compile your sources in the proper style.

Zotero – Taking a different approach to creating bibliographies, Zotero.org is a simple plugin tool you install on your computer. Zotero works with your Internet browser to help you cite sources including PDFs, images, audio and video files, even snapshots of web pages. Once you’re ready, you can download all your sources and research pages into one nice little package.

It’s easy to get sidetracked while scouring the Internet to avoid working on homework or studying for that major exam. With these study hacks, you’ll be able to get your assignments done quickly giving you time to spend doing the fun things college life offers.
