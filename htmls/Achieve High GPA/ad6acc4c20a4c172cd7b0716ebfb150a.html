WHAT IS A CASE STUDY?
A case study is generally regarded as a very useful tool. This is because it is an intensive study of a specific aspect or matter with the purpose of providing understanding to other similar or larger aspects. Case studies regularly follow a process, which is the establishment of a problem or question to be answered, followed by investigation and analysis that will lead to a conclusion. The findings of this case study will most likely be of relevance and importance for both the one who carried out the investigation as well as others interested in similar matters.

THE IMPORTANCE OF MAINTAINING A GOOD GPA
FOLLOW UP STEPS
With our research, we aim to create a workshop regarding “the study habits of college students” to make awareness on the correct study skills a student must follow to succeed in college and maintain a good GPA.

In high school, many students fail to create a study habit and procrastinate, submitting their work late. Because of this, when they enter college, they maintain the same bad habits as when they were in high school.

This workshop will teach them good study habits to maintain a good GPA.

When job searching in the future, a company most likely hires a person with a good GPA as it demonstrates responsibility. We believe that we must think ahead of time and make awareness on the study habits that will guarantee us a good job.

- The BBA program in the future could value this interview a lot because it will help them determine how freshmen students think about the fact of maintaining a high GPA.

WHY DID WE CHOOSE THIS TOPIC?
When entering college one of the most important things is maintaining a high GPA. During the first semester students take the most basic and easy classes, making it easier to get good grades, but then classes will be getting harder and it will be harder to maintain a high GPA. We chose this topic because we wanted to see what other freshman thought about the importance of a high GPA during their first semester and ways in which one can maintain it. This investigation seeks to encourage students to take advantage of the first semester andgive their best in every class. Working hard and at the beginning of your career is something that in the future, you will be glad you did.

ARTICLE ABOUT THE TOPIC
“What Is a Good Freshman GPA?
By Brooke Bowers, eHow Contributor

In school, achieving good grades allows you to advance your education and opens the door to a variety of opportunities that can help to ensure a better future. Even as a freshman, whether in high school or college, it is important to keep your grades above a certain level in order to demonstrate your capabilities to school administrators, college admissions officers, graduate schools and even future employers.




College

While some students find it difficult to balance their school work and their social habits during the first year at college, your studies should come first. If possible, try to maintain a freshman GPA of at least 3.0 to 3.5. This will set you on a path to achieve a final grade point average of at least 3.5 by the end of your senior year of college.

Graduate Studies

Maintaining a strong grade point average while obtaining your master's degree or doctorate could mean the difference between graduating and being placed on academic probation. Graduate school advisors are stricter with--and more aware of--their charge's grade point averages than undergraduate counselors. So, keep in mind your school's policies for expulsion and minimum GPA requirements for graduation.

Career Opportunities

Besides academic advisers and admissions officers, your grade point average will also matter to future employers. According to the National Association of Colleges and Employers 2005 Job Outlook survey, 70% of hiring managers examine the grade point average of job applicants, and the majority of these employers use a 3.0 GPA as the cutoff. To make your job application more appealing, attempt to achieve at least a 3.0 in your freshman year and continue to improve your grades until you graduate.”

Source: Bowers, B. (2011, July 17). What Is a Good Freshman GPA? | eHow. eHow. Retrieved December 13, 2013, from http://www.ehow.com/info_8747792_good-freshman-gpa.html

CONCLUSION
- Most students agreed that working hard and maintaining a high GPA during the first semester is very important.
- Students said this will help them in the future, when you are taking harder classes and you have higher possibilities of not getting good grades.
- However, several students who did not agree with this fact. We found some believe it harder to maintain a high GPA at the beginning because of the transition between high school and college.
- Others believe a low GPA in the first semester does not mean it will be harder to improve it in the following semesters
- Overall, most students agreed a good GPA is from 3.5-4 and that maintaining it high would open up more opportunities when you graduate college.
- It was an interesting experience to see the different opinions from the students. We even found students that had not thought about how important it was to maintain a good GPA and who from now on will put in more effort due to all the benefits it brings.
