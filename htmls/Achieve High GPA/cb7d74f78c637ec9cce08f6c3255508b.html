Eight Tips for College Students to Develop a Good Study Habit

Published on: 2016-12-07 08:07:47

We all know that college requires a whole lot of studying. It can be very tricky to learn how to study effectively sometimes. However, with the right tips, you can maximize the result from the hours you put in your study. 

 

We are living in the age of information and it has made a lot of things easier. However, with websites like Facebook, Twitter, and Reddit taking their toll on our concentration, it can be harder than ever for one to stay focused. 

 

Good study habits are essential if you want to succeed in college. Whether you’re just beginning college or you’re looking for that crucial senior year productivity boost, these study tips for college students will help you max out your GPA and get on that honor roll.

 

  1. Good Notes Equals Good Grades

Good note and good study habits have an undeniable correlation. That being said, taking a good note is not everyone’s cup of tea. The trick is to record the key points of the lecture or textbook without writing down too much unnecessary information. You can always ask you teachers during the office hours, if you’re worried you’re missing important details. You can also record the lectures so that you can listen to them afterword in order to verify your notes. Also, make sure you take permissions from your professor first.

 

  1. Stay Organised

It’s really important for you to stay organised in order to develop a good study habit. Make a detailed calendar with all your commitments, including classwork, social events and extracurricular activities. This way, you can block out time each day to study.

Organizing your class materials is also one of the most important study tips for college students. Use sticky notes to remember important textbook pages, keep your returned assignments, and make flashcards for key terms. You’ll thank yourself come exam time!

 

 

  1. Unplug and Reconnect

It’s no secret that the internet is the worst distraction there is. So limiting time on it is one of the best study tips for college students. This can be a little tricky, since most students use their computers and also the internet for their home works. However, you can limit time on it by setting boundaries for yourself: for example, no Facebook during class or study time.

 

 

  1. Don’t Cram 

Pacing yourself is important for you to develop a good study habit. Try to study a little bit each day rather than saving it all for the week before the exam. This will help you ward off exhaustion and remember what you learned.

 

 

  1. Don’t Over-Study

Time management is the most essential study tips for the college students. Avoid spending time on useless information and make sure you’re focusing on the key ideas of each lecture or textbook chapter. 

 

 

  1. Don’t Categorize yourself

Students often categorize themselves as visual or auditory learners, or as being left-brained or right-brained thinkers. According to the New York Times, research has shown that these distinctions are largely erroneous. It is more important to figure out which study strategies work for you than to worry about where you lie.

 

 

  1. Find Your Zone

Not everyone prefers the same kind of environment to study. For some, it’s a quiet reading room, while others prefer the bustle of a coffee shop. Some like listening to music while they study, and some need complete silence.  You can try a few different kinds of environments and see what works for you.

 

 

  1. Take a Break!

Studying for 30-50 minutes, with a 10-minute break is recommended. Try to take a day or two off per week if you can. It’ll help you stay energized and at peak mental condition. It’ll help you stay energized and at peak mental condition.
