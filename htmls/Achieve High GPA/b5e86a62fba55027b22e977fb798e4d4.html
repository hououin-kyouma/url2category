My Morning Routine

  • breakfast
  • routine

Charlene Hong

November 1, 2013

No comments

I’m sorry, guys, but I have to confess something. It deeply troubles me to say, but Harvard has lied to you.

You know the new admissions video? You can see it on the homepage or on YouTube here. The video makes you fall in love with Harvard, for good reason. The amazing financial aid, the incredible professors, the professional opportunities, the potential to become Jeremy Lin (?!). It’s all here at Harvard where anything can happen (croons Ellie Goulding). All these wonderful reasons to fall in love with Harvard are true. 

However, there is one blatant lie that stands out from all the scenes in the video. As a recent Crimson article points out: “The video shows not one, but two scenes with people eating breakfast. “Breakfast? What’s that?” current Harvard students are no doubt wondering. “But you don’t even have class until, like, 10 a.m.,” prospective students are thinking, “surely you can squeeze breakfast in before then.” Wrong. The truth of the matter is that you will not eat breakfast if your first class is at 10 a.m. or 11 a.m. or even 4 p.m. You will not eat breakfast at Annenberg or the Houses (obviously, because they don’t even have hot breakfast). You will not eat breakfast here or there. You will not eat breakfast anywhere.”

Yes, dear reader. Harvard has deceived you about how often students eat breakfast here. To be completely honest, I remember being awake early enough to eat breakfast only twice since I arrived on campus for orientation week. 99.9% of my friends do not eat breakfast. However, this week, I was determined to change that.

I actually managed to wake myself up for breakfast at 9 am sharp every day. I have class at 10 am or 11:30 am on varying days of the week, so I gave myself about an hour every morning to get dressed and get myself down to Annenberg for delicious oatmeal and waffles.

I love my new morning routine.

Changing the way I do mornings has made me feel so much productive as a student. My typical Monday looked like this last week:

9:30 am: Wake up and get ready

10 am: Rush to Korean class, get there barely on time

11:45 am: Head to lunch

1-3pm: History and Literature seminar

3-5pm: Chill in my room. Surf the web. Literally do nothing.

6pm: Dinner

7pm: IOP Internships and Career Services committee meeting

8pm: Entryway study break!

11pm: Talk with roommates

12am-3am: Frantically work on my homework

3am: Sleep

However, since I’ve changed my morning routine, I feel energized, not only because I have hot food and coffee in my system, but I get to bed earlier because I start my work earlier in the day. Since I already feel like I have achieved so much just by being up early (ask my roommate; she’ll tell you what an accomplishment this is for me), I feel so productive and that motivates me to work hard for the rest of the day. Because I’ve been more motivated to get my work for the day done faster right after classes, I have more time at the end of the day to hang with friends and relax before I sleep. Now, my schedule looks like this:

9am: Get up and go to breakfast

10am: Casually walk into Korean class on time from a good breakfast in the Berg

11am-1pm: Eat lunch while re-reading my seminar work and starting other work

1-3pm: Seminar

3-5pm: Sit in Lamont Library and get work done

6pm: Dinner with friends

7pm: IOP meeting

8pm: Study break

8-11pm: Work

11pm-12am: Chill with roommates and other friends

1am: Sleep!

When you become a college student, you have 24 hours, and you have complete control of what activities to spend those hours on. Too often, especially in midterm season, we college students start to procrastinate and start becoming really unproductive. However, the energy my mornings have given me have allowed me to have a better distribution of my hours on work, play, sleep, and eating so that I maximize every day of my life here. I only have 4 years to live what I’m sure I’ll look back on as the greatest time of my life. I have to make the most of every day!

Now, if I can only get myself to go for a run every morning, too…
