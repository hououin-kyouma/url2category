Product description

From the Back Cover

Grow Your Business with the Right Connections

It's easy to feel like networking is a waste of time, energy, or money--but that just means you're doing it wrong. In this new edition of Networking Like a Pro, networking experts Dr. Ivan Misner and Brian Hilliard reveal key networking techniques to help you grow your business.

In this comprehensive guide, you'll discover strategies that go beyond collecting business cards and turn networking into a profitable resource for your business. Dive into this book and discover how the most successful networkers leverage their brand, expertise, and customers to achieve greatness in life.

You'll learn how to:
  • Attract the right people with a carefully crafted Unique Selling Proposition
  • Gain your most valuable customers with referrals from networking partners
  • Make your best first impression with the 12 x 12 x 12 Rule
  • Choose networking events and activities that best fit your needs
  • Build and expand your network with a calculated follow-up strategy
  • Avoid behaviors that damage your reputation and push potential partners away

Plus, gain access to worksheets, templates, and the Networking Scorecard designed to help you get the most out of your network. If you're ready to build connections that turn relationships into profitable customers, the Networking Like a Pro is for you! --This text refers to an alternate Paperback edition.

About the Author

Dr. Ivan Misner is the founder and chairman of BNI, the world’s largest business networking organization. He has written eleven books including three New York Times bestsellers.
Brian Hilliard specializes in helping busy entrepreneurs market their business in less than 90 days. He is the owner of Agito Consulting and a popular speaker.
David Alexander is the chief networking officer of Referrals4life, LLC., one of the largest BNI franchises in the world. David is also an author and keynote speaker.

4.0 out of 5 stars Content repeats like garlic bread on an empty stomach - but it's tasty.
ByDominic Mount on February 25, 2016
Format: Paperback
The content repeats itself more than Garlic Bread on an empty Stomach. Chapter after chapter we are treated to the same information we just read. It is just shameful that in the 21st century no contributions were made by women in this book. NOT ONE! One has to assume that women have already mastered the skill of networking and that this book is for men only. We must really need the help guys. The authors refer to themselves, their business organization and other books they have written with narcissistic self importance. Burp, More garlic Bread. It's like they have a school boy crush on themselves. Where to network is no surprise. Who to network to, comes as no surprise. 'How to network' is where this book gets interesting: Networking is a skill that takes time. It's about developing quality long-lasting relationships, not how many business cards you give away. Social Capital is expressed through caring, genuine interest in others and consistently helping others. If this book is on a coffee table, flip to pages 87 & 88: meaningful ways to read the room. Anything that advocates being honest and genuine is great. This book does that. Listen, garlic bread is delicious especially if it's cheesy... enjoy.
