For me, the text book definition is found in the textbook, The Go-Giver, by Bob Burg and John David Mann. Hint: it involves making introductions to and for others. It is NOT about you.

Everyone knows when it is done correctly but unsure until that happens individually, “up close and personal.” Most of us learned to do it wrong before a proper mentor appeared.

Burg & Mann can fill that role for anyone wondering how to network like a pro… or give me a shout. I love doing business with, “People that I know, like and trust.” Another great resource is Toastmasters International. They have clubs just about everywhere on the planet.

All bets are off until you actually try to network with real professionals in a business setting. So don’t just sit there — Jump Up and Reach Out!

Networking is a deliberate activity to build, reinforce and maintain relationships of trust with other people to further your goals. Professional networking is simply networking focused on professional goals.

Many people are confused because the term is used quite loosely to describe also aspects of the sales process like prospecting. 

People trust preferentially people they know already, either directly or through another trusted person. This means that gaining that trust with new people is much easier when you have a strong network.

A strong network is where you have connections in all of your areas of interest with people who know who you are and what you want.

In practical terms you build this network by getting to know more people, building trust with them and telling them what you are looking for.

What counts is not who you know, or even who knows you, but who knows you, knows you are trustworthy, knows what you do AND knows what you want.
