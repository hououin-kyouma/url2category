How to Build your Professional Network?

Author: Savarapu Praveena |
May
01
2017
Posted in Employer No comments

Spending too much time on surfing for jobs alone, is not going to fetch any desired results. Moreover, it might lead to losing your confidence and eventually your communication skills will subside which are very essential for you at every stage of the recruitment process.

Importance of Networking

Building a network is a strategy and should be the main component of your job search plan. Studies prove that networking is comparatively more effective than just applying to job posts. Networking can successfully build and cultivate relationships which is a vital part of your job search, also landing the ideal job.

It’s the people, whom you know, that gets you the job.

Where to start?

Making connections with a targeted set of people can result in acquiring new facts about a specific type of organization, work, or a job opportunity. These connections can take you further in your search and even in your career.

Things to do before you start networking

  • Recognize your skills and experience.
  • Begin by initiating conversations with your inner circle friends and move outwards stretching your connections widely.
  • In addition to your contact’s information, gather their relevant details such as the organizations they work in, their interests, professional associations, etc.
  • Be patient and a good listener. Good listeners succeed over good talkers.

Make your presence at every social or professional gathering, such as conferences, career fairs, alumni activities, professional association meetings, and fundraisers. Engage in volunteering at organizations of your interest.

How to initiate?

How to start a conversation? How to be liked by others? How to start those relationships that can be productive in your job search, if nourished and managed properly? The answers can be found below.

Networking Events

  • Attend most of the networking events, professional conferences and it’s better to be early to know the attendees.
  • Build a relationship with the organizer. He would obviously know the attendees and there lies a fair chance, of you meeting up with the important personnel.
  • Be creative, inventive, and initiate the conversation and be interested in what the other person has to say.

Social Media Networking

  • Make use of the social networking sites that help in improving your connections.
  • Create your own personal profile, about your core competencies, the organizations that interest you, the position you are seeking, and then make connections by searching for people in that field of interest, organization, position etc.

Propagate your network

  • Once you establish your connections, it’s time to propagate your contacts.
  • Reach out to your contacts, provide a brief description about your background and interests, attach your resume, and request some time by email, phone or in person to discuss regarding your job search.
  • Convey that you are looking for advice and not for a job, as they may route you to the HR department.
  • Offer a personalized thank you note after the meeting.

 

Conclusion

Networking is an ongoing activity. It can lead to making you more effective in your job and provide you with additional employment opportunities and connect you to professional associates, partners and resources.

By offering assistance to the people in your network, you can build strong and nourishing connections that pave your way into your ideal position sooner. Networking is all about encouraging goodwill and sharing knowledge, be ready to reciprocate in any way possible.
