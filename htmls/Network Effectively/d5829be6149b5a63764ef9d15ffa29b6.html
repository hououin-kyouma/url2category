Five Strategies for Networking

by Shelley Frost

  • 2 [Communication Networking] | Effective Communication Networking
  • 3 [Proactive] | How to Be Proactive in the Workplace

Networking aids in securing better career opportunities and finding new clients. No matter what your career goals, effective networking is necessary on a regular basis. A proactive approach to networking helps you meet more business contacts. Your networking activities should also include efforts to maintain regular contact with your new business associates to make them more likely to help you.

Attend Business Functions

While networking is possible anywhere, you increase your chances of meeting contacts who are able to help you in business environments. If you're networking for job opportunities, look for job fairs. Business organizations within the community work for both college grads and those who are already working in the industry. Look for learning opportunities in the industry, such as a lecture or book-signing in the field.

Be Approachable

Simply attending business functions isn't enough to effectively network. If you are naturally outgoing, you likely won't have difficulty introducing yourself and meeting new contacts. If you are more reserved, break out of your shell and learn to approach potential business contacts. Even outside of business functions, a social attitude leads to additional contacts. Strike up conversations at your child's school, with your insurance agent or while you fill up your gas tank.

Go Online

In addition to in-person contacts, going online enables you to further expand your business contacts. Start with your personal contacts on social networking sites. Joining industry-specific message boards and online networking sites enables you to make new contacts beyond your acquaintances. Often you are able to naturally expand your contact list through online channels by connecting with acquaintances of acquaintances.

Add Value

When you meet new business contacts through your networking, make yourself valuable to the other person. While you're thinking about how the person is able to help your efforts, you also need to show her you are able to help her. For example, you might volunteer your time for an organization after connecting with its founder or volunteer to help run a business convention in your city. In the course of making yourself valuable, you are able to showcase your skills.

Maintain Contact

Once you meet a new business contact, keep in touch. The more natural business contact you have with the person, the more he gets to know you and your working abilities. He is more likely to think of you if a career opportunity opens up or if another acquaintance needs a referral for someone in your industry. A stronger connection with your contacts also gives you the opportunity to meet new contacts through him. If you regularly attend the same business events, you may catch him when he is with another associate. The two of you are introduced and you expand your business network even more.
