How To: Network Like A Pro

SHARE ARTICLE:

Networking is often named as the best way to land a job in the industry. So many music industry professionals have heard about upcoming opportunities through speaking to other professionals or have been introduced to a potential new employer through a friend.

To help you get a handle on this networking thing, we spoke to Sam Potts, co-founder of the Young Guns Network. The Young Guns Network (or YGN for short) is “a professional networking group for young media professional, aged 18 – 30” and have held events at Sony Music, Universal Music UK, PRS for Music, MMF, Spotify, Shazam, Somethin’ Else and BBC Introducing providing opportunities for young people to network, along with industry guest speakers.

The Young Gun’s Network came about when Sam was discussing the lack of opportunities for young media professionals to meet up and exchange ideas and experiences with one another, with his partner, Remi, and they decided to do something about it! YGN has been hugely successful and created job opportunities for its attendees, funding for entrepreneurial projects and led to the creation of a brand new event, Buzz Jam.

Here’s some top advice from Sam on how to network like a pro.

What are your top three tips for networking?

1) “Conversation should be two ways. You should maintain a natural conversation by asking and answering an equal amount of questions.”

2) “Make eye contact and don’t be looking over someone’s shoulder trying to spot the next person to talk to.“

3) “Make sure you do your research before hand. Try to learn a little bit about the people who will be at your next networking event and look for some possible conversation topics.”

Networking can happen naturally once you’ve established some connections within your desired industry, but ‘getting your foot in the door’ can be difficult. How do you think someone could make initial contact with industry professionals?

“Firstly, I would recommend attending the next YGN event. Apart from that, I’d suggest researching the companies you are interested in and seeing if you can find a contact online. Alternatively, I would see what connections I can make through family or friends, even if it’s through a friend of your mum’s friend, get in contact. The worst that can happen is they’ll ignore you but they could also be a valuable contact.”

Which do you think is most effective: social networking or face-to-face networking?

“I think nothing can replace face-to-face networking. While it can be useful to initiate the meeting online, the connection when meeting face-to-face is far stronger. I also think that emails can be far more effective than a Twitter DM or Facebook messenger. When you email someone, they are in work mode and you are establishing that relationship in a professional setting and creating a professional relationship.”

A lot of music networking opportunities are based in/around London, what advice would you give someone living outside London hoping to make it in the industry?

“I would recommend looking for groups of similar professionals online within your industry or researching companies nearby within your desired field. However, if you notice that there is a lack of something like that in your area, you should look into organising it yourself.”

For more information on YGN, check out their website and keep up to date by following them out on Twitter. Also, if you are an artist or manager, aged 18-30, check out their exciting new event Buzz Jam, which combines coding and creativity. You can find Buzz Jam on Twitter here.
