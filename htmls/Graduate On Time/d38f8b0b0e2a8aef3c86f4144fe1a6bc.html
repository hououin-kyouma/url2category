Graduation Rates of Texas Colleges

April 12, 2015 By Jolyn Brand

Texas College Graduation Rates Statistics

Texas Statistics Public Colleges Private Colleges For Profit Colleges
% of students who graduate in 4 years 24.4% 41.5% 9.9%
% of students who graduate in 6 years 49% 57.8% 18.2%
Texas’ place compared to all other states 32nd 29th 38th

 

The following charts are separated into 3 tables: Public 4 year colleges, Private 4 year colleges, and For Profit colleges.

All are sorted with the highest 6 year Gradation rate at the top of each table. The first column is the rate at which students graduate at  each college within 6 years. The second column is the graduation rate within 4 years. Parents are often shocked to learn that less than half of college students will graduate within 4 years, which is why it’s essential to not only select a good fit college, but also to adequately prepare for college with Pre-AP and AP coursework. Read more about how classes and course selection affect SAT scores here.)

The third column is the average amount of student aid at each college, this number is affected by the percentage of students with Pell grants (federal financial aid for low-income students) but also by scholarships and merit aid.

Client Testimonials

My daughter attends high school in Clear Lake (CCISD) and is a very bright student. She became a teenage mom during her senior year and lost all hope of going to college. She was a great mom but to encourage her to pursue her college options, the counselor recommended we contact Brand College Consulting. Jolyn met with us and agreed to take us on pro bono, she worked with us for free!  And since she was a teenage mom too, my daughter trusted her and her advice.

Over the school year, Jolyn encouraged her to apply to state and private colleges and walked us through the financial aid and scholarship requirements. My daughter thought she would have to attend a community college and work part-time, because couldn't afford full time tuition, plus daycare. They worked online and through Skype which was very convenient.

My daughter was accepted to several colleges, but one of them granted her a full ride. We just found out that she also got a scholarship that will cover her books and the daycare for her son. My girl is going to college! And I can't thank Jolyn enough!

Jennifer
Class of 2016 parent, Clear Lake
