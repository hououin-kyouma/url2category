How to Graduate from College in Less Than Four Years

Explore higher education options that let you start your career faster.

Print

Graduating from college is a big accomplishment. It takes a lot of long hours and hard work to complete all those classes. Despite the commitment involved, there are more and more students completing their degrees every year. What’s even more amazing is that they can do it in fewer years than it used to take. Now, with some careful planning, creative thinking and perseverance, a dedicated student can finish a bachelors degree in three years instead of the typical four and start their career that much more quickly.

Benefits of Junior College

Starting your college career by taking classes at a junior college can save you both time and money, as you can study close to your home and take summer classes to complete your degree more quickly. Then you can transfer to a 4-year college or university to complete your bachelors degree. Talk to the your college counselor to see how they transfer the junior college credits and start planning out your schedule. With the increased flexibility and cost savings provided by a junior savings, you can get your introductory-level credits out of the way in areas like foreign languages, basic math and sciences classes, lower-level English and history classes and basic social science classes, like psychology and anthropology.

Online Learning Programs

Online learning programs offer another affordable, convenient way to get your degree quickly. So many 4-year colleges and universities are seeing the value of online learning, and many have started offering entire programs using this teaching method. You can get the same great education as well as greater scheduling flexibility than you would with regular classroom training.

With online learning, you have the freedom to take classes when you want to, at your pace, on your timeframe. In most cases, you don’t attend formal scheduled classes; instead, you learn through reading and attending online chat sessions with your fellow students. Online courses focus on independent study, group projects and using technology to enhance the learning experience.

Some students take their entire college degree online, while others mesh online learning with classroom learning and take more classes per semester than you could otherwise. Concerned about the cost of doubling up? Don’t be. It’s usually much less expensive to take the same class in its online version than its classroom version, and you get the same quality of education.

Finding Schools

When you’re ready to tackle the quest for colleges, the Internet can help you find the right junior college or online degree program that will have the great basic courses to start you on your journey. It can also help you find that perfect bachelor's program that will land you your dream job when you graduate.
