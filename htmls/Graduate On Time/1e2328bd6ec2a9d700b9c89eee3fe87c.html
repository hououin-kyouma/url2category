I earned my Doctorate in Educationa l Leadership and Management from Capella in March, 2017. Overall it was a good experience despite the many changes within th ... e School of Education. I took me a little over 4 years to finish. Having earned both a Bachelor and two Masters' from the number one university in my state, I found Capella as or more challenging. Prior to enrolling at Capella, I read all of the negative reviews but I went in knowing that the school is a "For Profit" business therefore it would be up to me to ensure that I was fully committed to obtaining the degree. Early on, I found that many of my classmates would not be around for the completion of the program nor earn the Doctorate, the level of their commitment, not to mention the quality and substance of their work submitted for assignments were very substandard for someone pursuing a PhD or other Professional Doctorate. Of course I had my issues with the school dragging out approvals, changing forms and formats, milking financial aid changing professors but I expected that going in. I have friends and coworkers who are attending other universities and have experienced the same. One must remember Doctoral Degrees are earned and not given and I don't feel Capella differs from other universities in that aspect. Basically, you have to put your life on hold for at least the duration of the dissertation because the dissertation becomes your life. I am a proud Capella graduate along with the other 250+ PhD and EdD graduates that participated in the graduation ceremony in Atlanta in March. My Doctorate has yielded me a substantial pay raise on my job therefore, I'm well pleased and I will say I worked extremely hard for my degree, money well spent.
