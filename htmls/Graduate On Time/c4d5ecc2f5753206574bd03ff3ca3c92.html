Graduating Early: Why It’s Fun And Scary All At Once

March 14, 2016 by Anna Flahaven
0

What if you had the option of graduating school early would you do it? Well, I actually did. And as much as it pains me to say it I have mixed feelings about graduating college early.

During senior year of high school, students had the option of taking college classes as a part of high school instead of taking the typical AP classes. As a clever high schooler I thought this was fantastic opportunity. I could essentially take care of all my Gen-Eds (still silly we have to take these to get a degree) while in high school. I easily saved $18,000 which was just one year of school for me. After graduating high school I felt great; I had “one” year of college under my belt and had just saved all this money by knocking out these non-essential classes.

So, going into my freshman year of college I was considered a sophomore and was eligible to take classes for major if I decided to.

I declared a major in Marketing (thanks Mad Men for the influence) and would hopefully have 3 years to finish all the classes I needed to. Even though I knew I would be graduating early I had one worrying thought pretty much 24/7.

I am nervous of graduating and being on my own.

I have exactly 1 year of classes left in college. Woah. Talk about a wake-up call.

Next year I’ll be graduating and hopefully starting a job and moving to a new city to start my life. But I feel like I just arrived at college. I just made friends, figured out what profession I wanted to do and college decided it is ready to send me into the adult world? What if I want to take more classes? What if not quite ready to start my life at the ripe ole age of 20?

When I think of life I think of stepping stones everyone has to do. First, you get good grades in high school. Second, you graduate. Third, you repeat the same steps but in college. Fourth, you get a job after college that is secure enough that it will allow you to buy nice things and buy food that’s good for you instead of eating ramen again for the third time that week.

But, I feel the most vital step (being the fifth one) is learning to be independent.

Once college is done I feel like I will be completely on my own.

Of course I don’t mean like “Home Alone” where everyone leaves me but, I’ll have to start doing things on my own. I have to learn to be independent which scares me a lot. The thought of having to do so many common things on my own is a process that sometimes I wish I didn’t have to do and I am sure many people feel the same way. When I graduate I will have had 20 years of experience of relying on teachers, parents, and friends to help me deal with things. Of course my parents and friends will still be around but they also will have to learn to be independent on their own and I can’t always rely on them.

Once I graduate I feel like the alarm for life to begin will be going off and I don’t know if I should hit the snooze button again or get up and begin my new day. Add on top of that having to do all this earlier than most people and it seems like it is one extra-large anxiety cocktail.

However, even with all this nervousness I have, the thrill of starting my own life is out weighing all the cons of graduating early.

I have found college is a nice little transition period before becoming an adult. College is supposed to be when you find yourself and find things you enjoy doing and might want to do for the rest of your life. College is prepping you for the future no matter how long it may take you to complete it.

Don’t get me wrong I love college. But sometimes it tends to feel like high school all over again. I’m ready to be independent and live on my own. I want to escape the chaos of having to turn 14 page papers in on time or having to cram for a history exam.

I feel the sooner I can graduate college the quicker I can transition into “adulting” and beginning my life. I can find that cute apartment in Chicago, have a job I enjoy doing, and live that life I have been wanting to discover for so long. Of course, I’ll have to be doing some of this on my own but I feel like once I have done little things independently then the big steps in life will be much easier.

Yes, college may be ending sooner for me than most people but even in the 3 years I will have spent in college I feel I have matured, found myself, and found people I can be friends with (hopefully) for many years. If college was meant to last for majority of your life it would be much longer than 4 years. Everyone has to graduate college at some point whether it takes them 3 years or 7 years. I feel the sooner I graduate the easier I can transition into the life I wanna live.

Graduating early is going to be scary. I am going to have to adjust to a new life, new friends, and new experiences all in a short amount of time especially when I feel like I just got to college. I’ll also have to learn what being independent feels like a little bit sooner than some people. But college is not meant to last forever.

There is so much more in the world I am ready to discover. So after this year goes by and I graduate college I will be ready for ya world. Bring it on.
