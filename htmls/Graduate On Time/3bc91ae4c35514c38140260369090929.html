Graduation

Graduation

All students enrolled at UC Berkeley are assigned an Expected Graduation Term (EGT). For students admitted as freshmen, the EGT is set at eight semesters. For students admitted as Junior Transfers, this is set at four semesters of enrollment at UC Berkeley.  Summer enrollment – at UC Berkeley or elsewhere – is optional, and is not factored into the assignment of EGT.

Before you enroll in classes for your EGT semester, it is essential that you check to make sure that classes you have chosen will fulfill any remaining degree requirements.

  • In October 2017, a new degree audit tool will debut.  Stay alert for details and a new link for the “Academic Progress Report” on your CalCentral pages.
  • The Academic Progress Report will note all undergraduate degree requirements, including UC, Berkeley campus, college, and major requirements.
  • Although major requirements are reported upon by the APR for majors in L&S, it is advisable to check with your undergraduate major adviser (UMA) the semester before you graduate, to be sure that your major will be complete by the end of your last semester.
  • If you are uncertain about any of your requirements as you enter your final year, make an appointment with an L&S College Adviser before finalizing your planned schedule. Please note that scheduling an advising appointment in L&S requires several weeks of advance planning.  

Requesting a Graduation Term (formerly “Adding to the Degree List”) for Fall 2017

Undergraduates registered at UC Berkeley for Fall 2017, with eight or more terms completed (four or more terms for junior transfer students), were sent a notification in CalCentral stating, "Fall 2017 is your Final Term". This note does not mean that your current enrollments have been verified as satisfying your remaining degree requirements, only that you are to be reviewed for completion of all requirements by the last day of Finals for Fall 2017.  (Review Academic Calendars)

If you are not currently registered for Fall 2017 at UC Berkeley (i.e., enrolled at any other institution, including UC Extension, completing Incomplete course work, etc.) and plan to finish your requirements during this semester, then complete a Request for Graduation Term (https://ls.berkeley.edu/sites/default/files/advrequestforgraduationterm.pdf) fillable form and follow instructions on the form.

Important Note: the deadline to Request Graduation in Fall 2017 is October 13, 2017.  No late appeals will be considered.

Degree Completion while Enrolled Elsewhere

If you are enrolled in final course work at another institution during your last semester, there are several steps you must pursue.  In order to be eligible to earn a diploma from the College of Letters & Science at UC Berkeley, students must:

  • pay all fees,
  • clear any Student Conduct hold, and
  • document completion of all requirements.

Students enrolled in California Community College courses should consult Assist.org for “General Education/Breadth” or “Information – American Cultures Graduation Requirement” options.  Be sure to use the pull down menus for the specific requirement lacking, and for the academic year the requirement is planned (or was completed). Remember, community colleges generally do not offer Upper Division course work; students lacking Upper Division work must find a way to enroll in an accredited four-year college or university, and likely will need departmental approval for such courses to be completed off campus.

Upon completion of the course/s needed, you must order an official transcript from the school you attended (once you verify relevant grades are posted). Be sure transcripts are sent to UC Berkeley in one of the two following manners:

If the school where you completed your last requirement offers electronic transcripts, then choose that option!  This method is faster, cheaper, and more reliable than paper transcripts.  Arrange that the other school/college send e-transcripts by deadline (see below) to our Business Operations office: busops@berkeley.edu

If the school you attend for your last requirement offers only paper-copy transcripts as official, have it sent by the deadline (read below) in a sealed official envelope to:

Add Credit Degree Candidate: UCB SID# (insert your SID)

16 Sproul Hall, MC 0608

Berkeley, CA 94720-0608

Transcript Deadlines

Fall Degree Completion: January 31

Spring Degree Completion: June 30

Summer Degree Completion: September 7

Note: Course work taken at other institutions must officially end by the following dates in order to be included in a degree for that semester. If your off campus course work ends later than the dates indicated below, you will not be permitted to graduate in that term, and will need to request graduation for the following term.

Fall Degree Completion:  December 31

Early Proof of Graduation/Degree Completion

If a prospective employer or graduate school requires an official statement that you have completed your degree requirements, you may petition for either a Certificate of Completion, or Expedited Degree Check Out at Cal Student Central.  Each of these requests requires several weeks to complete and can only be requested after grades are all posted from the graduating term.  This is especially difficult if you were enrolled elsewhere that term; please plan well in advance in order to support your future goals.

 

Proof of Intent to Graduate

If you are in your Expected Graduation term and your scholarship or military entity requires proof you will complete a degree with “in progress” coursework, you must document that requirement to a College Adviser (in both Colleges, if you are a Simultaneous Degree student).  You must:

  • be fully enrolled in final requirements (wait list unacceptable),
  • agree to make no changes to such enrollments,
  • obtain written statement regarding major completion from your Major Advisor/s, and
  • provide College Adviser with exact mailing address and language needed.

A College Adviser in the L&S Office of Undergraduate Advising can provide a letter of Intent to Graduate only in a scheduled advising appointment.  Be sure to plan several weeks ahead of the required letter’s due date, and make an advising appointment when you meet all conditions noted here.

 

Certificate of Completion/Early Degree Posting defined

If you have finished all your degree requirements and need proof of graduation in the period of time after grades are posted, you must request a Certificate of Completion or Expedited Degree Check Out.  Please note: degree processing requires several months; do not request early certification if you are simply wondering about your status.

The Certificate of Completion is an official document issued by the Office of the Registrar verifying that all degree requirements have been met. This document requires approximately 15 working days to create, once all grades for the term are available.

Expedited Degree Check Out is only offered in cases where students have met the following criteria, AND a Certificate of Completion is not accepted by the outside entity (Teach For America and early-start graduate programs are examples).  Criteria for Expedited Degree Check Out are:

  • all fees are paid,
  • any Student Conduct hold is cleared, and
  • documents regarding completion of all requirements are received and posted.

Commencement Ceremonies

Official degree completion, also called graduation, is not the same as participation in commencement ceremonies.

It is not necessary to be in your EGT in order to participate in commencement ceremonies, which are conducted in May and December. In many cases, major departments allow students with some requirements outstanding to participate in commencement exercises. If you have not finished all of your requirements but wish to participate in a ceremony, you must check with your undergraduate major adviser 2-3 months in advance about eligibility, deadlines and tickets.

 

Transcripts and Diplomas

An official transcript with your degree noted is normally available 10-12 weeks after posting of final grades.  Diplomas are mailed to all graduates about four months after the end of your graduating term; US addresses and international addresses are welcome, but delivery times will differ.  Please verify your diploma mailing address is accurate by logging on to CalCentral and updating your Profile (under Community) or through the Office of the Registrar.
