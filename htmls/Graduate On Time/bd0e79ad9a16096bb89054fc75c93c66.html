More Indiana high schoolers graduating early

By Kate Chappell | 
Posted: Tue 7:22 AM, Jun 02, 2015

MISHAWAKA Graduation season is in full swing, but more high school Juniors will be collecting their diplomas this year alongside their Senior classmates. A financial incentive, passed with the Indiana state budget in 2011, is encouraging more young students to start college early.

Indiana students who complete their Core 40 requirements by July 1 of junior year are eligible for the Mitch Daniels Early Graduation Scholarship. It's $4,000 for students who apply to an eligible college within five months of graduation.

Early graduation is a growing trend at schools like Penn, where the number of students graduating early has grown from 25 to 70 in the last 3 years.

"It's a popular thing I think, first of all, because high school isn't for everybody," said Don Cronk, the Director of Counseling at Penn High School. "This has opened the avenues to a greater population of students."

The reasons to graduate early vary. For some students it's getting a jump start on college; while for others, it's to work and save money before college.

"I didn't play any sports this year because I knew I had to make extra time, so I already prepared myself," said Junior, Manpreet Kaur. "I had to take a lot of online classes. It was difficult because I had homework and that, but I had time for it, and I was ready for it," said Kaur who is graduating this year.

Like Kaur, many students choose to take online summer classes to get ahead on credits. Others take night school during the year at Pennway Alternative School.

"It's an opportunity for these students to work at their own pace and earn credits at their own pace, which allows them to graduate early a lot of the time," said David Falkenau, the director at Pennway.

Statewide the number of scholarships awarded increased from 170 students during the 2012-13 year to 265 in 2013-14.
