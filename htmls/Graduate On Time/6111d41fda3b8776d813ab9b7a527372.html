42 Pieces Of Advice For Seniors Graduating High School Heading Into Freshman Year Of College

Delaney Burgess
| May 1, 2017
2116
0
143
SHARES
SHARE TWEET

High school seniors, this one is for you.

Everyone says it, but wow, it truly does feel like just yesterday I was walking on stage in my blue cap and gown, shaking my principal’s hand, and receiving that diploma. I remember the innocently beautiful confusion I felt as I had no idea what was to come or what I was going to make of my life. Let me tell you, no matter how old you are, no one has it figured out. We are all confused beings traveling the peaks and the valleys that our journey called life brings our way.

To the seniors about to graduate in just a few weeks, I want to give you a few pieces of advice as you end your high school experience and head to college. Before you know it, you’ll be a senior in college wondering how the heck it all happened so fast.

1. Your life is really just beginning, and the best days are to come.

2. You don’t need to know your major or career goals right now so don’t let anyone tell you that you should have it figured out.

3. Spend as much time as you can with your high school friends because before you know it you’ll all be spread out across the nation.

4. But also, don’t forget to spend time with your family because whether you believe it or not, you will miss them.

5. It doesn’t matter how cool you were in high school, college is completely different.

6. STOP comparing yourself to others. Seriously, stop.

7. Don’t be nervous or discouraged if you don’t really know your roommate yet. I didn’t know mine at the time but today I can say with 100% confidence she will be one of my bridesmaids.

8. You don’t need to bring your entire closet to school with you.

9. I get it, graduation is bittersweet and some of you might be sad about it but please be more excited than sad. A few years from now you’ll be graduating college and let me tell you THAT is what sadness feels like.

10. College will be fun but it’s not all about the party.

11. You’re about to meet your bridesmaids. Let that sink in.

12. The guy that you can’t get over won’t matter soon enough. Trust me on this one.

13. You might meet your husband in college but probably not so don’t get your hopes up.

14. If you’re going to drink, be smart about it. And if you’re not going to drink…drink. Obviously, I’m not encouraging alcoholism but I am encouraging the social life because you never know who you’ll meet and the memories you’ll make.

15. Think about your biggest problem currently. Now, let me tell you that “big problem” is actually super small.

16. When you get to school involve yourself but also don’t put too much on your plate at once.

17. The people who were mean to you high school will be most likely be the lowlifes of society in a short time, so don’t waste your time on them.

18. Go on spring break freshman year. Seriously. You’ll learn so much and you’ll have a good time doing it.

19. Get to know people who live on your floor at the dorms, you’ll be with them for a whole year.

20. Who cares about calories, eat the dang cheesy bread.

21. Get off your phone and experience the moment.

22. Who cares if he opens your Snapchat and doesn’t reply? Seriously. I mean IF he did reply and you thought that was charming then maybe it’s time to reevaluate your standards.

23. You will never, I repeat, NEVER, please everyone.

24. You could be the juiciest peach on the tree and someone is still going to claim to hate peaches.

25. Every person you are about to meet has a story. Take time to learn it and don’t judge from the getgo.

26. Go Greek. I never considered myself a “sorority girl” because I thought that meant being the stereotypical girl I had seen in the movies. I was wrong, it’s nothing like that. Being in a sorority has expanded my network, encouraged me to keep up with my grades, given me lifelong friends, and inspired me to always be the best person I can be.

27. Study, study, study. But also, don’t stay in on a night that you could’ve gone out.

28. You will never be a freshman again so embrace the confusion, chaos, and learning experiences that come with this chapter.

29. Everyone is looking for friends, don’t be afraid to be the first person to reach out.

30. Never text and drive. Never drink and drive.

31. WRITE. Once a week write about your life or anything that’s on your mind. I wrote a letter to my older self from my freshman year self and it’s been super cool to see how things change overtime.

32. Before you graduate high school, make sure coaches, teachers, students who’ve inspired or influenced you throughout your life know about it.

33. Your independence will truly be tested in the coming year whether you’re ready for it or not.

34. You might miss home but stay at school as many weekends as you can.

35. Utilize the school’s gym.

36. Call home at least once a week.

37. It’ll be easy to get caught up in the bubble that comes with being on campus but don’t forget to step outside of that bubble from time to time.

38. You might not meet your best friends your first year, just hold on tight they’re coming.

39. Stay out late. You won’t remember the nights that you got plenty of sleep.

40. Work hard but don’t forget to stop and smell the roses.

41. Enjoy ever second, before you know it you’ll be off into the “real world” and trust me it’s a scary reality.

42. Always remember, there’s a last time for everything.
