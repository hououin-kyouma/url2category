Points/Tips for college graduates and fresher engineers

It’s been almost 8 years I am into the Software solutions/development field and during the span of this period, I have been responsible as a Technical Lead or Head of the department for last 2 years. Being a Technical Lead/HOD, I am responsible for technical aspect of the android and mobile team, also about team structure and team management which includes hiring and firing candidates (We haven’t fired anyone yet!)

During the span of last 2 years, I have interviewed almost 100+ candidates. Also being an Organiser of Google Developers Group, Ahmedabad, I have interacted with thousands of students and experienced engineers. And here I want to share my experience of conducting interviews, with considering that it would be helpful to college graduates and fresher engineers.

I would divide points into 2 categories:

  1. Learn and Explore as much as you can
  2. Put your learning into the execution

Category 1: Learn and Explore as much as you can

1. Clear concepts and fundamentals

First and very much important is point that you should focus more on learning and mastering concepts and fundamentals​ while pursuing graduation. Fundamentals​ include basics of programming, OOPs concepts, database concepts (includes query, having clause, group by, order by, etc), data structure and algorithms.

Very first question I ask is “Difference between Abstract class and Interface”? “Give me examples of it’s usage in Android?” and I have found 3 candidates out of 10 are not answering. Hope you have got my point of learning and mastering concepts very well!

More you are clear with programming and database concepts, your chances of getting selected in any jobs would be high!

2. Good command over communication

This is another important point while applying for job, If I look at Ahmedabad (or even India) based companies and startups, they have started focusing more on communication as well along with programming.

While pursuing your graduation and when you are freshers (by any chance you are selected and doing job since 1–2–3 years) or experienced, you should start focusing more on getting your communication improved!

There are thousands of articles/videos and other resources are available over web and so I will not write here! but you can definitely start improving english by writing blogs,

3. Attend events/seminars/meetups

There are many benefits of attending events and meet-ups happening around you.

Very first benefit of attending events is you are learning by someone’s experience and so a chance to learn many tips/tricks and advanced things which you might not know or even might not learn during your graduation or during your initial jobs.

Second benefit is, you are making contacts and building your network for the future. You never know when a contact made during events would be helpful you in future! also you never know when someone (may be CEO, CTO or HR) from events comes and found you fit for their openings!

Third, currently you are attending events and if you are passionate of sharing your knowledge then someday in future, you would be on the stage! Ultimately it’s matter of passion and learning how to deliver talks.

Actually it’s upto you how you leverage your time and what you learn and what you do at events!

How can I find out events are happening around me?

Well, there are many sites but I think you can at least try searching for events on meetup.com and if you are developer then go and check whether any Google Developers Group event is happening. If you are in Ahmedabad then Google Developers Group Ahmedabad is there rocking developers community in Ahmedabad since 2011. Likewise there are many other communities available, if not available then take a lead and take an initiative (it’s same like if Google doesn’t have an offer for you then make a Google yourself :) )

4. Attend online courses and Watch videos

These days there are many websites and start-ups coming up with online courses and initiatives around online contents.

If I talk about India then there are many universities and based on my experience of interacting with students of approx 20–25 cities, I have found most of them used to say their syllabus are not updated. My dear friends, if your syllabus are not updated then there is always an internet! who is stopping you from learning, there are many websites like Udacity, Udemy and many others, they have many good online courses. At least, you will learn basics of android, web or IoT programming. And this basic knowledge is only required for getting very first job!

Even though you are experienced engineers, I would highly suggest you to watch videos, especially event videos like Google IO 2017 videos, there are endless things to learn from Google engineers delivering talks, bonus point is while seating at home! Don’t say you are poor and so not able to buy Google IO ticket or any other conference ticket, because these days almost all the events and conferences are used to record sessions.

Category 2: Put your learning/concepts into the execution/practical:

1. Open source contribution

Again, based on my interaction with university students, I have mostly found them saying industry doesn’t have projects or internship for freshers, here I would say they have actually! My dear friends, In India if you look at number of IT companies and start-ups coming up every day, they are generating thousands of jobs everyday and I am pretty sure start-ups are always have openings for freshers, at least when they want to develop MVP (minimum viable product) while not funded or due to a lack of finance.

Another point, to step up and to start putting your knowledge into the execution, I would highly suggest you to start making contribution into open source projects available over websites like Github.

Initially I would suggest you to start making small contribution, 1–2 lines of code commits and then I am sure slowly and gradually you would start making more contributions.

By contribution into open source projects, you are actually putting your knowledge and learning into the execution, and these projects would help you in showing your practical knowledge.

2. Rock your last year internship/projects

I have seen there are many students who are just doing last year internship projects just for the shake of doing, but rather I would say when you are in last year, I would advice you to focus on getting internship at good start-ups or companies, again you would be able to unlock internship or initial jobs at good companies through your open-source contribution or projects that you have done so far.

Internship is actually a turning point of your career because you are just starting for the professional world. Internship is a time where you should try to learn and explore as much as you can. You should not finalise or stick to the particular technology that you will only go for Android or iOS or IoT, I mean don’t stick to any particular technology but be open to any.

But that doesn’t mean you start exploring everything coming on your way, yes you should but stay focused on what you are doing currently, let’s say for example if you are doing android project then focus on it completely and try to learn as much as you can.

3. Prepare contents (write articles/blogs, videos, deliver talks)

It’s not like only experts deliver talks or write blogs or prepare videos, initially they were also a beginner, with this I would say you should start practicing things like writing blogs/articles, prepare video contents, if not delivering talks then at least try to attend events happening around you.

These all above practices i) would help you in learning a lot many things from fellow developers present in the community ii) also will help you in getting unlocking many opportunities, you won’t believe if I share :) iii) also help you in sharing your knowledge with your friends and fellow developers.

There are also many other benefits of generating contents, one of the popular and people favourite is you can generate revenue by putting advertisements, I would say this is what all the students are expecting at least :)
