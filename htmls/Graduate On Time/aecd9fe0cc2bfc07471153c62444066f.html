What are the chances of a freshman graduating in 4 years at N.J. colleges?

Posted May 04, 2016 at 07:00 AM | Updated May 04, 2016 at 01:39 PM
Comment
gradrates.png
Kelly Heyboer | NJ Advance Media for NJ.com
The faces of Rutgers' commencements
The long path to graduation

Most college freshmen arrive on campus with the intention of donning a cap and gown for graduation four years later. But, federal data shows less than half of incoming freshmen earn their degrees in four years at most New Jersey colleges.

College officials say the reasons for low graduation rates are complicated. Some students switch majors and need more time to complete their degrees. Others transfer or drop out for financial reasons. Students also say class scheduling-difficulties and overcrowded courses often make it difficult to graduate in four years.

NJ Advance Media analyzed data gathered by the federal Department of Education to determine the graduation rates for New Jersey's 28 traditional four-year college and university campuses. The data includes the percentage of freshmen who started bachelor's degree programs in 2008 and graduated within either four years or six years.

The following list ranks the New Jersey campuses from the highest to the lowest four-year graduation rate.
