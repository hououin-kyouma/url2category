Graduating High School Early…

Mon Mar 7 2016

Some motivated high school students want to graduate high school early for any number of reasons such as starting college early, traveling, earning money, or taking care of a sick relative, to name a few.

Graduating high school early can be right for some students. Here are some questions that students and their families may consider when making the decision whether or not to graduate high school early.

  • What is their motivation for graduating early?
  • Are they both academically and socially prepared to enter college?
  • Have they gotten all they can out of high school—academically and socially?
  • Is Youth Options or Course Options during their senior year of high school a better fit for them than graduating early?
  • What are their plans for their time after graduating early and before they start college? How will they stay engaged in academics and ready for college-level work?

Those questions are mostly academic-related. There is a social side to think about when graduating early, too.

If a student intends to start college in the spring instead of the fall, the student should know that a small percentage of freshmen begin in the spring. Therefore, orientation and welcome activities are compressed. The student is entering a residence life situation where friendships and communities already exist. And there are fewer new students wandering around, trying to figure things out. These types of situations might lead to the new student feeling isolated or disconnected in his or her new life, and we know that beginning the college experience as part of a community leads to student success.

Is graduating early the right choice? Maybe. It depends. In some cases. For some, but not for everyone.
