Published on Jul 7, 2017

Graduating College has a lot to do with being disciplined and staying focused at all times. YOU ARE ALLOWED to have fun, but don't get too carried away! I went to Bloomfield College from Fall 2013-Spring 2017, I spent exactly 4 years with NO EXTRA CLASSES. I failed two classes, did NO summer classes, and no help from any programs at BLOOMFIELD COLLEGE. I took 4 classes each semester with an exception of one semester where I took 5, If I was able to do it you can do it too!

There are many things people will not tell you about college.
Be mindful and do your research.

*Effective on July 1, 2012, you can receive the Federal Pell Grant for no more than 12 semesters or the equivalent (roughly six years). You'll receive a notice if you're getting close to your limit. If you have any questions, contact your financial aid office.

-TAKE AP Courses in Highschool and make sure the schools you are applying to accept those credits


-Try to get into your college's EOF program [Other states call it something else]

-PAY VERY CLOSE ATTENTION TO DEADLINES

-TIME MANAGEMENT IS KEY, IF YOU ARE FORGETFUL GET A PLANNER OR ORGANIZE YOUR PHONE'S CALENDER

-The Syllabus is KEY in every call you take

-TRY NOT TO TAKE CLASSES EVERYDAY. [I took all my classes on 2-3 days a week, that way I had time for extracurriculars, work and homework]

-PAY MINIMAL ATTENTION TO YOUR SCHOOL'S ADVISORS, Pay more attention to your MAJOR advisor/Chairman


-Cut out all distractions
