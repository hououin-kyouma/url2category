5 Tips to Graduate College in 4 Years

Everyone’s talking about how expensive college has become.  According to the Department of Education, only 40% of students graduate in 4 years, with the average for college graduation closer to 6 years. College costs can really ramp up if you have to stay in school an extra semester or more. Consider the costs of room, board, tuition and even lost wages when you don’t graduate on time. Here’s the 5 top ways you can be sure to graduate on time!

  1. Choose a major by your sophomore year – and stick to it!  Too many times, students waste valuable college credits by switching majors, and that comes with new course and credit requirements.
  2. Be sure to take a full course load.  For many colleges, students need to take 15 hours per semester in order to earn the required 120 credit hours in 4 years.  At a course load of only 12 hours, it will take the student an extra year to earn the 120 credit hours.
  3. Utilize test for credit.  If you can’t get the courses that you need, or cannot get a schedule of 15 hours in a semester, be sure to earn credits through popular test for credit programs like CLEP and DSST. Be sure to be ready to pass the course by taking a test prep class like a JumpCourse.
  4. Make sure you are ready – academically and socially.  Entering college without strength in some core topics like reading, writing and math can lead to taking entry level courses that do not count toward credit.  The best way to be ready is to take some college level courses while still in high school, either through AP or IB.  Even if these courses are not offered at your high school, you can still take the test and earn the credit even before you begin college.
  5. Don’t take on too much outside of academics.  Whether it’s social activities or work, be sure to focus enough energy on your academics to ensure you succeed.  Your primary goal is graduation – don’t lose sight of that!

Stay focused on graduation, and be sure to graduate in 4!  It’s one of the best ways to save money on college.
