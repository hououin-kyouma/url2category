Published on Nov 25, 2012

If you're planning to earn a four-year degree at college - most studies suggest you should plan to take five or six years to get it. We don't agree that this as a reasonable plan! An additional year or two could cost you ten's of thousands to a hundred thousand dollars in extra college costs, living expenses and lost wages.

Completing a bachelor's degree in four years—pays off in dollars and cents.

On this College Smart Radio program we talk - from a Professors' Prospective - about how to Make Sure Your Student Graduates in 4 years.

My guest on this College Smart Radio show is Jeremy S Hyman co-author of the book "The Secrets of College Success."

Remember that college educations come at all levels of cost, and that research, good planning and financial aid may reduce that cost.

If you want to learn more first hand, join me, Beatrice Schultz, at one of my community workshops. Find out more at www.westfacecollegeplanning.com.

This show was originally broadcast on College Smart Radio on November 24, 2012.
