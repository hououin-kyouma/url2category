    Graduating Early: Is It Worth It?

    Bailee Palmisnao, Staff Writer
    December 11, 2015
    Filed under Student Life

    Share on Facebook
    Share on Twitter
    Share via Email

    Hang on for a minute...we're trying to find some more stories you might like.


    Email This Story






    High school is supposed to be the best time of your life, right? This is definitely not true for all students. Some people have awaited graduation day since freshman year. Not everyone is cut out for school. Some people are not social, others do not like being told what they can and cannot do, while others cannot deal with ongoing academic struggle. There are many different reasons why students choose early graduation, and not all are negative. Some students choose to graduate early so they can start college a semester early and in turn start the fast track to their career, while others choose to enlist in the military and graduate early depending on their ship date to boot camp.

    “

    Utilize early graduation if it fits into your future plans, not just as a way to escape high school. ”

    — Bailee Palmisano

    Nicholas Porch, a graduating senior at Granite City High School has enlisted into the Army. His ship date is just a few short days after early graduation. Nick’s father was an Army Ranger, which motivated Nick to join the same branch of service. Nick enlisted in the Army on his 17th birthday, and has been counting down the days ever since. He has a great plan for right after graduation. He knows what he wants to do, and has worked hard to make it all happen in his shortened senior year of high school. GCHS should be proud of a what a great student Nick is and we are thankful that he is choosing to serve our country.

    Assistant Principal, Ms. Petrillo, said seventy to eighty students are graduating early and that number grows each year. She believes that students should only plan to graduate early if they have an exact plan for their future. Ms. Petrillo is concerned about the growing trend of graduating early, and says, “Unless you can tell me exactly what you plan to do in January, you should not graduate early.” Early graduation was intended for the students who choose to start a semester ahead in college, join the military, or start full time into the workforce. It was not created as an easy way to finish high school early. Ms. Petrillo suggests that students think hard about their future plans before making the decision to graduate early.

    Another GCHS student, Samantha Wise, is planning on getting a job that will eventually help her career after early graduation. She is planning on working at Granite City Nursing and Rehabilitation Center as a dietary aid. She wants to gain hands-on experience in the field she intends to pursue in college, so graduating high school early helps her achieve this goal. Next fall she will attend Southwestern Illinois College to get an associates degree in science to pursue her lifelong career.

    Some December graduates, like Sam and Nick, have a game plan. Other students that I spoke with have no idea what they are going to do after graduation. Graduating early is a big decision. Anyone considering this should have an open discussion with their parents or a responsible adult you respect. We have an excellent Guidance Department here at GCHS who are always willing to answer questions and help discuss the pros and cons of some major life choices. Talk it over, do the research, and then you can make an informed decision. Even if high school isn’t enjoyable for you, stick it out one more semester to give yourself a chance to decide what the future holds for you. If you have survived this long, you can survive four and half more months. As for those of you who are about to graduate in just 15 days, good luck to you in your future endeavors. Remember your time spent here at GCHS, the friendships made, and the lessons learned. Before Christmas you will be a high school graduate! Make the most of the days to come and congratulations on all your accomplishments!
