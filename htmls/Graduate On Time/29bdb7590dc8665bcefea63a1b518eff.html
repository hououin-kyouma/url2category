MoneySmarts

This multifaceted financial literacy program was established as part of an overall program to assist students to make informed financial management decisions and decrease overall student debt. Students can participate by interacting with the MoneySmarts website, listening to the “MoneySmarts U” and “How Not to Move Back in With Your Parents” podcasts, and scheduling an appointment with the IU MoneySmarts Team, a group of students who provide one-on-one and group financial education.

Free Course Guarantee

Indiana House Enrolled Act 1348-2013 requires that public higher education institutions in the state provide degree maps to all new first-time, full-time students beginning fall 2014. It also provides an important new guarantee for students: Follow your map and register during priority registration, and you are guaranteed a space in the courses you need or else the institution must provide the course for free in a future semester.

Learn more about the free course guarantee

Banded Tuition

In an effort to encourage on-time graduation and decrease the cost of education, undergraduate tuition for IUPUI and the regional campuses will move from a per-credit hour rate to a flat rate for students taking 12 to 18 credit hours effective fall 2016.
