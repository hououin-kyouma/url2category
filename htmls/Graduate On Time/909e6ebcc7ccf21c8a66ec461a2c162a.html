UCR BY THE NUMBERS

#1

university in the country based on graduation rates, affordability and the percentage of Pell Grant recipients. (Time Magazine, 2014)

Top 4%

best colleges for educational quality, affordability and alumni success. (Money Magazine, 2017)

#2

UCR's ranking based on contribution to the public good. (Washington Monthly, 2014)

4th

in student diversity among Forbes' ranking of America’s Top Colleges. (Forbes, 2016)

Top

in the nation for African-American graduation rates. (The Education Trust, 2017)

98%

of UCR faculty have a Ph.D. or equivalent.

3rd

best college for Hispanic students based on academics, tuition, a cultural center, degree programs and scholarships. (BestColleges.com, 2015)

#3

in student community service participation and hours served. (Washington Monthly, 2016)

6th

in the nation in the number of faculty members receiving prestigious awards. (Washington Monthly, 2014)

Top

"Golden Age" university, established between 1945-1966, scoring high for research and teaching. (Times Higher Education World University Rankings, 2017)

19:1

student-to-faculty ratio. Work closely with faculty!
