8 Tips to Graduate College in 4 Years

Recent studies have shown that those with a college education will earn on average $800,000 more than someone without a degree.  Great!  You are ready to further your education and get a foundation for a great career.  All you have to do now is go to college and get in and get out in 4 years, right? Well that is not the case for most students. Did you know only 38% of students actually graduate in 4 years?  This is adding to the national student loan debt, and we here at JumpCourse think everyone should have the goal of #Graduatein4!

Here are 8 tips that can help stay on track and graduate in 4 years!

  1. Start planning early – Start making decisions about college during high school! Start figuring out which college you would like to attend by late junior year or early your senior year. This can help you map out ways to enter college having already earned some college credits that are accepted at your school of choice!
  2. Take AP classes in high school – Earning college credits while you are in high school can give you a head start! Advanced Placement classes or AP, are college level courses that allow you to take an exam at the end of a semester to earn college credits. The test is scored 1-5 and these credits are accepted in almost all US colleges and universities.
  3. Decide your major during your freshman year of college – The earlier you can decide your major, the faster it will be to finish. When you declare your major, you will have the opportunity to map out every class you need! Your academic advisor can be a huge help in mapping your path to graduation, as many classes require specific pre-requisites.
  4. Earn credit over the summer – Taking summer courses or pursuing test for credit can speed up the college process. While most people are taking it easy, you can earn college credits in an environment that is lower key than normal and in a shorter time period.
  5.  Take a minimum of 15 credits per semester – For most colleges, they require a minimum of 120 credits to graduate. So let’s do the math! If you want to graduate in four years, that is eight semesters. If you take 15 credits that equals exactly 120.
  6. Meet with your Academic Advisor every semester or more – Meet often with your adviser, and when you do be prepared with ideas and questions about which courses you would need to take.  Also visit your adviser if you need other academic assistance.  They are a great resource to make sure you stay on the path to graduation.
  7. Keep your grades in order – Always keep your grades on your mind. For most academic programs, they require a minimum GPA standard. If you are below, you will have to take “dead credits” until your grades get better.
  8. Take advantage of testing for credit – Testing for credit is one of the hidden secrets of college! Testing for credit allows you to earn college credits by only taking a test. This allows you to avoid certain classes but still fulfill your requirements. Popular test for credits are CLEP and DSST. Take a look at jumpcourse.com, which offers prep courses for these tests!
