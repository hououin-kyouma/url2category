What are some tips for graduating college in four years?

Update Cancel

Elizabeth H. Simmons, Dean, Professor, ACE Fellow, Physicist
Answered 112w ago
I suggest that you speak with an academic advisor as early as possible in your college career. Depending on your college's procedures, this may be a faculty member in your major or a specially trained staff member.

Ask the advisor to help you create a plan for which courses you will take in each term for the full four years. This will ensure that you will meet all general college requirements as well as requirements for your major. It will also let you see whether you'll need to take any courses outside the regular academic year (e.g., in summer session if you're in the U.S.).

Try to make a draft plan yourself before meeting with the advisor, so you are familiar with the requirements and can narrow down the list of questions you'll need to ask.

Revisit the plan every term to see whether you're still on track or need to make changes (for example, you may decide to change majors after a few terms). If you need to make changes, do check in with your advisor again, to make sure you don't mistakenly forget about any requirements.
