Aligning my Career Intentions with my Paycheck, or, Why I Quit my Job


Recently, my boss called me into her office to offer what she called "a lateral career move." She was proposing a shift in my responsibilities, loosely tied to some other changes that were occuring. She thought the new position would be more in line with my interests and talents. She was right, but I declined her offer. Because I had already made up my mind to resign from my job-- on that day, no less. 

Everything about my job sounded pretty okay on paper-- interesting work, excellent benefits (free health insurance), a handful of wonderful colleagues. But I had to quit. I'd been there for over five years, and somewhere along the way, my job became exactly the thing I didn't want. In turn, I lost my passion. And drive. Which, it turns out, make it extremely difficult to really show up for work every day.

In the course of those five years, my life has changed so, so much. I started working on my master's degree in library science in January 2007. I was 22, fresh out of undergrad. The job market for librarians wasn't so great even then, but I was passionate. My plan was to work hard and then be willing to move pretty much anywhere for a job. Which was a great idea, in theory. Life had other plans.

First, my parents (and younger sister) moved back to the Austin area from upstate New York (where I had grown up). I love my family and enjoy spending time with them (worth noting, since some do "family" better when it's from a distance). Second, I went on a date in February 2008. The guy was cute and funny and smart, and by April 2009, we were living together. And that certainly didn't make moving "pretty much anywhere" a very appealing option when I graduated with an MLS the following month. 

I was determined to stick it out in Austin. I had a full-time job in a public library, so I was still gaining valuable work experience. The job didn't pay great, but at least now I had a gainfully employed boyfriend to subsidize my housing arrangements. 

For the next couple of years, I dutifully searched for jobs in the Austin area. I applied to many. I interviewed for a handful. I often got very, very frustrated (it was ugly, really). My boyfriend became my fiance. We planned a wedding and bought a house, all while I continued to work the same job I had while I was in library school. 

At that point, I had to come to grips with the fact that my intentions for my career and how I was actually earning a paycheck were extremely out of whack. Somewhere in the space of those last few years, my husband and I decided we wanted to start a family together in the nearish future, and not only that, we wanted a parent (me, by default, because he's the primary breadwinner) to stay at home with our children when they were small. My intention became to actually be able to work from home, so as to not deal with the difficult reality of taking a decade off from the workforce and then trying to get back into it. 

And the thing about working in a public library is that you generally can't do it from home. So what was I doing working in one?

Eventually, I stumbled into freelance book indexing as a viable career path. It seemed like it would be a good fit for me, and my library science background would definitely be a benefit. I registered for a distance education course, which I finally completed in July of this year. I did well in the class and found that I enjoyed the process (I also find it incredibly challenging in the best possible way). I also socked away most of my income, knowing I would need cash to invest in my business, as well as cover bills until I could generate income.

And finally, I resigned from my position. It was liberating.

While I could say that I feel lucky for even being able to make such a career shift, I think that downplays how hard my husband works to keep our little family unit in the black. It's a decision that was not made lightly, and not without many, many tears and pep talks from my husband (who, thankfully, truly believes in my success).

So this is where I am today. I've set my intention (to generate a hearty income working from home). I'm doing the hustle work (more on that soon).

And I know the magic is about to happen.
