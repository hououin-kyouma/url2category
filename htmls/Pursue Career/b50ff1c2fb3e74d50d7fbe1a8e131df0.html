Need New Job in Michigan said: HR managers feel this way because when you get someone from college, you get a clean slate. If you hire someone with experience, they come from another company whose culture may differ from the new company, and there's a fear of baggage.

I've got my HR guys trained well. They filter out people who want too much money, don't live in the physical location of the job, and people who need visa sponsorship. Everything else comes to me as a hiring manager, so I don't have to worry about them filtering someone who is good. The downside of this approach is there are a LOT of people who apply to stuff that they shouldn't, so I spend time weeding out people who HR would have eliminated if I gave them more control. Probably 5-10% of the resumes who hit my desk end up with a face-to-face interview.
