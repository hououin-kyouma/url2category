Book Club: Developing your skills to a dream job

Have you ever been told to follow your passion?

Last week during my first Lever book club, we discussed this common advice that has guided the career paths of so many new grads, job seekers, and unhappy workers. But as Cal Newport, the author of So Good They Can’t Ignore You, warns us, it’s actually terrible advice.

Advising young people to blindly “follow their passion” may sound inspiring, but it de-emphasizes the value of hard work and intentionally developing skills that are valuable to businesses and the world. Too many professionals have been unintentionally misled when they were told they just need an abundance of courage to follow their dreams — without first building the expertise and skills that would support long-term viability. The book explains, you should instead aim to build rare and valuable skills until you are “so good they can’t ignore you.” Developing career capital takes time and effort, but also lends itself to a much more realistic path to a dream job, when you take a more active, deliberate approach rather than expecting one to be handed to you.

The book and the following discussion with my team was also a great way to get to know my coworkers beyond the everyday interactions. For example, Ciara shared how she didn’t want to spend her twenties playing it safe and blending in, even though she was rising up in the ranks at one of the most well-known tech companies in the world. She joined Lever’s Customer Success team back in 2014, despite her parents’ concerns and warnings. (“We can’t even find this company on Google! Are you sure it exists?” They told her.) But ultimately, Ciara’s determination for impact and desire to achieve new levels of growth pushed her to take the plunge.

Phil shared how his brother became a massage therapist after years of being trapped in a string of unfulfilling jobs. By chance, he realized what he truly wanted to do was help people heal. He then dedicated himself to earning his massage therapist license (a no easy feat), and he now loves his work. In contrast, many others (including myself) in early professional years, need to scratch the itch of pursuing a “passion” and thought our careers had to lie in areas like writing, music, or art. But passions-turned-work can often lead to burnout and thus, our passions are often better enjoyed as hobbies.

Book club gave me an eye-opening perspective as a new Leveroo. Every day, I get to hustle and build my career capital, while feeling a sense of accomplishment. My role in Product Ops stretches me every day to learn troubleshooting skills and build technical expertise, and my team is the smartest and most impressive I’ve ever worked with.

I’ve worked at a dozen companies in my career, and Lever is by far the most genuine and dedicated to helping employees’ growth by cultivating an environment that is collaborative, supportive, diverse, and transparent. Like the rest of my team, I joined Lever because I sought a challenging environment to develop and apply my skills, hitting goals that are always slightly beyond my reach. Every day, I’m so proud of the the small and big victories from this very focused and driven group of people who strive to be so good you can’t ignore them.

Recently, I asked a group of friends, “Who likes their jobs?” and two of them immediately replied, “You do.” As a recovering former finance professional who left a stable job to pursue a “passion” career in writing, I never would have expected to land at a startup like Lever. My career has been circuitous with many ups and downs (oh, such low downs), but I’ve enjoyed the journey and finally feel like I’m in the right place at the right time. Lever couldn’t be a more ideal company for me, not only for the great people, the benefits like catered lunches, or that the company color is gray (also my favorite). But ultimately, it’s because Lever instills constant learning for my own personal growth and career capital. I’ve learned a lot in my short tenure at Lever so far, and am so excited to hit the next set of ambitious, aggressive goals with my team.
