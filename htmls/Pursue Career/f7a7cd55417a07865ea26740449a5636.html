Summary
Are you trying to figure out what your next career move should be? 'What job should I pursue?' 'Should I accept this promotion?' 'Should I start my own business?' Before you answer this question, it's good to know what your values are. Values are the things that you believe are important in the way you live and work, and determine your priorities. Everyone has heard the advice that you should pursue your passion, but sometimes that's easier said than done. Many people become stuck and don't know where to begin, or find themselves on a career path that doesn't align with their values. When it comes to mapping out a career path, one size does not fit all and there are even significant differences depending on what stage one may be at in their life.

Andy Ku

Andy Ku is the Director of Product Marketing at LinkedIn, with focus on career products. With degrees from Harvard and Stanford, he has led his own start-ups in addition to having a previous stint with Google.

Gwen Lane

Gwen Lane is a blogger, influencer and entrepreneur focused on helping other individuals pursue their passions as their careers. She is also the founder of The LA Girl, a Los Angeles lifestyle blog that seeks to inspire discovery and passion within the city.

Megan Lathrop

Megan is a certified Money Coach in San Francisco with the Capital One Money Coaching program where she helps people form a more authentic connection to their money. She specializes in helping people "re-charge" their relationship with money and see money through a new lens.

Ben Ricciardi

Ben is the Founder and CEO of Times10, a creative agency based in Burbank, California. He is a branding and social media maverick who followed his heart, instincts and entrepreneurial vision to build a business from the ground up.

Jon Wolske

Jon Wolske is the Culture Evangelist for Zappos Insights, a part of the Zappos Family of companies headquartered in Las Vegas, NV. Jon uses his eight years of experience in the Zappos Family to share stories with a focus on culture and customer experiences and their importance in business today.
