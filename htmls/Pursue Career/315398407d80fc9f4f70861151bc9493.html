8 Things You Can Do to Figure Out Your Career Path

By Gabby Romero Last updated Mar 28, 2017
Share

When you’re young, it’s understandable that you don’t really know what you want to do with your life. While it’s totally normal, you can’t live like that forever. Eventually you’re going to need to figure out your career path. If you need a little (or a lot) of help, here are eight things you can do to get on track.

Do some soul searching

If you don’t have any idea what you’re going to do with your life in regards to your career, it’s time to sit down and really think about what you’re doing now and what you want to be doing. Being honest with yourself is the first step you need to take to finding success.

Weigh your passions and your skills

When you don’t have a career path, thinking about what you love to do and what you’re good at are two good places to start. You want to do something you’re passionate about so you won’t end up getting bored with your career. In addition, you need to recognize your skills so you can pursue something you’re actually good at.

Explore new internships

If you want to test out new career fields, consider applying for different internships nearby. You’ll be exposed to different workplace environments and types of work, so you can gain a greater understanding of what you want to pursue and what you don’t want to consider.

Try for a promotion

If you’re not willing to drop everything and start over in a new field, consider leveling up in your current job. Make yourself known to your employer and take advantage of every opportunity you’re given to climb the professional ladder.

Expand your network

It’s true that it’s not really about what you know, it’s who you know. Expand your network through your current job or use social media websites like LinkedIn to improve your relationships with other people. This will ultimately serve you well and help you discover and follow your career path.

Take initiative

The best way to move forward in your career is to avoid just blindly listening to your boss. Instead, take some initiative in the workplace, either through small things or larger projects. It’ll show your employer that you have what it takes to be truly successful in the company.

Find a mentor

A mentor can help guide you towards the right path in your career. They’ll offer you advice, help you build your skills, and help you gain meaningful connections with other people in the field. If you’re lost in terms of what you’re doing with your career, find a mentor.

Establish goals

Ultimately, you can’t really find success in your profession if you never set a goal. Take some time to figure out what you want to do and what’s realistic for you. That way, you’ll know what steps you need to take to eventually find success.
