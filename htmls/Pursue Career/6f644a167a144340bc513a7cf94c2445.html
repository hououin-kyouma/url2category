How to Figure Out Your Career Path: The Easy Way…

By Aisha

On March 28, 2017

In Career

Spread the love

Two of the most important decisions we must make in our adult lives are “which career to choose?” And how to identify the right career path? “Regardless of whether you are planning your career path for the first time, or if you are thinking of having a career change.” Below are few steps that will help you in the most effective way to take that first step in the right direction.

Examine yourself

Each one of us has a unique set of skills, experience, knowledge, and interests. Some of us may be good at art, others may be good at business, while others may find their comfort in music. Individual interests, skills can give you an idea of what career path you should be considering.

Write down your plan

After analysing your different skills, experience, knowledge and interest. The next step is to write down what you hope to achieve in the next few days, weeks, months and if you can think far ahead, then go as far as jotting down your goals for the year. This write up from you will help you to narrow down your options and start to have a feel of what kind of career you will like to step into. Next will be highlighting a minimum of three to five jobs that best suits your skills, experience, knowledge, and interests.

Career prospects

After highlighting three to five jobs that will best suit you or you might be interested in, you now need to look at the requirements for each of the roles. At this stage, you need to take note of what is required for the chosen job/s. This will be the best time to reach out to family, friends or people you might know that are already working in this area of interest, to find out more about such roles and the feel of it.

Goal setting

From the advice, you have received speaking to people, you should be able to narrow down your job list to hopefully two. Narrowing it down to two will help you to be more focus and considerate in picking the right career path.

Next… you now need to develop an action plan. One way to develop a good action plan it to make sure your plan or the goal you tend to achieve is Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic and Timely (S.M.A.R.T). To achieve a goal that is Smart, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic and Timely, you need to remember to be consistent, focus, and put in the work.

Training

Having a SMART plan will help you to identify the missing link: such as maybe more training, experience and skills that will help you to step into the career path of your choice. This is the last and most important step in determining how to make or establish your career. This is a step that consumes most of the time, effort and a stage where people tend to give up.

The most important thing is that as a mum whichever career path you decide to move into, let it be the career path that will be worth waking up in the morning with a smile on your face, knowing that it will be all worth it.
