What are the important skills to develop in Marketing?

Breaking into the Marketing Industry - eBook

Untitled Document

If you waited to acquire every available skill used in marketing before making your next move up the career ladder you would be waiting for decades –literally.

Marketing is one of the most multi-skilled occupations there is. And if your career ambitions lie in the here and now and not when you should be drawing your pension, learn to master the skills needed for the job you are already doing and identify those you will need for the next job you have your sights set on.

Each new role in your career will introduce you to a whole new set of skills that you will be expected to learn on the job. But you’re in marketing right- surely you will want to make yourself stand out from the rest of your colleagues who may also have ambitions to win that next promotion?

And the best way of doing just that will be to show an employer that you already have some of the skills needed to do a good job in the role you are applying for.

For example, if you are looking to move from Senior Marketing Executive to Marketing Manager your new role won’t simply mean an increase in responsibility, it will teach you how to become a Jack of all trades.

From having greater control and influence in the final decision making process when it comes to things like product development or marketing strategy, to managing disparate groups of people such as designers, production, advertising and PR’s or coming to grips with employment issues, marketing managers have more responsibility placed on them than you might initially expect.

By recognising the diverse range of skills that are needed in your next job and taking proactive steps to acquire as many of them as you can, your application will stand out from everyone else’s.

Not only will you be demonstrating your commitment to your own personal development and passion for marketing, you will give your career prospects a huge boost because you will be more likely to hit the ground running rather than need a honeymoon period to find your feet in your new role – and for marketers who love to save time and make money that makes you a sure-fire investment.

But if you are unsure what new skills you need then ask. Observe the people in your office doing the job you want, look at the job descriptions in adverts or better still obtain a copy of the full job specification for your HR department and circle the key attributes that your company looks for in each role that you want.

Once you know what you need to learn find out what in-house or external training courses are run by your employer, ask to shadow someone already doing the job if you can watch learn and assist in some of their duties. And contact the professional association that represents your specific field of expertise.
