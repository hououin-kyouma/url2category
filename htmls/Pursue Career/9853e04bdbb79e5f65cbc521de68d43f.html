Student jobs and internships help you to pay for your college bills and give you valuable work experience at the same time.

Federal Work-Study Program
Work-study is a form of financial aid that provides students with financial need, part-time jobs in fields similar to student's degree.

Learn more...

Internships
Internships are a great way to get practical work experience in your field of study. Internships can be paid or unpaid, required or optional, for credit or non-credit, depending on the area of study. Gateway internships are directly coordinated through the appropriate academic department.

See Program Requirements for Internships...

Employment Connections
Gateway's Career and Employment Services office provides students and graduates with employment assistance. We educate students in the areas of career planning, employment trends, job searching, and connect students with employment opportunities and employers.

For more information or an appointment contact Career and Employment Services, 1-800-247-7122.

Wisconsin TechConnect
Wisconsin TechConnect is an online job board and a great tool for Gateway students to search for employment and internships. This is a free resource available to Gateway alumni and students enrolled in a Technical Diploma or Associate Degree Program.

Instructions on how to register, search for jobs and post a resume on Wisconsin TechConnect.
