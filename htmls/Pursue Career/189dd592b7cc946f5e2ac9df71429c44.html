Originally Answered: How do college students find jobs after graduation?

Full disclosure: I work for WayUp, the #1 site for college students and recent grads to find jobs and internships. Our mission is to connect students and grads with awesome opportunities at over 9,000 companies,from startups to Fortune 500s, posting listings on our site. We work with students from all over the country every single day to make this happen. Here’s a breakdown of how to be as efficient as possible in securing a job.

Identifying what you want to do

Take some time to think about what it is you want to be doing – what industry, what function, etc. Keep an open mind. Just because you’re a marketing major, for example, doesn’t mean you have to choose a marketing company – almost every industry has a marketing function/department. This should be more about what interests you – you want to enjoy the work you’re doing.

The Resume

This is the employer’s first impression of you, and most hiring managers only look at your resume for mere seconds (6-15 seconds on average), so you want this to be polished. We have a bunch of blog posts about cleaning up your resume, but the most important things you want to ensure are:

  1. There are NO spelling and grammar mistakes
  2. Your resume only has experience relevant to the job you’re applying to
  3. Your resume is concise. Use only a few well-crafted bullet points for each previous job.

If you make an account on WayUp, you won’t need to create an additional resume – your profile acts as the resume and cover letter.

Finding the Job and Applying

This is the first actionable step and can be the most intimidating (just like getting started on any project).

  • Use your campus resources. Career services are there to help, and some of your tuition dollars help fund this department – so you might as well take advantage! Check out one of our blog posts about this.
  • Public job boards can be a starting point, but they can be overwhelming – so many jobs, and you can never be sure if you’re qualified for the job (even if in reality, you are). Submitting your resume to these places can feel like sending your resume into a deep dark abyss – no updates if it was received, read or declined.
  • If you use WayUp, you can make a single profile and you will only be shown jobs that you are actually qualified for, meaning everything thing you see, you’re all set to apply for. You can apply to as many jobs as you want, and you can apply to most jobs with just 1 click. You can be sure that the employer received your application, and you’ll also hear back no matter if you made it to the interview or not. WayUp lets you see jobs all over the country from over 9,000 hiring employers.

The number one thing here is to keep pushing and to not lose motivation. Many say finding a job is a numbers game, that the more places you apply to, the more likely it is you’ll land one.

The Interview

Of course, be prepared for an interview. This is your opportunity to sell yourself and make yourself stand out against all other applicants. Our team at WayUp has tons of resources to help you prepare for an interview, including this cheat sheet of questions and some interview secrets from our CEO and former Googler, Liz.

Companies like Disney, Elite Daily, Google, and Yahoo! are posting jobs on WayUp, as well as incredible startups which help give students a ton of experience.
