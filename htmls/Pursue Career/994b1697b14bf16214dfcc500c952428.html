How a Skills Inventory Can Help You Figure Out Your Career Path

Posted: May 23rd, 2012 | Author: astra | Filed under: Tips and Advice | No Comments »

If you’re thinking about a new career or even a move within your current career, it can be scary to think about the options you have. One of the best ways to figure out your career path is to take an assessment of your skills. Figuring out what you’re best at and what skills you really love to use can help you steer your career in a meaningful and fulfilling direction.

So how do you figure out where your talents lie?

1. Use a skills list. Mark all the skills you’ve ever used or that are even slightly related to something you’ve done. Then go back through your checked items and mark the skills that you really enjoy, or that you believe make you marketable. This is the first step to figuring out what skills you would like your new career to utilize. Here is a good list to get you going.

2. Take a personality test. Sometimes even the most basic, free tests can help you gain insight into what you truly love to do. Here’s a basic test to get you started.

3. Read job descriptions. Look for job descriptions that interest you and highlight or copy and paste the parts that you are qualified for. The more a description is highlighted, the more likely the job is a good fit for you.

4. Know your values. Taking inventory of your core values can help steer you in the right direction. You will learn what type of job environment is more suited to your life values. Here’s a good place to start.

Whatever your career path, it is helpful to know what skills you have so you can help potential employers understand the value you can bring to their company.
