3 Ways To Tap Into Your Inner Passion

by Susanna Halonen - February 3, 2014

How many times have you heard the term find your passion? Probably quite a few.

Well I’m here to suggest a different approach to follow your passion.

You don’t have to choose one specific activity or thing to direct your passion to. Instead, you can choose to bring it out of yourself and express it all across your life. This is one of the keys of becoming engaged at work.

Recent research on the passionate way of being has shown something very interesting:

Passion doesn’t have to be that limiting that it only covers one aspect of your life.

When you choose to pursue a passionate way of being, you choose to bring passion out in all aspects of your life, helping you live a more fulfilling life where you feel energised and free. It’s also been tied being to more resilient to stress and more adaptable in the face of challenge. What more could you want?

So how can you put this into action in your workplace?

Here are three steps which will help you pursue a passionate way of being so you can become more engaged in your work.

1. How is the work you’re doing & the company you’re working for aligned with who you are?


A big part of being able to bring out your passion is aligning your behaviour at work with your true values and beliefs. You have to build a career that’s aligned with what’s important to you as this will make you the happiest and best performing you.

Spend some time on this question and create a list of all the different levels on how you connect to your role and company.

2. What is the positive impact you’re creating with your role or by working for this company?

How is what you’re doing contributing to the awesomeness in this world? What’s the mark that you’re leaving on the planet, and what are you getting known for by doing the work you do?

This is really about understanding the why behind what you do, and kind of personal brand you’re creating. Remember that positive impact can come in many shapes and forms, it doesn’t mean you have to work for a charity or be a surgeon saving lives every day.

You could be the one in the team who’s got the optimism and is always infecting others with their positivity. Get creative here and don’t limit yourself. Think about all the possible ways you’re having a positive effect right now.

3. How could you use your natural strengths in a variety of ways in your work daily?


Using your natural abilities at work gives you more enjoyment and boosts your performance.

It’s counterproductive (and no fun) to focus on your weaknesses when you have all these amazing, unique strengths you can use. Using them in new, different ways daily improves creativity, avoids their overuse, and also contributes to developing your weaknesses.

Good at communication? Can you do more work that helps you write, present or helps others communicate better? The opportunities to try different things are endless.

Your Turn to Tap Into Your Inner Passion

Now it’s up to you to put this into practice. Become more aware of how what you do is aligned with who you are, the positive impact you’re creating, and the natural strengths you’re putting to good use. Then you can start at creating even more ties by finding ways to incorporate more projects into your role that are even more aligned with your values and your why.

Go and get started – and be ready to let your passion come out.
