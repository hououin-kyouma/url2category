How can you land your dream job?

17 Apr 2016

Ning Pan with Kristofor Lawson

Skip YouTube Video

FireFox NVDA users - To access the following content, press 'M' to enter the iFrame.

YOUTUBE: How to find your dream job

In this episode of Studying in Australia employers and career consultants give us some tips about finding jobs and meeting potential employers.

Australia attracts tens of thousands of international students each year who come to study at universities across the country.

After spending so much time and money on their education, finding a dream job after graduation becomes the ultimate aim for most international students – and the perfect end to their Australian experience.

But with more than 180,000 Australian students graduating each year, landing your dream job isn't easy. In this series of Studying in Australia, we sit down with some sought-after employers, career consultants and successful job seekers and ask them about how you can stand out in your internship, resume and interview to give you the edge when job hunting.

In this episode, we discuss where to find the best job opportunities.

Here are the top tips from the career consultants and employers we spoke to:

  • Debra Eckersley from PwC suggests students meet and impress the company representatives during the campus campaign time to stand out from the crowd.
  • Stanley Litow from IBM encourages students to engage in community service to demonstrate their initiative and abilities to future employers.
  • Chong Ren from Financial Services emphasises the importance of networking. He suggests students join social groups and participate in events that the university hold with big companies to meet contacts.
  • Kate Abraham from Melbourne Careers Centre says students should make full use of the fast growing professional social media websites such as LinkedIn to look for opportunities.
