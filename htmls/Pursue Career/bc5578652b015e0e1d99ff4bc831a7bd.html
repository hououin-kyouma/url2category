How Do You Find a Job You Love?

By: Jamie | Published: September 8, 2017

Most people spend a large proportion of their adult life at work, thinking about work, or talking about work. As such, if you don’t like your job, it can make for a rather unpleasant existence. At SpotOn Connections we take the time to get to know every jobseeker as an individual so that we can find out what it is that they want from their career and life. By doing this we are able to place individuals in roles that they genuinely enjoy and help them to thrive, ensuring a mutually beneficial outcome for clients, candidates, and us. We are so sure of our placements that we offer an industry leading three month refund policy on all our placements. In the spirit of finding a job that you love, here are a few things to think about.

What Would You Do If You Weren’t Getting Paid?

Whilst money is an important part of every job, it isn’t the single defining factor of how you will feel about a certain job or career. Ask yourself what you would be doing if money wasn’t an issue. This will help you understand what makes you tick and what will make you happy on a daily basis.

Speak to People Around You

Oftentimes we don’t know ourselves as well as we think we do, and it is the people around us that can see clearly because they have an outside perspective. Speak to people you are close to – friends and family – and ask them what they see you doing. Getting that extra perspective can be extremely insightful.

Where Do You See Yourself in the Future?

You don’t need to look too far ahead, otherwise you will lose sight of how to get there, but where do you see yourself 1, 2, or 5 years from now? What do you need to get to that place? This will help you balance what you want with what you need and help you to realise a path to get to the place that you want to be.
