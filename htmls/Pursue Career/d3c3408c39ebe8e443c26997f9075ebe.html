Tips For Finding A Job You Love


The importance of you happiness within your job can not be stressed enough. Besides the obvious reason, you will be miserable, there are a million other reasons why loving what you do is essential. Remember, an ordinary 9-5 job takes up 2000 hours a year, 40 hours a week and 8 hours a day. Now imagine being unhappy everyday of your life for 8 hours a day. You will dedicate at least 25% of your life to work, so you might as well find a work that you love and feel like its worth 25% of your life.

1. Aptitude Tests To Narrow Your Options
If you are uncertain about what you wish to do concerning your career, an aptitude test can help you narrow down your options. These test estimate your suitable career based on your strengths, weaknesses and interests. It also helps differentiate between your skills and your passion because you don’t always have a passion for what you’re truly good at.

2. Constantly Try New Things
If you have a job that makes you unhappy, leave as soon as possible. I know this is easier said than done considering South Africa’s shortage of jobs but it’s a risk you should be willing to take if you truly want to love your job. A safer option would be to find a new job first before leaving the current one you are in. Internships are a great way to explore a career that might appeal to you or spark an interest. Even offering to do free work to shadow someone in your field of interest. As long as you get out of your comfort zone and try new things.

3. Curiosity Killed The Cat (Or so they say)
Passion should be driving your career moves, but it is your curiosity that will ultimately help you find your way. For instance, your passion might be watersports, but you’re not particularly good at swimming, so instead, you’ll find your way to writing on water sports for a magazine or website, etc. That is just one example, but the point is that you should follow your curiosity.

4. Make a decision
After step 1, 2 and 3 you should have somewhat of an idea on what you want to do, now all you have to do is decide. It shouldn’t be that hard now that you have tried multiple things. Allowing your passion to drive you and your curiosity to guide you have gotten you this far. Now you have to trust your instinct. If by now, you still haven’t found your niche, it’s time to step out of your comfort zone and follow the process with a different passion or keep trying new things within your current passion.
