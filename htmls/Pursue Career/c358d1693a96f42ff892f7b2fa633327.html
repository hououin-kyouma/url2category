What is Your Dream Job? How to Find your Dream Career?


Ads By Google

When we were young and asked in school what we wanted to be when we grew up, we used to come up with a number of creative answers – someone wanted to be a policeman, another person wanted to be a doctor, a firefighter, some even wanted to be a rock star.

Of course, not everyone has achieved their childhood dream job, and there are several reasons for that.
While it would be unrealistic for a person to achieve their childhood dream job or his/her innocent childhood fantasies, wouldn’t it be great if you could find out your dream career – the job of your dreams?

Here are some tips that you might use:

A.) Making your Dream Come True – Identifying and Analyzing Career Interests

Everyone has dreams.
It is only the ones who follow them in a dedicated manner and make changes in their lives to pursue these dreams that actually turn them into achievements.

Assess your interests and desires,

  1. What are your key interests?
  2. What drives you?
  3. Who do you really want to be?
  4. Do you identify any area of choice?
  5. Do you recognize any field that you are passionate about?
  6. What jobs you would find most interesting?
  7. What do you like to achieve in this career?

You will have clear vision of your future after finding answers to the above questions.

Don’t miss reading: How to Find that Dream Career: What could be the Career of your Dreams

B.) Make Yourself Competent for your Dreams – Career Prospective

Assuming you already have a dream of a career, you have to focus on the requirements. You should try to bring about the abilities and fulfill the requirements that are mandatory for pursuing a career in the profession you wish.
For example, if you dream of being a Human Resource officer, you should make sure that you educate yourself and get the required degrees to pursue the career of a Human Resource officer.
However, that’s not all. You may want to research more about the career requirements –

  1. Identify the background of successful professional in this filed.
  2. What skills do they possess?
  3. What formal education is required?
  4. Does one need unique skills to peruse a career in this industry?
  5. Any special commitment?
  6. How hard is it to enter this career? What are the entry-level difficulties?
  7. Does it require changing way of life?

This is true for almost any career that you might want to pursue.

C.) Hone Your Core Competencies

While most requirements for the dream job can be achieved via training and education, there are some aspects that come naturally to a person.
In the case of a singer or an actor, while they can educate themselves in screen presence, dialogue delivery, etc, it is their core competency of acting that makes them the true star. This is not only true in the field of media, but several others – like if you wish to be a computer programmer, you should find out whether your core competencies are in mathematics, logic, etc.
If you wish to go into web designing and digital graphics, your core competencies should be a fine hand when it comes to art, the right choice in colors and a creativity that makes you different from the thousands of other digital artists around.
Of course, this does not mean that if you do not have the core competencies, you cannot pursue your dream career.

However, it is definitely true that if you are  natural at something, you will progress a lot more in that particular line of work.

Make an insight inventory of your distinguished qualities:

  1. Personal Abilities: skills and key strengths you have
  2. Personal Traits: characteristics you posses
  3. Early Knowledge and interests on the field

D.) What Does Your Dream Career Require From You?

When you are young, you have a lot of thoughts and ambitions.
Since it is quite difficult for a person to discover his inner resources, you should make sure that you sit down and think hard about your desired career actually is, and then go ahead to plan the path of your career accordingly.

Learn about the initial path of this career

Most careers require formal education, some sort of professional training and initial experience.

  1. Education requirements? How many years and the level of degrees?
  2. Does career education include having intern jobs to get the initial expertise?
  3. Any continues education you need during your career?

 Gather information
Ask people from this field about various aspects of that career. For example, you’d probably want to know more about climbing up the career ladder?

Therefore, look for information about:

  1. Jobs and on-the-job trainings you need to have.
  2. Career Programs.
  3. Professional experience you need to get from each job.
  4. Responsibilities and day to day tasks of the jobs.
  5. Average daily hours and salary packages.
  6. Skills gained from each position.
  7. Is it a team-work type of job or an individual one?
  8. How many jobs people change in this career life span (on an average)?
  9. Travel requirements or relocation.

Once you identified the career of your dreams and gathered information about it, plan your way toward achieving your goals. Ask yourself:

Am I ready to take further steps? Find out more answers about what it takes to achieve your dreams and move forward.
