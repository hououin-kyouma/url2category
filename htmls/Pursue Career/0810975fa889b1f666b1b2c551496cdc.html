Tips to Narrow Your Career Path

Jennifer Parris FlexJobs Career Writer

When you were younger, what did you want to be? A police officer? A fireman? A nurse? An astronaut? Chances are, you’ve changed your mind about your career path a couple times (or a few hundred) since you were that little kid trying to figure out what you would do when you were bigger.

Now that you’re an adult, well, sometimes it can still be hard to figure out what you want to do.

If you’re struggling with narrowing your career path, these four tips might help make your decision easier:

1. Assess your current career.

What is your career field right now? Are you in marketing, accounting, the healthcare field? More important than what you’re doing is why you’re doing it. Are you in the kind of field that makes you eager to wake up every morning and look for a job? Or are you feeling a little, um, lost?

If you’re unsure of what you want to do, you’re not alone. A U.S. Department of Labor report found that people changed jobs 11 times between the ages of 18 and 44. It’s important to keep in mind that a career path is rarely in a straight line; there are always offshoots from the path that, if you dare to tread upon them, might lead you to the career you’re meant to have. So first things first: determine if you want to continue working in the same industry or dare to dream for something new. That said…

2. Don’t give yourself too many options.

You love pottery making, public relations, and you have strong interests in nonprofit work. It can be exciting to have many interests (and you should), but giving yourself too many career options can lead to chaos. And it can also paralyze your job search because you won’t be able to focus on what you really want to do. If you’re undecided about what you’d like to do, make a list of everything that you love—and then cut the list down. Then…

3. Distinguish between career and hobbies.

You might love writing, but the thought of having an editor rewrite your work might make you cringe. Or you may really enjoy helping your friend promote his business on social media, but you’re uncertain if you’d want to do that as a “real job.” It’s important to look at what you love and ascertain if it’s something you enjoy doing as a hobby or if you want to turn your hobby into your job.

It’s okay for some interests to remain passion projects, or to perhaps look to volunteering if you want to take it to the next level. That way, you can have a job you enjoy that allows you to earn an income, and still stay connected to the hobbies and interests that you truly enjoy.

4. Have short- and long-term goals.

When you close your eyes, you see yourself as a doctor. But right now, you might be a nursing assistant. A dream job doesn’t have to remain a dream, but can become a reality if you know how to make it happen. If your end goal seems so far away that it tempts you to ditch the idea altogether, start smaller by making short- and long-term goals.

Outline the necessary steps you’ll have to take and then break it down into smaller, bite-sized steps. Set up each stage with a smaller goal and a deadline. By having a deadline you’ll stay motivated, focused, and feel accomplished as you complete each phase on your journey to the career that you want. Or, you might find a new career along the way that you love even more.

Narrowing down your career path can be difficult if you’re unsure of what you want to do. So take the time to figure out what you love (and what you don’t), make a plan, and take it step-by-step to make it a reality!
