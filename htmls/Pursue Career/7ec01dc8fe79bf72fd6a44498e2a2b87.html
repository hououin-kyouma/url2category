Practical Tips to find a Teaching Job right after College

By Guest Coach in Contributor Content, Job Search Advice on October 27, 2016

0 Comments / Leave a Comment

An article by contributor Jessica Gust.

When you leave college, it’s often difficult to find a job right away. But with the right process and hard work, it is possible. The teaching industry is often looking for recent graduates and new teaching staff to join schools for the beginning of the school year, so you have a little time to find that opportunity. Here are some practical tips to help you land that teaching position right after you graduate.

Do your research

First of all, you need to get a good idea of which schools in your area might have positions available. There are several reasons why a job might be open. It could be that a teacher is retiring, or is moving on to a new position elsewhere. It could also be that a school is starting to offer lessons in a certain discipline for the first time. Keep in mind that you might not have any openings in your specialty in your immediate area, so you need to cast the net wide. Look out for announcements and job listings, and speak to people who might be in the know. Having a good idea of where you want to work is a really good start for getting that job.

Prepare ahead

If possible, you also need to make sure that you prepare ahead of time. Don’t wait until after your final day of college to start looking around for job opportunities. You can begin to build ties and network right from your first placement. Keep in touch with people who will be able to tell you about vacancies and put in a good word for you. Concentrate on getting good results and doing well on your placements so that your resume looks good from the start. This will all help you to be more likely to net a good job after you leave. Don’t forget that you can also apply to open positions before you finish college, so long as you would be starting work after you graduate. You can include predicted exams results as well as testimonials from placements.

Join specific networks

Normal job boards are all well and good, and lots of teaching opportunities may come up on them. But there are other networks where you can find a lot of job listings specific to the teaching industry. This means looking for sites which cater to teachers only, as well as looking on social media. Facebook groups can be an unusual but effective way to hear about positions through the grapevine! When you are chatting to fellow teachers constantly, you can often hear about things before the general public does, giving you more time to prepare.

Know your interview technique

Finally, there’s no better way to get a job than to ace the interview. If you are fully prepared with a strong interview technique, then your chances of getting the job will be so much higher. Make sure that you have a suit of the right quality to make a good impression, and that you can answer any question thrown at you with ease. The most important thing is to be as confident as possible. If you are a nervous wreck in your interview, you won’t make the impression of someone who will deal with students well! You know what you are doing and you deserve to get this job, so hold your head high with confidence.

By following these tips, you have a much higher chance of finding that dream teaching job as soon as you finish college. Even if you don’t find one immediately, keep trying and it will happen sooner than you think!

Jessica Gust is a Marketing Assistant at Localpeek.co.uk– a new postcode finder. She is passionate about new marketing strategies, she is always eager to share her ideas through blogging.
