Atul Wasan

Sports

Atul Wasan, a former Indian cricketer, runs five academies across Delhi, along with his team of coaches who provide training in cricket. Virat Kohli came to their academy as an 11-year-old and rest, as they say, is history. A consultant in many countries, Atul is actively involved in cricket commentary, among a host of activities. He holds sports camps across India and is a qualified sports coach after completing his training from England. Atul says, while sports in India is much revered now as compared to past, we have more of arm-chair sports enthusiasts who need to go out into the field. They need to play cricket or football rather than just watching the premier leagues. Team spirit, discipline, handling peer pressure and how to live with people are some of the takeaways of learning any kind of sport, he adds.

Ashwath Bhatt,

Educating through theatre

Laughter Lines It is appropriate too that Ashwath Bhatt is with us: For if indeed a man can be tasked to laugh and make others laugh on human failings, it is him. Bhatt is a pioneer of the Red Nose Clowning theatre in India - an art form that makes a serious business out of the act of clowning. When the clown takes to the stage, he comes dressed in the imperfections of the world. He falls to the ridiculous; wallows in silliness; misses his step, and hits his nose hard on the floor - just so as the audience could point a finger towards his rising red blob and laugh out loud. That is, however, a deeper act: Essentially the clown's way of reminding the audience that red noses are everywhere; and that the audience could well do with a nose job of its own.

D. Ranganathan,

A mentor and a thought leader

A life lesson well stated We introduce Ranganathan: a man you might want to talk any time about the journey of life, including careers. "Enjoy the journey!" he is most likely to say, considering the manner he has led his own reasonable life. For the backgrounder, he is a finance professional of some standing, a welcome figure in some of Delhi's liberated circles and someone who, even at 56 years, continues to pursue his passion and hobbies. All this and his otherwise calm persona, he says, is the by-product of an equanimous home under a single roof where people from 4 different generations live happily and interact on a daily basis - thus lending a soothing tranquility to the ambience within... despite the new-gen members pushing past setting clocks. Why him, you might ask! Well, for the reason that... he is a just another person across the street in an everyday setting but who continues a successful, happy and peaceful living for himself!

Prasun Mukherjee

Music

For singer-trainer Prasun Mukherjee, every student who comes to him to learn music is gifted. He believes in nurturing their talent as a friend and not as teacher. It extracts the best out of them, he says. With over 40 albums to his credit, Mukherjee, nephew of late Hemant Kumar, is a passionate mentor with students who never miss a chance to attend his class. A graduate in anthropology from Delhi university, the singer started learning Rabindra Sangeet early in life Though he wanted to be a lecturer, his calling in music got a direction when he was spotted by Gulshan Kumar from T series. In 1986 his first cassette came out and it was an instant hit. Since then, there has been no looking back for him. From expression to voice modulation, he trains his students to become professional singers and find their place in the music world.
