How to Figure Out Your Own Path to Career “Success”

September 15, 2016 by Siobhan Barnes Leave a Comment

Chances are, that growing up, you were taught to follow a traditional career path.

Maybe you preferred Art, Drama and Religious Studies, but were gently nudged towards Maths, Science or English so that you could pursue a “suitable” subject at university/college and “keep your options open”.

Growing up in Hong Kong, in an Asian culture, it was definitely encouraged to pursue a traditional path.

Lawyer. Doctor. Accountant. Banker. Dentist. Business person.

Anything that was definable and respectable was considered a good choice.

Plus it showed that you were smart enough to pursue such a challenging subject.

Can you relate?

I believe that’s why so many of us find ourselves so off-track in our 20’s, 30’s, 40’s and beyond.

At school, we decided to listen to our parents, teachers and Society at large to help us define what we should be doing with our careers.

Whilst they were well-meaning, sometimes the advice just wasn’t right for you. And that’s when you checked out and decided to ride the wave of your choices and keep climbing up the corporate ladder even though it’s really not you.

Now that you’re all grown up, you’ve probably never stopped to challenge the beliefs and rules around “success” that you learned as a kid.

Perhaps you feel you’ve invested too much in your career already…that you’ve got to be responsible…that it’s too late for you.

So you now find yourself…

A full-grown adult feeling trapped and unhappy in a job that you don’t love.

Well, my friend, today is the day that you get to define what a successful career looks like to you.

I’m not buying into those excuses that you’ve been telling yourself, and neither should you.

I know years of conditioning are hard to undo, but instead of thinking of this as a major decision, a do-or-die moment, let’s have fun with the process, shall we?

It’s time to reconnect to your purpose and let things be easy!
