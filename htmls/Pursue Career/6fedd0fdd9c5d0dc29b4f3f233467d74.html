Maria Holt graduated from the Crosby MBA program in December 2014. She will finish the degree as a full-time student after semesters balancing her professional responsibilities in St. Louis with her academic requirements in Columbia. Through it all, Holt has worked as a marketing coordinator for EPIC Systems, an engineering firm, where she markets its emerging projects.

Your personal success after graduating from an MBA program will be determined largely by the skills you take with you into the working world. These skills go beyond the materials mastered in the classroom, and can be as subtle as developing the right attitude for the right situation.

This leads to the question, “What skills should you focus on developing while earning your MBA?” Below is a list of the top ten skills MBA graduates must have:

Build a strong reputation

You will work with many people in your career, but only a select number will be reliable resources. Having a reputation for quality work will make you a valuable resource for others and create opportunities for you. Joan Gabel, Dean of the Trulaske College of Business, gives the following advice:

“Always give your best effort. It will distinguish you because you’ll find that others simply don’t.  I’ve found that an earned reputation for quality has led to my most interesting opportunities and I know you’ll find the same.”

Solve problems for others

“The ability to empathize with people, and find solutions to their problems from which mutual benefit can be gained, is the most important ability in my mind.  I don’t think this is a skill; it is more like a guiding principle,” says Gregg Martin, Associate Teaching Professor of Management.

Similar to having a reputation for quality work, solving problems for others will make you a valuable resource and a respected member of your organization. In return, others in your organization will be much more willing to help you with your problems when they arise. Approaching work as a collaborative effort will make you and the people around you more successful.

Collaborate successfully

Working with people from different backgrounds, with different motivations and in various job functions can be one of the biggest workplace challenges.

“Working with others requires not only knowledge of organizational dynamics, but also a certain level of emotional intelligence that allows one to be aware of such dynamics and the ability to decide what to do in so many different situations.

MBA programs typically provide numerous opportunities for group/team assignments in which students address various issues over the course of the program.  This interaction encourages both self- and environment- awareness, as well as an appreciation for diversity of styles and approaches,” said Joe Stephens, former Assistant Dean of MBA Programs.

Build your network

The previous three skills contribute to building your network. The more people who look to you as a resource, the more people you can build lasting relationships with. Your network is key for many reasons; problem solving, finding employees for your company, locating new resources, and learning of new career opportunities, to name a few.

Practice humility

As Brent Beshore, CEO of AdVentures, said in 9 Things Every Graduate Should Know, no one likes working with someone who constantly self-promotes. In fact, others enjoy people who give credit where credit is due and who recognizes others’ ability to contribute. Give tribute to others whenever possible and try to adopt a “we” instead of “I” mentality.

Communicate effectively

Learning how to effectively get your point across is not as easy as it sounds. Adjusting your message after understanding your audience’s expectations, assumptions and motivations is crucial to achieving your goals when communicating.

“Excellent oral and written communication skills are paramount for success.  If a person places too much emphasis on impersonal technical abilities they will encounter at least two disappointments:  First the skills will become outdated very quickly and second, using the skills will become more important than empathizing and communicating mutually beneficial solutions,” emphasizes Martin.

Address complex problems

In your career you will often be faced with complex problems and uncertain solutions. Being able to successfully navigate these problems is important to becoming a successful manager.

“Every manager/leader is faced with imperfect data, imperfect context, and varying angles of perspective when problems occur.  Gaining the ability to focus on the priority, cull things that don’t matter, and  make sense of those that do enables decision makers to exceed a 50% correct result, or flip of the coin.  Over time, those decisions add up to build a successful organization,” says Stephens.

Continue to learn

You will leave the MBA program with a set of skills acquired from your classes. However, just because you’ve earned your MBA doesn’t mean the learning is over.

“It is critically important, for the long-term, to prepare for lifelong learning. If the MBA program is approached as simply acquiring a credential, the return on investment will be minimal,” counsels Michael Christy, Adjunct Professor and former director of the Crosby MBA Program.

Understand the business environment, especially from different perspectives

Keeping up with what’s happening in the overall business environment is a part of life-long learning, and improves your ability to solve complex problems. Seeking different perspectives is paramount as well, enabling you to form your own opinion.

“Every student should avail himself of opportunities to be conversant with the dynamic business environment while they are students and consider how they may use their analytical skill sets to advance their careers.  This is accomplished by knowing more about potential scenarios than your competitors.  In short, read the newspapers and business analysis columns as often as possible,” Christy recommends.

Prioritize effectively

You will be responsible for more tasks than can be completed. Being able to effectively prioritize these tasks is a vital to your success. Which tasks are most important to the future of your company? Which tasks will help others move their projects forward? Which tasks have urgent timing? These are a few of the things that have to be balanced.

Additionally, the discipline to complete unpleasant tasks in a timely manner is equally important. Just because it’s difficult or tedious doesn’t mean it’s not important.
