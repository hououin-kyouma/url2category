Financial Leadership Program

The Financial Leadership Program (FLP) is a two-year leadership development program designed to prepare high-performing, high-potential talent for leadership roles within Fidelity Finance. The program combines four challenging, six-month assignments, workshops and training sessions to develop critical leadership, business and analytical skills.

Since 1998, the FLP has been developing and training candidates from diverse backgrounds into future leaders within Fidelitys Finance organization. The FLP offers exposure to a variety of business groups and core finance work to provide associates with a strong foundation in Fidelity Finance. FLP associates also have a large support system to ensure they receive the guidance and opportunities necessary for their continued growth and development.

Rotations: FLP associates work on strategic, high-priority projects addressing critical initiatives of the businesses. Through these projects and other assignments, associates work alongside senior finance leaders, providing powerful analysis to drive decision-making and recommendations to improve performance. Taking on a leadership role and building meaningful relationships with business partners are essential to drive projects to successful completion and to have a lasting impact on the organization.

Rotation Opportunities

Pricing +

Siobhan

Business Unit: Asset Management

I worked as part of the Analyst Team, a central team of finance analysts that support Line Finance for each of the advisors (e.g. Equity, Fixed Income, etc.). During my time with the team, I had two distinct priorities. First, I worked closely with Analyst Team Members, Line Finance, and our reporting team in Westlake, TX to develop an updated management fee revenue model to forecast future management fee revenue earned by our mutual funds. Leveraging the knowledge I had gained of how Asset Management generates revenue, I was also tasked with supporting Asset Managements Department of Labor (DOL) analysis as part of an enterprise wide finance team to develop analysis identifying the impact of the regulation on AMs future revenue.

Financial modeling was a key element of my rotation. From building a detailed revenue model with appropriate documentation for future users, to developing a model to support understanding the financial impact of the DOL ruling on AM, I was constantly fine-tuning my Excel skills and working with data from various sources across the firm. Working on both the revenue model and the DOL modeling provided me with high-level exposure  both in AM and also across Fidelity finance through my DOL work. This rotation  especially with the DOL element  was definitely a highlight of my time during the FLP. Not only was I better able to understand how truly interconnected each of the business units are, but also I gained a true appreciation for how important communication is among finance teams throughout the firm.

Forecasting+

Anna

Business Unit: Fidelity Institutional

My rotation required me to perform pricing analysis for the RIA and Bank Trust segments. Pricing is a critical function in FI and team served as an important partner to the business. During my time I had the opportunity to price numerous deals and work on important projects such as re-setting rack rate pricing for the Retirement segment.

My responsibilities varied on a daily basis and were based on the business goals at that time. The deals ranging in size from ~20M in assets to ~100B in assets and as the size of the client grew the complexity of the deal would as well. Often times the larger clients required me to work with multiple business partners to better understand the client, operational compatibility, long term strategic considerations and competitive pressures. This rotation made a lasting impression on me as it allowed me to build upon new skills such as negotiations and communicating with a broad audience.

Business Case Development+

Kevin

Business Unit: Fidelity Trading Ventures

I worked on a small team to prepare and launch an alternative trading system, or dark pool, designed to address the challenges Fidelity and other large firms had been facing to efficiently trade in large blocks. During the six-month rotation, I had the opportunity to see the project become a reality, culminating with the launch of trading for the newly created company.

My responsibilities included analyzing trade volume scenarios, creating financial projections, addressing questions from the board, supporting the companys senior team and assisting with the transition of financial operations and reporting duties. In this fast-paced environment, I had exposure to leaders of both organizations and was relied upon to deliver input and analysis that drove decision-making. It was a terrific opportunity to influence a high-priority project while learning about trading operations and what is required to launch a standalone business. This project will continue to be a highlight of my FLP experience and something I leverage as I move forward with my career at Fidelity.

Profitability Analysis+

Eric

Business Unit: Personal Investing

In my rotation in Personal Investing (PI), I was tasked with developing a better understanding of representative profitability. When a PI representative meets with a client at a branch, certain expenses are incurred related to that interaction and consequently future expenses and revenue are generated as that client begins to deposit assets into their Fidelity accounts. My project was to develop a new model to track the true profitability of these interactions and then identify certain representative and client profiles based on the outcome.

Through the modeling process I worked with business partners and the PI Finance organization to suggest new approaches to understanding profitability. Under this new approach my resulting model provided greater clarity into client profitability and flows while also proving that the rep interactions generated much higher client profitability than previously assumed. Following my analysis I had the opportunity to present my findings to the PI Senior Finance Leadership team and formalized the modeling into a repeatable process that will serve as a key input into some of the organizations current strategic directives.

Product Development+

Callum

Business Unit: Professional Services Group

PSG is a central group within Fidelity that provides Finance and management support for Funds Network, Benefits Consulting and, the group I was assigned to, Investment Product.

Investment Product is responsible for the creation and pricing of new mutual funds, CITs and passive products. I was responsible for the finance support of the business cases put forward by the Investment Product Managers. For example, one of the business cases involved the launch of Fidelitys first Smart Beta ETFs. In order to create the forecast model required to price the new products, I had to work with Fidelitys distribution businesses (PI, FI, WI) to forecast likely flows and understand P&L impacts to those businesses, Asset Management and Fidelity as a whole.

During this rotation I developed my network through the company, refined my modeling skills and learned a huge amount about how financial products operate, are created and what types of products work well for Fidelity Customers.

Capital Structure Evaluation+

Tyrone

Business Unit: Corporate Treasury

During my six-month rotation in Corporate Treasury, I worked closely with the Treasury Finance team to create a capital structure scorecard and analyze the firms exposure to changes in interest rates. The project concluded with an updateable scorecard that could be used as a guide to highlight the impact of interest rate changes.

My responsibilities included understanding the existing corporate capital structure, creating a catalog of applicable interest rates and forecasts, researching competitors views on and exposure to interest rates and creating financial projections. I was expected to contribute during meetings with senior leadership and explain my findings in a concise manner. This opportunity to work on a firm-wide analysis gave me an extensive view of the organization and played a key role in my understanding of the breadth of Fidelitys business units.

Financial Planning & Analysis+

Jeff

Business Unit: Workplace Investing

I was tasked with improving Workplace Investings (WI) annual forecasting process during my Financial, Planning, and Analysis rotation. WIs annual net flows represents both plan sponsor and participant activity across both Fidelity and non-Fidelity funds. Given the dynamics and materiality of these flows to stakeholders across Fidelity, I was given the opportunity to refresh and improve the WI flows forecasting process, models and infrastructure to provide better insight to the WI CFO and WI Senior leadership team, as well as improving alignment with various stakeholders across Fidelity.

The success of this project heavily relied upon quickly gaining subject matter knowledge in our WI recordkeeping business. I was able to develop strong relationships with members of the WI Finance, Sales, and Relationship Management teams in order to better understand the recordkeeping business and existing forecasting process. I was able to strengthen my analytic and modeling capabilities due to the complexity of client and participant behavior and market action which drives flows. Overall, I derived a significant amount of value from this rotation which increased my institutional knowledge, broadened my network within the firm, and enhanced my financial modeling skills.

Sponsor Relationship: Each associate is provided a sponsor who helps to ensure that the associate is successful in achieving their goals. Sponsors are senior Finance leaders who serve as the associates mentor and manager throughout the program. The sponsor guides the selection of assignments, provides business insight, feedback and coaching

Development & Training: Fidelity Finance hosts a series of day-long conferences, breakfast meetings and trainings, many of which are unique to the FLP. These events are designed to broaden each associates understanding of Fidelitys businesses and prepare them for a leadership role in Finance. FLP associates also have the opportunity to get involved in the on-going support of the program through taking a leadership role in the program's recruiting efforts and planning leadership series with senior business leaders from across Fidelity. These tasks provide FLP associates with the opportunity to help structure the program and learning opportunities throughout their tenure in the program.

Team Project: The team project provides an opportunity for 1st year FLPs to collaborate and gain additional exposure to a business unit beyond their four rotations. The project also allows a business unit to address an important challenge/opportunity and explore ideas outside of the normal workflow. While learning from one another and strengthening their finance skills, the team project drives decision-making and promotes innovative thinking within the business. The team project is led by a 2nd year FLP providing additional opportunities for associates to develop team and project management skills.

As the project lead, I was responsible for sourcing, planning and managing the team project. Over the course of six months, we worked closely with the product, pricing and technology groups to deliver a product level P&L and an updated pricing strategy based on our market research, competitive analysis, and the profitability goals.

This project allowed me to improve my planning and management skills in a controlled environment, while also contributing to an important initiative and gaining exposure to senior leaders. Ultimately, the leadership role and experience I gained were instrumental to my career development, as I leveraged the work to earn a full-time position within the Fidelity Clearing and Custody Solutions (FCCS) pricing team.

- Tyrone, Director, FCCS Finance

Internships +

Participants in Fidelitys 10-week FLP Summer Internship Program will have an opportunity to work with our Finance leaders, participate in development activities and learn about Fidelitys Finance organization. The program offers challenging and diverse assignments that allow participants to be an integral part of a team and contribute to one of Fidelitys businesses. Interns are evaluated upon completion of the Summer Internship Program and, based on that evaluation, may be offered a full-time position in the two-year Financial Leadership Program, upon completion of their MBA program. Intern assignments are primarily located at Fidelitys New England offices (Boston, MA and Smithfield, RI).

Qualifications/Characteristics:+

We are looking for highly motivated individuals with analytical skills, leadership potential, and an interest in pursuing a career within Corporate Finance. Qualifications include:

  • Four-to-six years of significant work experience with increasing responsibility
  • Strong financial, analytical, modeling and strategic thinking skills
  • Excellent project management, communication, collaboration and people management skills
  • Demonstrated leadership capabilities and ability to influence and manage change
  • Ability to work with minimum supervision, quickly assess situations, analyze a problem, complete a project or analysis and move on to a new project
  • Potential to become a senior Finance leader or CFO
  • MBA required
  • FLP will consider students who have an unrestricted right to work in the US on a permanent basis.

Application Process+

All full-time MBA candidates are eligible to apply. We actively recruit and hold events at a number of schools, however we encourage qualified candidates to learn about the program through our website and inquire about the application process with your school's career services center.
