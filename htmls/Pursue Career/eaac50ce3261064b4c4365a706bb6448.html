Building Skills

The skills that you develop as you study and explore career options will help ensure your success. You'll be building field-specific skills from your curriculum, as well as transferable skills from campus activities or a part-time job. To stand out in the applicant pool, however, you also need the professionalism that comes from hands-on experience. This means trying out what works for you, developing and improving skills, and confirming where your interests lie.

Goals:

  • Strengthen your communication skills
  • Develop global awareness
  • Refine your career goals
  • Begin developing your post-graduation plan
  • Try out career interests through experience
  • Build your professional network, including faculty and staff

You, in a nutshell: the elevator pitch. Learn how to make an impression in no time flat.

What Should I Do Next?

  • Schedule an appointment with a career counselor at the CCD to discuss internship/job search or graduate school application questions.
  • Develop your proactive and reactive internship or job search plans by attending an internship or job search workshop.
  • Use Handshake to apply for internship or job opportunities.
  • Complete the Summer Experience Survey (returning undergraduates).
  • Attend campus recruiting events, including employer information sessions, meet and greets, and career expos. Explore opportunities and build your contact list.
  • Practice interviewing in person with CCD staff.
  • Identify 3-6 faculty, supervisors, or advisors to serve as references. Ask if they are able to provide a strong recommendation for you.
  • Attend a resume workshop or sign up for our review hours to develop your resume and learn how to target it for specific positions, industries, and professions.
  • Develop and practice your elevator pitch to so you can market yourself in the most compelling way possible.
  • Identify contacts who might help you in your career, including friends, family members, and faculty.
  • Conduct informational interviews to get a day-in-the-life view of the profession(s) or position(s) in which you are interested and to determine which paths are best for you.
  • Get hands-on experience with an internship to learn whether a career field or job type is a good fit for you.
  • Pursue an officer position in a student organization or club sport to hone your leadership skills.
  • Practice writing a cover letter to learn how to tie your resume to a position description. Sign up for our review hours to get expert advice on your draft.
